# COMPREHENSIVE PLAN FOR THE DESIGN OF FRANKENGRAPHDB

*A blank-slate, memory-safe, ultra-high-performance graph database in Rust, built entirely on the Franken/asupersync ecosystem, designed to leapfrog every commercial and open-source graph system in existence.*

---

## 0. The Thesis: What "Leapfrog" Actually Means Here

Every graph database on the market today is a compromise fossilized around a decision made years ago:

- **Neo4j** fossilized around pointer-chasing "index-free adjacency" on the JVM: superb ergonomics, mediocre analytics, painful memory behavior, and a query runtime that took 15 years to get vectorized.
- **TigerGraph** fossilized around MPP analytics with a proprietary language and an operational footprint that makes it a platform decision, not a library.
- **Kùzu** got the query processing story *right* (columnar CSR + vectorization + factorization + worst-case-optimal joins) and then the company folded in 2025, leaving a scattering of community forks and proof that the ideas work but the execution surface was incomplete: single-writer, no replication story, no temporal story, no deterministic testing story.
- **Memgraph / FalkorDB** are fast in-memory engines with thin durability and no principled answer to larger-than-memory, history, or verification.
- **JanusGraph / NebulaGraph** fossilized around generic KV underlays (Cassandra/RocksDB) and pay a permanent impedance-mismatch tax on every traversal.
- The **academic frontier** (Sortledton, Teseo, LiveGraph, GTX, Spruce, RadixGraph; factorized WCOJ processing; DBSP; AeonG) has solved, *in isolation*, essentially every hard subproblem — transactional adjacency at near-CSR speed, unified join processing, automatic incrementalization, cheap temporal storage — and **no shipping system has ever composed them.**

FrankenGraphDB's leapfrog is not one trick. It is the *composition* of six bets, each individually at or beyond the current frontier, made feasible only because the foundation libraries already exist:

| # | Bet | One-line statement | Why nobody else can do it |
|---|-----|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **B1** | **One Version Universe** | MVCC publication, retained time-travel history, replication order, change subscriptions, and git-style database branches share one append-only, content-addressed, RaptorQ-protected commit mechanism, while their retention and proof products remain explicitly distinct. | Requires ECS-style durability substrate (frankensqlite) + fountain codes (asupersync) designed in from byte zero. |
| **B2** | **Graph-Structured LSM ("Strata")** | Adjacency lives in three temperature tiers—versioned delta blocks → sealed compressed CSR runs → archived checkpoints—so one store can target high transactional ingest and static-CSR-class scans; actual rates remain §17 benchmark gates. | Existing systems choose different write/scan/space points; Strata makes the representation choice per stable adjacency descriptor without changing semantics. |
| **B3** | **Unified Factorized/WCO Execution ("Loom")** | One Free-Join-style physical family covers binary and multiway join strategies plus factorized intermediates, using only the trie orders that Strata/Beacon actually expose and building spillable COLTs for missing orders. | Kùzu and Free Join validate important pieces; the integration over mutable, authorized, larger-than-memory graph storage remains the engineering bet. |
| **B4** | **Incremental Everything ("Ripple")** | One checked Z-set delta algebra powers the registered recursive-query subset, materialized views, standing queries/subscriptions, and eligible incremental analytics; final committed effects produce the canonical ordered delta batches. | Requires B1's final-effect stream and a typed operator inventory whose derivatives, deletion laws, recursion bounds, and fallbacks are explicit. |
| **B5** | **Determinism, Evidence, and Calibrated Adaptivity** | CGSE tie-break policies, complexity witnesses, and evidence-closed manifests make every eligible **STRICT** result reproducible and auditable while its full closure is retained. Every adaptive decision emits a replayable **decision card** under a versioned policy epoch. The **Sextant** calibration plane evaluates policies against pinned deterministic fallbacks, but statistical evidence is never mislabeled as a safety proof. The whole database runs under asupersync's lab runtime for bounded DPOR exploration, seed replay, and chaos injection, with scoped Lean/TLA+ anchors. | Requires franken_networkx's CGSE + asupersync's lab runtime + a codebase that threads `Cx` capability contexts through every effect from day one. |
| **B6** | **Agent-Native by Construction** | Branch-per-agent isolation with semantic intent-log merge, capability-scoped (macaroon) subgraph authorization, provenance as first-class edges, hybrid vector+text+graph retrieval in one planner, and retained-evidence replay of strict agent reads—purpose-built for GraphRAG and multi-agent memory. | Requires B1 (branches), B4 (fresh incremental views), B5 (scoped replay), and asupersync's macaroon security layer. |
The 2026 market context makes the timing exact: GraphRAG and agent memory have become the first mainstream non-fraud use case pulling enterprises into graph databases; practitioners have converged on hybrid vector+graph architectures; and reviewers explicitly flag governance/access-control and freshness-of-derived-state as the unsolved problems of current systems. Meanwhile the strongest technical design in the space (Kùzu) is orphaned. There is a Kùzu-shaped hole in the market, and the correct move is not to fill it — it is to build the system Kùzu would have become in 2030, plus the durability, temporal, verification, and multi-writer stories it never had.

**Anti-goals of this document:** There is no "MVP", no "v1 simplification", no "phase where we use RocksDB temporarily." Section 19 sequences *integration*, not ambition: every workstream below is specified at full strength. Horizontal sharding is explicitly post-1.0: the 1.0 formats reserve the identities and fencing fields it needs, but this plan does **not** claim that adding distributed atomic commit, distributed SSI, or a global-time service is a configuration-only activation. Those protocols have their own workstream and proof gates before sharding may ship.

## 1. Constraints and Non-Negotiables

1. **The dependency universe is closed.** Allowed: `core`/`alloc`/`std`, the Rust nightly toolchain (both asupersync and franken_networkx already pin nightly), and the three foundation projects — `asupersync` (with whatever it vendors internally; its choices are its own), the `fnx-*` crates of `franken_networkx`, and *design-level* reuse of `frankensqlite` (we fork/adapt specific modules into `fgdb-*` crates rather than linking `fsqlite-*` wholesale, because graph objects are not SQLite pages — see §2.3). Everything else — compression codecs, sketches, ANN indexes, inverted indexes, radix trees, wire protocols, columnar readers — is built in-house (§18 is the complete inventory). No serde, no tokio, no rocksdb, no arrow, no tantivy, no hnswlib. The Python package may consume `fnx-python` as an allowed `fnx-*` foundation crate and therefore its already-pinned transitive binding runtime, but `fgdb-*` crates may not add a direct PyO3 dependency; the database engine, server, CLI, and durable formats remain free of that packaging boundary.
2. **Memory safety is structural.** Every ordinary crate root and the workspace default use `unsafe_code = "forbid"`. Rust's `forbid` level cannot be lowered, so the few raw-pointer implementations (buffer arenas, SIMD kernels, VFS mappings) live in separately named `fgdb-unsafe-*` boundary crates whose roots use `deny(unsafe_code)` plus narrowly scoped, ledgered `allow` sites. Safe-facing crates never relax `forbid`. Every unsafe operation gets a ledger row: path, invariant, evidence, scalar or safe fallback, and no-claim boundary; CI rejects an unledgered site.
3. **`Cx` everywhere.** Every function that performs I/O, takes a lock, allocates from a shared arena, or can block accepts `&Cx` (asupersync's capability context). This is what makes B5 possible: swap the `Cx`, and the entire database runs under the lab runtime with virtual time, deterministic scheduling, and fault injection. It is also what makes cancellation-correct query timeouts, deadline propagation, and capability narrowing (read-only connections *cannot* express writes) structural rather than conventional.
4. **Deterministic by default, with an explicit domain.** The strict contract is: same evidence closure (root object and branch head, normalized query and parameters, semantic/capability/policy epochs, executable artifacts, index generations, numeric profile, and recorded external inputs) ⇒ byte-identical **canonical logical-result encoding**, including order. Transport chunking is not part of logical identity. Cross-machine strict mode uses the portable numeric profile in §8.6; relaxed floating execution is opt-in and certificate-declared.
5. **Single artifact, three postures.** One codebase produces: (a) an embedded library (`fgdb`) usable like Kùzu/DuckDB from Rust and Python; (b) a server binary (`fgdbd`) speaking the native protocol, HTTP/2, gRPC, WebSocket, and a Bolt-compat subset; (c) a CLI (`fgdb-cli`). Larger-than-memory operation is first-class in all three.
6. **The published recovery root is the source of truth.** Before history truncation, logical state is reconstructed from an authoritative checkpoint plus the globally ordered committed-effect stream. After the truncation horizon advances, the newly published checkpoint and retained suffix become the authoritative recovery basis; reclaimed capsules are no longer claimed to be available truth. The only **published** bytes updated in place are the two fixed root-pointer slots in §5.1. Newly created staging files may be written until sealed, but are unreachable and disposable by proved recovery rules; every reachable root object, checkpoint, marker batch, capsule, and derived object is immutable and content addressed. Derived indexes and views never become authoritative for base semantics or constraints.
7. **No JVM, no GC, no mmap-as-durability, no generic KV underlay, no eventual-consistency defaults.** These are the fossils we refuse to inherit.
8. **Narrowed capability contexts.** Subsystems receive purpose-typed wrappers over `Cx` (`QueryCx`, `TxnCx`, `CommitCx`, `MaintCx`, `ReplCx`) exposing only their legal effects as obligations (`pin_snapshot`, `reserve_wal`, `publish_segment`, ...). The sharpest instance: the merge ladder's intent-replay evaluator receives a `Cx` with **no clock, entropy, network, or filesystem capability** — deterministic rebase is guaranteed *by construction*, not by review.
9. **Prohibited shortcuts (constitutional).** No global-lock "interim" transaction model; no `HashMap<VId, Vec<EId>>` presented as storage; no snapshot isolation quietly labeled "ACID"; no parser-interprets-AST engine; no non-durable benchmark mode reported as a result; no serde-derived enum as a durable format; no detached background thread. Early code may implement a *subset* of each final abstraction — never a substitute for it.
10. **Adaptivity is classified, or it does not ship.** Every adaptive decision is exactly one of: *answer-preserving physical* (cache eviction, prefetch, equivalent kernel selection, local pacing); *answer-affecting execution* (approximate generation/candidate/fusion/stopping choices represented by the execution's `AnswerRequirement`/realized `AnswerContract`); or *canonical-state-affecting* (view/index publication, watermark advancement, security/retention/configuration changes). Answer-affecting choices are legal only inside the requested requirement, are fully pinned in the bound plan/certificate/replay closure, and emit decision cards; a policy-table change that can alter them is stream-sequenced. Canonical-state-affecting choices change only through logical commands under stream-sequenced `PolicyEpoch` or their own typed epoch. Physical-only choices may adapt locally but still log cards and must pass answer-equivalence tests. Logged propensity supports off-policy evaluation only for candidate actions covered by the logging policy. Statistical promotion evidence is `FG-EVID-*`, never an `FG-INV-*` safety proof, and the deterministic fallback remains valid when assumptions fail.
11. **Claims have types.** Every load-bearing statement is registered as exactly one of: `invariant` (must hold on every execution), `proof` (theorem within a named formal model), `bounded_model` (checked only within declared bounds), `statistical` (confidence statement under named assumptions), `slo` (empirical operational target), or `benchmark` (measured on a pinned fixture). A weaker claim class may inform policy but may not enforce or justify a stronger one. `invariants.toml` contains only exact safety/liveness invariants; `evidence.toml` and `slo.toml` carry statistical and empirical claims.

**The deep synergy:** asupersync is not "an async runtime we happen to use." Its obligations model maps exactly onto database invariants (a `CommitCapsule` publication is a two-phase obligation; a checkpoint lease is a lease obligation; an un-acked subscription delivery is a send permit). Its lab runtime turns MVCC interleaving bugs into seed-replayable test failures. Its region heaps make query memory accounting and cancellation-time reclamation structural. FrankenGraphDB is, in a precise sense, *a database written in the asupersync programming model*, the way FoundationDB is a database written in Flow.

### 2.3 frankensqlite (~1.28 MLOC): the architectural donor

frankensqlite is inspiration-by-dissection: we adopt its *designs*, re-instantiate them for graph objects, and where a module is genuinely object-agnostic we fork it into an `fgdb-*` crate (attribution preserved) rather than depending on `fsqlite-*` and dragging SQLite's page/B-tree world along.

| frankensqlite design | Verbatim lesson | FrankenGraphDB instantiation |
|---|---|---|
| **Page-granularity MVCC**: version chains in a bump arena, `Snapshot { high: CommitSeq }`, O(1) current-line visibility fast path, EBR reclamation, and local chain/lock invariants | Choose the versioning granule to match the storage granule, while treating branch ancestry as semantic visibility rather than forcing a donor's integer-only rule | **Block-granularity MVCC** on Strata adjacency blocks and property chunks (§7.1); applicable chain/lock rules become FG-INV-02/03, while durable transaction idempotency replaces a non-load-bearing monotone TxnId rule |
| **SSI (page-Cahill/Fekete) + first-committer-wins + eager locking** | Conservative dangerous-structure detection at storage granularity is a useful donor pattern; deadlock freedom and SSI soundness do not transfer by analogy | Graph-SSI plus logical predicate witnesses (§7.3); final physical/constraint locks use the independently modeled canonical/no-wait protocol in §7.1 |
| **Intent logs + deterministic rebase merge ladder** (semantic replay → structured patch → abort) | Same-granule conflicts can sometimes be semantically reconcilable; eligibility depends on reads, constraints, authority, and the operation algebra | Context-derived graph rebase and explicit-base branch merge (§7.4, §5.6), with hit rate measured rather than assumed |
| **Native mode / ECS**: content-addressed objects, `SymbolRecord` envelopes, root publication, capsules/markers, and mark-and-compact with leases | The architectural starting point, not a verbatim format contract | Chronicle (§5) re-specifies the root protocol, full-digest identity, encoding variants, final-capsule ordering, branch/global ancestry, and replicated payload availability; graph durability does not inherit correctness merely by analogy |
| **WriteCoordinator**: centralized final validation/order with group durability | A useful ordering shape, not a promise that final-effect regeneration, authorization, payload availability, or consensus is off the critical path | §5.2/§7.6; the complete D1/D2/Raft path is benchmarked and failure-tested |
| **ARC buffer pool, MVCC-aware; cache-line-padded sharded lock/siread tables; aligned direct-I/O buffers; prefetch on descent** | Mechanical sympathy checklist | Buffer manager (§6.7) |
| **Time travel** (`FOR SYSTEM_TIME AS OF`, snapshot ring today, VersionStore design) + **checkpoint+delta** thinking | Temporal support should ride the MVCC machinery, not duplicate it | Chronicle retention tiers (§5.4); history is queryable for the configured retention contract, never described as unbounded when policy permits reclamation |
| **Encryption**: Argon2id KEK hierarchy, per-object protection, encrypt-then-code | A design starting point whose nonce, AAD, per-encoding DEK, symbol-MAC, rewrap, and KMS/HSM details must be respecified | The exact §5.1/§12.5/App. A composition; no correctness is inherited merely from the donor |
| **Verification posture**: conformance corpora, differential harnesses vs. reference engines, feature-universe ledgers, exit-criteria contracts, metamorphic tests | The QA house style | §15 throughout |

### 2.4 What the foundations do *not* provide (and we therefore build)

Columnar graph storage, adjacency compression codecs, a query language front end, a cost-based optimizer, a vectorized/factorized executor, WCOJ machinery, a Z-set incremental engine, secondary/FTS/vector indexes, graph statistics sketches, the wire protocol's graph payloads, the calibration plane's graph-specific scores and ledgers (`fgdb-calibrate` wraps the runtime's e-process/conformal machinery rather than reimplementing it), the verifiability accumulator (Merkle Mountain Range + transparency checkpoints), and the replication state machine (Raft sequencing itself — though membership, anti-entropy, coded fan-out, and convergent metadata are *compositions* of existing protocol machines per §2.1, not new construction). That is the actual FrankenGraphDB codebase, estimated 275–440 KLOC of new Rust — large, but *smaller than frankensqlite*, on a foundation that already absorbed the hardest systems problems (scheduling, cancellation, networking, fountain coding, deterministic testing, sequential statistics). The complete build-it-ourselves inventory is §18.

## 3. SOTA Distillation: The Field, and Where It Falls Short

A condensed map of the review (systems + literature), organized as *adopt / adapt / reject* decisions. Full bibliography in Appendix E.

### 3.1 Dynamic graph storage (the write/scan/space trilemma)

| Source | Core idea | Verdict for FrankenGraphDB |
|---|---|---|
| **CSR** (static baseline) | Contiguous offset+neighbor arrays; unbeatable scans; zero update capability | **Adopt** as sealed per-`(stable partition, edge type, direction)` runs with explicit ordinal maps and per-list codecs; mutable labels remain membership/derived projections, never primary placement (§6.3) |
| **Sortledton** (VLDB '22) | Sorted neighborhoods in cache-friendly blocks; unrolled skip lists for hub vertices; transactional; ~1.22× CSR analytics at ~2.1× CSR memory | **Adapt** as the *delta tier* block layout (§6.2): sorted 256-edge blocks, per-block version pointers; hub vertices escalate to skip-list-of-blocks |
| **Teseo** (VLDB '21) | PMA + ART fat tree; great point latency, rebalance latency spikes, high memory on skewed graphs | **Reject** PMA as primary (rebalance storms conflict with p99 goals); **adopt** its lesson that the vertex table must not be a straitjacket — our vertex directory is an index, not the store |
| **LiveGraph** (VLDB '20) | Purely sequential per-vertex logs; fast ingest; scans degrade, deletes awkward | **Adapt**: the *hot-vertex overflow log* (§6.2.4) borrows the append-only trick for write bursts, but logs are bounded and compacted into sorted blocks |
| **Aspen** (PLDI '19) | Purely functional compressed trees; O(1) snapshots; single writer; 3–10× CSR memory | **Adopt the spirit** (immutable structural sharing ⇒ free snapshots) at *run granularity* instead of tree-node granularity — same benefit, a fraction of the pointer overhead |
| **GTX / Spruce / RapidStore / RadixGraph** ('24–'26) | Per-vertex delta chains for concurrency; ART-indexed buffers; decoupled read/write paths; latch-free log append; space discipline | **Adapt**: delta-chain-per-block concurrency (GTX lesson) + latch-free MPSC block append (RadixGraph lesson); the 2025 "Revisiting DGS" study's headline — 4–9× CSR space overhead across the field — is precisely the disease Strata's tiering cures (hot minority pays delta overhead; cold majority sits *below* CSR via compression) |
| **AeonG** (VLDB '24) | Current/historical hybrid stores; anchor+delta history; anchor-based version retrieval; ≈5.7× storage reduction, ≈2.6× temporal latency reduction, <10% OLTP overhead | **Adopt and unify**: our MVCC retirement path *is* the historical store; anchors = periodic sealed snapshots in ECS; deltas = the commit stream we already durably have (§5.4). AeonG bolts temporal onto Memgraph; we make it a corollary of durability |

### 3.2 Query processing

| Source | Core idea | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Kùzu** (CIDR '23) + Graphflow lineage | Vectorized batches, factorized intermediates, WCOJ plans, and dense projection indexes | **Adopt as executor baseline** while keeping stable VId independent of labels; dense IDs are snapshot/index-local `OrdinalMapId` projections (§4.5) |
| **WCOJ theory** + EmptyHeaded/ADOPT/HoneyComb | Cyclic patterns need multiway intersection; attribute order and parallelization matter | **Adopt** through declared trie cursor orders. Elias–Fano neighbors support direct rank/select; other codecs use skip/decode paths (§6.3, §8.5) |
| **Free Join** | Continuum between binary joins and Generic Join; lazy column-oriented lazy-trie (COLT) access paths | **Adopt** for conjunctive inner joins. Strata supplies only the endpoint orders it physically has; property/key/alternate orders require COLT or Beacon, and overlays/preprocessing are costed (§8.5) |
| **Factorized databases**, FAQ | Many operations admit factorized evaluation under specific algebraic laws | **Adopt** a recursive factorization type plus an operator preservation matrix; flatten when no registered law applies (§8.4, Appendix C) |
| **Morsel-driven parallelism** (HyPer), push-based vectorized engines, **Umbra** (variable-size pages, pointer swizzling), **LeanStore** (optimistic lock coupling) | The modern engine checklist | **Adopt**: morsels = asupersync tasks in a query region; buffer manager per §6.7 |
| **GQL ISO/IEC 39075:2024**, **SQL/PGQ 9075-16:2023**, openCypher | The standard exists; quantified path patterns and the full minimum-conformance closure are hard; current products expose GQL-compatible surfaces and publish feature matrices but must not be called conforming beyond their claimed/passed class | **Adopt** GQL with a content-addressed language contract and openCypher compatibility (§8.1); publish exact ISO feature IDs, prerequisites, and gaps from the start |
| **Converged relational-graph optimization** (SPJM work) | Pattern matching and relational ops must share one optimizer | **Adopt**: one algebra, one cost model, no "graph engine bolted to SQL engine" seam (§8.3) |

### 3.3 Incremental computation, temporal, vector

| Source | Core idea | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **DBSP** / Differential Dataflow / streaming-graph work | Integer-weighted Z-set deltas and registered derivatives support rich incremental computation | **Adopt DBSP's linear-time core** over canonical post-commit deltas. Only the validated operator/recursion subset is incremental; deletion may require retained support state and is not promised equal physical cost (§9) |

### 3.4 What we deliberately reject

Generic KV underlays (JanusGraph/Nebula tax); Gremlin as the primary language (imperative, optimizer-hostile — provided only as a later compat shim if ever); RDF-first modeling (property graph first; RDF import/view later); JVM anything; mmap-as-the-durability-story; PMA global rebalancing; eventual consistency as a default; external crates (constraint #1).

Four seductive alternatives were evaluated in depth and rejected with recorded reasons: (a) native hyperedges; (b) an unbounded per-fragment representation zoo; (c) a separate full GraphBLAS executor; and (d) plan racing as the default. Racing survives only behind deterministic-output buffering and registered safe cutovers (§8.5). Weaver-style refinable timestamps were set aside for 1.0 because one global scalar commit order simplifies Chronicle, branches, Ripple, and replay; post-1.0 sharding workstream W12 must preserve that contract through a meta-Raft global transaction record or explicitly reopen the decision.

Further rejections: (e) a second Calvin-style transaction lane unless measured aborts justify reopening it; (f) WASM as a foreign UDF runtime; (g) external-codegen JIT; (h) TrueTime-class infrastructure for 1.0—HLC plus consensus-fenced read contracts supplies the semantics actually specified, not arbitrary follower linearizability; (i) FHE/MPC/ORAM in the initial threat model; and (j) learned indexes as authoritative access paths. DP and leakage measurements remain scoped mechanisms, not substitutes for noninterference.

## 4. Data Model

### 4.1 The FrankenGraph model

A **bitemporal, schema-gradient, multi-label property multigraph**:

- **Vertices** carry a *label set* (not a single label), properties, and identity.
- **Edges** are typed (exactly one edge type), directed (undirected exposed as a view/semantics flag per type), may be parallel, and carry properties. `EId` is the unconditional parallel-edge discriminator; an optional user edge key participates only in a declared key/uniqueness constraint.
- **Stored property values** use `CanonicalPropertyValue`: `Null, Bool, Int64, UInt64, Float64, Decimal128, String, Bytes, Date, LocalTime, ZonedTime, LocalDateTime, ZonedDateTime, Duration, Point(2D/3D geo), Uuid, List<T>, Map<String,T>, Struct, Embedding{dim, dtype: f32|f16|i8}` under the pinned scalar profile. The **bound-query type lattice is separate**: it adds graph element/reference, path, binding/table, absence/`NOTHING`, and every required or optional GQL constructor/coercion named by the exact `LanguageContract`. Property storage therefore does not pretend to be a “strict superset” of query-only GQL types. The fnx bridge is lossless only for the registered intersection with `CgseValue`; unsupported values require an explicit projection/coercion law.
- **System time (bitemporal axis 1)** is universal and automatic, but branch-aware. Every vertex/edge/property version carries `created_by: MarkerRef` plus zero or more retirement/supersession `MarkerRef`s. At a selected branch head, the effective interval is the creation followed by the earliest retirement **visible in that ancestry**; a sibling-branch deletion cannot retire the version elsewhere. Linear trunk generations may cache `[created_seq, effective_retired_seq)` only as a projection whose lineage/head is explicit (§5.3–5.4).
- **Valid time (bitemporal axis 2)** is opt-in per element type: schema can declare `VALID_TIME` columns and the planner gets temporal-join operators, declared interval non-overlap constraints, and temporal path semantics (`DURING`-constrained patterns; a time-monotone path mode where each hop's validity must overlap-and-advance) over them.
- **Provenance** is first-class: any element can carry a `provenance` system property (source id, extraction confidence, agent/session id), and the `DERIVED_FROM` system edge type is reserved — the GraphRAG lineage story is modeled, not improvised.

### 4.2 Schema as a gradient, not a gate

Three enforcement postures, per label/edge type, changeable online:

1. **Open** (NetworkX/Neo4j-style): any property, any value; types inferred and recorded as statistics.
2. **Shaped**: declared properties are typed and validated; extra properties allowed into an overflow map column.
3. **Strict**: declared properties only; constraints active (existence, uniqueness/key, range/regex/check, edge endpoint label constraints, cardinality constraints like `AT MOST 1 :MOTHER edge out`).

Schema objects (labels, edge types, property defs, constraints, indexes) are versioned in the commit stream like data (a schema root/epoch for every bound coordinate, as frankensqlite's design does for one database), so **schema changes are transactional, time-travelable, and branchable**. Migration between postures — and any typed-column promotion of a hot overflow property — is an online shadow-build job reusing the seal/publish protocol (§6.3): build against a pinned snapshot, catch up deltas, validate, atomically publish the new schema epoch; old snapshots keep their epoch's interpretation. The **per-coordinate schema binding is part of snapshot identity** (§5.3) and of every plan-cache key, so prepared plans invalidate precisely rather than consulting one ambient epoch.

### 4.3 Graphs, branches, and the catalog

A **database** has a stable `DatabaseId` and contains named graphs with stable `GraphId`s. Each graph has a trunk branch plus arbitrary user branches (§5.6). A branch coordinate is `(GraphId, BranchId, head: MarkerRef)`; names are versioned catalog aliases, not identity. Queries address `graph@branch` or `graph@branch AS OF <time|seq>`; the default is `trunk@now`.

A single-node transaction may touch several graph/branch coordinates and commits them atomically through one global marker carrying `head_updates[]`. This atomicity is a property of Chronicle's global commit protocol, not something inferred merely from sharing a sequence counter. The post-1.0 sharded posture must supply the distributed atomic-commit protocol in §14.6 before cross-shard atomicity is claimed.

The role-free state coordinate is `CoordinateSchemaBinding { graph, branch, selected_head: MarkerRef, schema_root_oid, schema_epoch }`; there is no transaction-wide “current schema epoch.” Query binding wraps it as `GraphReferenceBinding { binding_role_id, coordinate: CoordinateSchemaBinding }`, where the binder deterministically derives `BindingRoleId` from the canonical graph-reference occurrence. Bindings are unique by role, so one statement may compare the same branch at two `AS OF` heads without conflating visibility, schema, retention leases, authorization, or witnesses. A schema mutation instead records `CoordinateSchemaTransition { graph, branch, apply_basis: MarkerRef, before_root_oid, before_epoch, after_root_oid, after_epoch }`. The newly created marker becomes the `selected_head` of the resulting coordinate binding, avoiding a capsule that recursively names its own future marker. Catalog, binder, cache, permit, certificate, and replay contracts use role-bearing bindings for query inputs and role-free bindings for state ownership; only genuinely database-global topology and policy epochs remain scalar.

### 4.4 Reserved system surface

`_id`, `_labels`, `_type`, `_key`, `_created_seq`, `_retired_seq`, `_commit_time`, `_valid_from`, `_valid_to`, `_provenance`, `_embedding` (conventional default vector slot), `_score` (query-time). `_retired_seq` is the effective retirement in the selected snapshot ancestry, not a branch-global stored scalar. System edge types: `DERIVED_FROM`, `SAME_AS` (entity-resolution assertions with confidence—the agent-memory dedup primitive that current merge/upsert APIs only approximate). These fields are ordinary protected data from Warden's perspective: a capability may mask provenance, embeddings, commit metadata, or selected properties even when it can see the element (§12).

### 4.5 Stable identity, dense projections, and mutable labels

- **VId is label-independent and never recycled.** Within a graph, `VId` is a 128-bit `{ allocation_epoch: u64, local_id: { partition_id: 20 bits, monotone_slot: 44 bits } }`. `AllocationEpoch` is allocated by the externally anti-rollback `IdentityContinuityRecord` described below before first use; deleted or abandoned leased slots remain spent forever. External identity is `(DatabaseSecurityNamespaceId, GraphId, VId)`, so history, backups, branches, clones, and client references remain unambiguous. Local exhaustion advances to a newly reserved epoch or returns a structured limit; it never wraps, reuses, or silently aliases a GraphId.
- **Dense ordinals are snapshot-local projections, not identity.** Each sealed generation may map stable VIds to compact `u32` or `u64` ordinals for CSR, bitmaps, and SIMD. A projection is named by `OrdinalMapId` and cannot escape its segment/index generation. Compaction may renumber projections without changing VId.
- **EId and creation order are stable.** Every edge receives the same 128-bit epoch+partition+slot identity shape in an edge-kind domain; it is never a run position or reused. A sequence-neutral first-creation effect stores `BirthOrdinal { intent_ordinal, merge_ordinal, element_id }`; its creating `MarkerRef` supplies `commit_seq`, yielding durable `OriginBirthOrder { commit_seq, intent_ordinal, merge_ordinal, element_id }` in applied versions/checkpoints/runs without rebuilding the pre-order capsule. Importing the already-existing identity into another branch preserves that origin and separately records branch admission (§5.6). `(src, edge_type, dst, user_key?)` is an indexed logical tuple, while run positions/property locators are replaceable physical addresses. Parallel edges remain distinct across compaction, merge, and time travel, and `InsertionOrder` never depends on layout.
- **Allocation is canonical state plus an external anti-rollback fence, not a process counter.** `IdAllocatorState` in `LogicalStatePayload` stores the current `AllocationEpoch`, per-GraphId/kind/partition high-water marks, spent-range commitments, topology epoch, and exhaustion state. Before an epoch may issue an ID, a threshold-signed/witnessed `IdentityContinuityRecord { database_security_namespace_id, allocation_epoch, predecessor_digest, status }` is durably CAS-published outside the rollbackable database closure (operator HSM/KMS registry or configured witnesses) and copied into Chronicle. Proposals/follower preparation use temporary IDs. Finalization either assigns permanent IDs in commit order or consumes a previously stream-sequenced `IdRangeLease`; lease ranges are disjoint, authority-scoped, crash-recoverable, and never recycled even when partly unused. Lease expiry ends issuance authority but never removes the range from the permanent spent set. Branches share the graph allocator rather than forking its namespace. Merge/import verifies source identity namespace and remaps only through an explicit recorded graph-clone transform. W12 range grants are meta-Raft fenced before shard use. A same-identity restore must acquire an epoch strictly above the external continuity floor before writes; if that authority/floor cannot be proved, the database opens read-only or requires `CloneNewIdentity`.
- **Labels are versioned membership, never placement identity.** A vertex stores one versioned label-set reference. Label membership indexes map `(label, partition) -> VId/ordinal bitmap` for a specific snapshot generation. An edge is stored exactly once in the canonical forward source-partition family. A reverse family is a declared synchronously maintained materialization when enabled for that edge type; when disabled, reverse expansion uses a registered spillable fallback and cannot claim O(1) reverse adjacency. `(src_label, type, dst_label)` access paths are derived indexes that may duplicate locators but never own the edge. `AddLabel` and `RemoveLabel` update membership and affected derived projections without changing VId or rewriting incidence.
- **External keys** belong to a declared constraint domain, not necessarily one label. A persistent canonical `ConstraintStateRoot` is scoped by `(GraphId, BranchId/head, schema_epoch)` and resolves `(constraint_id, canonical_key) -> VId/EId` with structural sharing across forks. A sibling branch may resolve the same key differently without leaking or conflicting until merge; merge rebuilds/revalidates affected entries into the target root. Bulk import publishes the same state through §7.5's fenced protocol.
- **Distribution reservation, not a promise of free activation.** Partition IDs, `topology_epoch`, and allocator epochs are durable from format v1 so identities need not change when ownership moves. They do not solve distributed atomic commit, SSI, or time; §14.6 supplies those protocols before sharding ships.

## 5. Chronicle: The Commit Stream, Durability, Time, and Branches (Bet B1)

Chronicle is the ECS-native durability substrate — frankensqlite's Native mode, born as the *only* mode, for graph objects.

### 5.1 Objects

Every durable logical object has a full 256-bit `ObjectId = BLAKE3_keyed(K_oid, "fgdb:logical:v1" ‖ DatabaseSecurityNamespaceId ‖ canonical_plaintext_header ‖ canonical_plaintext_payload)`. `DatabaseSecurityNamespaceId` is an immutable random 256-bit database-creation identity, distinct from the human/catalog `DatabaseId`; `K_oid` is an immutable random database identity key shared only with authorized database replicas/restore principals and wrapped through the root/bootstrap/key-escrow chain. Both are available before any ObjectId is verified. Honest-but-curious storage still observes equality and size/access patterns among opaque ObjectIds inside one namespace, but cannot perform an offline plaintext dictionary from the public slot alone; profiles that expose `K_oid` explicitly accept that leakage. Same-identity restore preserves namespace and key, while clone restore creates both anew and must re-address and re-encrypt the complete logical closure. A 128-bit prefix is only a lookup accelerator; collision buckets always verify the full digest, object kind, length, and canonical plaintext before deduplication or substitution. Logical identity therefore survives recompression, re-encryption, and RaptorQ re-encoding within one keyed security namespace. Deduplication does not cross security namespaces unless an explicit deployment policy accepts equality leakage and uses a separately specified shared namespace/key domain.

Physical protection follows one noncircular pipeline. Canonical plaintext is deterministically compressed under `CodecProfile`; a random per-ciphertext DEK performs **one object-level AEAD** operation with AAD `("fgdb:object-aead:v1", ObjectId, CipherDescriptorWithoutDigest)`. The cipher descriptor excludes FEC and placement. `CiphertextId` hashes that descriptor, ciphertext, and profile-sized object tag. A canonical `EncodingDescriptorWithoutId { ciphertext_id, fec_profile, transfer_length, oti_common, oti_scheme, symbol_size, source_block_count, symbol_auth_profile }` contains every parameter that changes RaptorQ decoding or symbol authentication, and `EncodingId = BLAKE3("fgdb:encoding:v1" ‖ canonical(EncodingDescriptorWithoutId))`. The same authenticated ciphertext may be recoded under another complete encoding descriptor without re-encryption. Physical locations and symbol inventories use `PlacementDescriptorWithoutId { encoding_id, placement_epoch, failure_domain_policy, location_form }`, where `location_form` is either one fully described contiguous span with an authenticated symbol-inventory digest or an explicit canonical symbol/locator inventory. `PlacementId = BLAKE3("fgdb:placement:v1" ‖ canonical(PlacementDescriptorWithoutId))`; neither the ID nor a digest of the record is an input to itself. Adding or moving symbols creates a new descriptor/record, never a new encoding identity. RaptorQ encodes the complete ciphertext-plus-object-tag. A domain-separated `K_symbol = KDF(DEK, "fgdb:symbol-auth:v1" ‖ EncodingId)` authenticates the exact canonical `SymbolRecord` transcript: domain/version plus **every serialized header field except the MAC bytes themselves**, followed by the payload. This includes framing lengths, identities, object kind, source block, ESI, transfer length, OTI, flags, auth profile, and payload length; there is no shorter competing transcript. Symbols do not reuse an object AEAD nonce as per-symbol nonces, and symbols from different `EncodingId`s are never mixed. DEK/ciphertext identity is separate from `wrap_key_epoch`, so KEK/KMS recipient rewrap changes only immutable `KeyWrap` records, not `CiphertextId` or `EncodingId`. Appendix A fixes algorithms/profile IDs, tag lengths, AAD/MAC transcripts, bounds, and partial-tail rules. RaptorQ protects erasures only within the declared source-block and placement model; replication/backup protects failure-domain loss.

Object kinds include `CommittedEffectCapsule`, `CommitCommand`, typed `ControlCommand` and their sequence-neutral spec objects, canonical `LogicalCommandRecord`, `LogicalStatePayload`, `LogicalStateRoot`, replica-local `PreparedCommitRecord`, `LogicalDeltaTemplate`, materialized `LogicalDeltaBatch`, `CoalescedDeltaPayload`, `DeltaDeliveryEnvelope`, `DeliveryLeaseRecord`, `DeliveryLeaseRegistryRoot`, logical `CommitMarker`, physical `MarkerBatch`, `AdjRunSegment`, `PropChunkSegment`, `VertexDirectorySegment`, `RecoveryCheckpoint`, `CheckpointStateVector`, `IndexSegment`, `ConstraintState`, `ResourceLedgerState`, `StatsSegment`, `SchemaSnapshot`, `BranchManifest`, `BranchMergeLedgerState`, `PrivacyContinuityRecord`, `ReplayManifest`, `ExecutableArtifactSet`, `ReplayArchiveRecord`, `ReplayRegistry`, `TransparencyCheckpoint`, `PolicyEpoch`, `AuditTicket`, `AuditContinuityRecord`, `AuditCloneBoundary`, replica-local `AuditVisibilityFence`, `AuditSegment`, `KeyDestroyRecord`, and replica-local `RootManifest`. The canonical content graph is a DAG: every strong, conditional, and weak-digest value in newly encoded bytes must already be known. A future applied result may be described only by a sequence-neutral spec or assigned scalar sequence, never by its not-yet-computable ObjectId/digest. Self IDs, mutually strong cycles, and an input command that names its own applied result are invalid. G0 generates a reference-graph cycle checker from the durable schemas.

To avoid the state-root/command-head cycle, canonical state is layered. `LogicalStatePayload` contains the complete semantic state at `{ applied_logical_command_seq, applied_commit_seq }` but no logical-command-head wrapper. After deterministic apply has constructed every result object and the payload, it creates `LogicalCommandRecord { logical_command_seq, predecessor: ConditionalCommandRef, command: StrongRef<CommitCommand|ControlCommand>, transaction_marker: StrongMarkerRef?, logical_command_hlc, resulting_state_payload_digest: WeakDigest }`. It then creates `LogicalStateRoot { payload_ref: StrongRef<LogicalStatePayload>, logical_command_head: StrongCommandRef? }`; only genesis has no head. The payload digest cross-checks the sibling object; it is not a reachability edge. Thus the final root commits both the complete state and its transition record without either object naming a future ObjectId. Checkpoint cuts are the only normal predecessor-reclamation boundary. Each canonical `CommitMarker` is independently encoded and hashed before packing. `MarkerRef { marker_oid, commit_seq }` is the transaction-history identity used by branch heads, version origins/retirements, checkpoints, MMR leaves, and merges. A `MarkerBatch` merely packs length-delimited marker bytes; the object-location index maps each marker OID to its authenticated `(batch_encoding, offset, length)` subobject locator. Objects are assembled in uniquely named staging files, fsynced, sealed with an immutable trailer, renamed to final segment identity, and made directory-durable before publication. Staging files are unreachable state and are cleaned only by a recovery procedure that proves it.

`manifest.root` is a fixed two-slot bootstrap file; these are the only published durable bytes updated in place. Each 4 KiB `RootSlot` contains `{ magic, format, slot_generation, local_writer_fence_epoch, database_id, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, root_manifest_oid[32], RootBootstrap, tear_checksum }`. `RootBootstrap` supplies everything needed **before** the object-location index exists: the complete canonical `CipherDescriptorWithoutDigest` for this root; its complete `EncodingDescriptorWithoutId` (including transfer length, both OTI fields, symbol size/count, and symbol-auth profile); `CiphertextId`, `EncodingId`, and ciphertext digest; a complete specialized `ContiguousSpan` placement descriptor containing placement epoch, failure-domain policy and domain ID, segment/offset/length, and symbol-inventory digest; and a noncircular opener bundle for both the root DEK and `K_oid` (inline wrapped keys or authenticated KMS/HSM locators plus wrap digests). Recovery locates the span, scans and authenticates its exact symbol inventory, verifies the inventory digest and reconstructed `PlacementId`, reconstructs the exact object AAD and encoding transcript, decodes the root, verifies its keyed ObjectId, and only then consults its indexes. No descriptor or identity-key field comes from the object being opened. `ObjectId` is already the full logical digest; there is no redundant `root_full_digest`. Recovery rejects a root whose authenticated namespace/database/incarnation tuple disagrees with the slot.

Publication alternates slots, writes one complete slot with `pwrite`, and performs every required file and parent-directory durability action. Recovery first discards slots whose fixed record fails structural/tear validation, then selects the highest remaining generation. Once a slot is structurally credible, authentication or mandatory-closure failure triggers symbol/replica repair and then **fails closed**; it never silently rolls back to an older authenticated state that may precede an acknowledged commit. Using an older generation requires the explicit restore/rollback protocol in §16, with fencing, incarnation advancement, and an audit/transparency record.

Replicated semantics and local recovery metadata are distinct. Immutable `LogicalStatePayload` contains `{ applied_logical_command_seq, applied_commit_seq }` plus the authenticated database/security-namespace/incarnation tuple, canonical global transaction-marker head, branch-head map and merge ledger, catalog/schema, graph-partition state, ID allocator/continuity, constraint/resource/privacy ledgers and external privacy-continuity link, transaction/DP-outcome indexes, checkpoint/retention map, derived/replay registries, delivery-lease registry, HLC, topology/policy/revocation, replicated key/signing/capability-verifier roots, audit-ticket/audit/audit-continuity/transparency/backup roots, GC-decision root, and configuration-payload root. The sibling `LogicalCommandRecord` and outer `LogicalStateRoot` provide the command head as described above. `LogicalCommandSeq` advances for **every** canonical state-machine mutation (transaction, schema/policy/revocation, delivery lease, DP/accounting, key/audit/configuration/GC, and so on); transaction-only `CommitSeq` advances only for graph commits and remains graph system time. Root ObjectIds must agree across replicas at the same logical-command prefix, not merely the same transaction prefix. Replica-local `RootManifest` repeats the identity tuple and contains `{ logical_state_root_oid, replica_id, local_writer_fence_epoch, metadata_tree_bootstraps, object_location_index_root, local_encoding/key-opener roots, prepared_payload_root, raft_state_root, local applied/durable/visible positions, active_audit_visibility_fence_ref?, nonretaining_predecessor_root_digest }`; it may have a different ObjectId on each replica. An active audit fence is also rooted from `RaftStateRoot` and strongly owns the deterministic unpublished candidate state until external resolution and its mirror command permit publication. Each local metadata index is a bootstrappable immutable tree, but its entries are explicitly nonretaining `WeakLocator`/`PhysicalRealizationRef` records: semantic/prepared roots determine liveness, then GC resolves each marked logical/subobject ID to the exact container encoding, placement, and key opener it needs. Mere index membership never roots data, while a live subobject marks its containing physical realization. Each tree root's exact descriptor/location/tree-key opener is embedded in the manifest. Every authenticated internal edge carries the complete child descriptor and locator plus an authenticated child-key opener—normally `wrapped_child_dek_under_tree_key`, its wrapping profile/AAD, and wrap digest—because opening one tree key does not magically derive independently random child DEKs. The tree therefore never needs itself to locate or decrypt itself. Once opened, it resolves ordinary objects, prepared payloads, and `raft_state_root`. The predecessor digest is audit metadata, **not a GC reachability edge**. Snapshots retain both roots but semantic replay pins `LogicalStateRoot`, never a physical locator map.

The current `RootManifest` is deliberately absent from every metadata-tree generation that it bootstraps: its slot's `RootBootstrap` is its sole opener, and a tree `closure_digest` covers only that already-constructed tree closure, excluding the slot, the current manifest, and the bootstrap bytes. A later generation may index an older manifest only as nonretaining history after that older identity is known. This exclusion prevents a future-root identity from entering the tree whose identity the root must already name.

Exactly one process per replica directory may publish. It holds an OS-supported exclusive writer lease and advances `local_writer_fence_epoch` on takeover. The fence lives in the slot, replica-local root, and local segment/seal metadata used to reject a stale publisher; it is deliberately excluded from canonical capsule/marker/`LogicalStateRoot` bytes, so replicas can agree. Read-only multi-process opens are supported. Filesystems without the required lock and durability semantics are rejected by `fgdb doctor`, not silently accepted.

### 5.2 Commit protocol

Single-node and replicated commits use the same state machine; single-node mode is the quorum-one specialization. The durable unit is **not** the user's unresolved intent log. It is a post-rebase, post-constraint, post-SSI canonical effect capsule.

No caller can submit bare mutation intents. `fgdb-authz-types` defines a nonconstructible `AuthorizedMutationPermit` produced only by the trusted policy verifier. It binds database security namespace/incarnation, tenant/principal/issuer, authority digest and non-widenable operation ceiling, policy-semantics ObjectId, policy and revocation indices, root/snapshot and branch scope, exact read `GraphReferenceBinding[]` and write `CoordinateSchemaBinding[]`, topology epoch, expiry, channel/session binding where applicable, and a unique nonce. The coordinator verifies the permit's provenance and scope, recomputes authorization over the **final** effects and any `CoordinateSchemaTransition[]`, and orders the authorization decision against revocation/policy changes. Warden administers issuance later, but the verifier, permit type, and fail-closed revocation interface are W1/W2 dependencies—not optional server middleware.

Re-evaluable intents bind `IntentSemanticsOid`, the content digest of intent-schema version, `language_contract_oid`, semantic/scalar profiles, binder/normalizer version, and every procedure/UDF/module artifact that influenced intent construction. The merge ladder evaluates canonical typed intents under this object, never reparses source text under a new corrigendum or server default; if an artifact is unavailable, rebase fails closed.

1. **Propose.** A writer submits `TxnProposal { snapshot, expected_write_coordinates[], intent_semantics_oid, intents, predicate_witnesses, authorized_mutation_permit, ephemeral_reservation, requested_durable_charge_effects, txn_token }`. It may precompute physical candidates, but no candidate capsule is publishable truth. `txn_token` and every server handle are scoped as §13/App. D require.
2. **Order and validate.** The WriteCoordinator deterministically orders a group, verifies permit provenance/scope, writer fence, schema/topology/policy/revocation epochs, resource reservation, and expected branch heads; then it performs FCW/SSI validation and the merge ladder when necessary. Each accepted transaction is evaluated against the committed basis plus earlier accepted effects in that group. A rebase produces new reads, writes, constraints, and effects; it never blesses an old capsule.
3. **Canonicalize final effects.** Resolve temporary IDs and generated keys; evaluate CAS/upsert/no-op behavior; expand cascades; compute explicit logical retractions and insertions with `BirthOrdinal`s only for genuinely new identities; preserve source origin and assign `BranchAdmissionOrder` for imports; recompute final authorization including hidden-state cascades; validate constraints and the authoritative durable-quota ledger; materialize canonical `ResourceChargeEffect`/release effects; and rerun SSI over the final footprint. The result is a sequence-neutral `CommittedEffectSet` plus `LogicalDeltaTemplate` (§9.1), independent of block/run layout. The later canonical `MarkerRef` supplies `CommitSeq`, so apply derives origin/admission/effect order and ordered delta time without changing the approved capsule digest.
4. **Finalize capsules.** Persist one canonical `LogicalDeltaTemplate`, then construct `CommittedEffectCapsule { read_snapshot_basis, validated_apply_basis, intent_semantics_oid, read_graph_reference_bindings, validated_write_coordinate_bindings, coordinate_schema_transitions, topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, committed_effects, final_read_witnesses, final_write_summary, constraint_evidence, authorization_decision_ref: StrongRef<AuthorizationDecisionRecord>, authorization_decision_digest, resource_transition_refs: StrongRef<ResourceLedgerTransition>[], resource_charge_effects, txn_outcome_preparation_ref: StrongRef<TxnOutcomePreparationRecord>, logical_delta_template_ref: StrongRef<LogicalDeltaTemplate>, logical_delta_template_digest, optional_physical_accelerators, txn_token, final_effect_digest }`. A repeated digest is an integrity cross-check, never a replacement for the typed strong edge. The template digest must match the referenced bytes and the template must match the final effect set; neither field may appear without the other. Physical patches carry an apply-basis digest and may be ignored; unresolved or conditional commands are forbidden. Capsule builders return bytes and a digest to the coordinator, which verifies them against its approved final effects.
5. **Payload durability barrier D1 and stable prepared ownership.** Seal and make directory-durable the final capsule **and its entire mandatory strong closure**: coordinate effect payloads, `LogicalDeltaTemplate`, schema/constraint/resource transitions, authorization decision, merge record, outcome-preparation record, optional accelerator bytes actually referenced by the command, and every newly introduced semantics/module/profile object. Its immutable base inventory defines `PreparedOwnershipId = H("fgdb:prepared-owner:v1", storage_member, replica_namespace_or_incarnation, prepare_id, configuration_epoch, base_closure_inventory_digest)`. On every replica or availability donor issuing a receipt, create an immutable replica-local `PreparedCommitRecord { prepared_ownership_id, prepare_id, record_generation, predecessor_ref, database/incarnation/configuration/fence tuple, txn_token, capsule_ref, command_ref?, base_closure_inventory_digest, current_closure_inventory_digest, strong_logical_refs[], exact_realizations[{ object_or_subobject_id, encoding_id, placement_id, key_opener_ref }], status, receipt_nonce }`; its typed references and predecessor are strong. Every successor repeats the stable ownership ID and identical base closure, while its current closure is identical or a strict authenticated superset—for example after adding the completed command. `PreparedPayloadRoot` maps stable ownership ID to the latest record; no record is mutated in place. Insert the record, publish authenticated nonretaining locator entries, persist the updated `RootManifest`, and durably publish a `RootSlot` **before** D1 is complete or a payload receipt may be emitted. Pre-existing objects are revalidated and promoted into the prepared closure; a digest or locator without reachable authenticated bytes is insufficient. A `PayloadAvailabilityCertificate` covers this exact **pre-command base closure** under the configured failure-domain predicate, including the quorum-one local predicate. Each receipt binds the stable ownership ID and base-closure digest; an observed record OID/root generation is diagnostic freshness, never the sole ownership identity. Bytes not yet admitted to a prepared root remain staging data and are unreachable after a crash.
6. **Authorize, command, order, and publish barrier D2.** Immediately before ordering, the coordinator revalidates expiry/current revocation-policy state and proves the final effects remain within the permit ceiling. It then persists the normative immutable `CommitCommand { command_version, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, txn_token, capsule_ref: StrongRef<CommittedEffectCapsule>, logical_delta_template_ref: StrongRef<LogicalDeltaTemplate>, authorization_decision_ref: StrongRef<AuthorizationDecisionRecord>, resource_transition_refs: StrongRef<ResourceLedgerTransition>[], txn_outcome_preparation_ref: StrongRef<TxnOutcomePreparationRecord>, payload_availability_certificate_ref: StrongRef<PayloadAvailabilityCertificate>, expected_logical_command_seq, expected_branch_heads, topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, proposed_physical_hlc, final_effect_digest }`. The certificate already exists and covers the base payload closure, so the command does not participate in its own availability identity. The coordinator publishes a prepared-record successor that adds and strongly owns the completed command, then root-syncs it. **Every durable database, including embedded and server single-node posture, orders through the same internal Raft log; single node is a one-member/quorum-one configuration.** Only `:memory:` runs the same state machine without a durability claim. After log acceptance, the Raft closure and certificate donors retain the command/base closure through apply and compaction. A prepared owner may abandon only an unreceipted, provably unordered attempt under a current writer/configuration fence, or a receipted attempt after consensus proves it cannot be ordered and every certificate/configuration retention floor has expired. Timeout or process death alone is never abandonment proof.

   Deterministic transaction apply verifies the command and closure, assigns the next `LogicalCommandSeq`, assigns the next transaction-only `CommitSeq`, and advances HLC as `max(increment(previous_commit_hlc), normalize(proposed_physical_hlc))`; it canonically builds the standalone `CommitMarker`/`MarkerRef`, terminal outcome and other applied records, the new `LogicalStatePayload`, then the `LogicalCommandRecord`, and finally the outer `LogicalStateRoot` in that acyclic order. Semantic ownership transfers into those objects before the prepared record may become terminal. Every other canonical schema/policy/revocation/delivery/accounting/key/audit/configuration/GC operation is a typed `ControlCommand` and follows the same payload→record→root construction, incrementing `LogicalCommandSeq` but not `CommitSeq`. `ControlCommand.typed_payload_ref` always names a sequence-neutral pre-order spec—such as `TxnSubmissionSpec`, `CheckpointInstallSpec`, `IdRangeGrantSpec`, `MergePrepareSpec`, `DpTransitionSpec`, `DeliveryLeaseTransitionSpec`, `AuditTicketAdmissionSpec`, `AuditTerminalSpec`, `AuditContinuityMirrorSpec`, `KeyDestroyProposal`, `KeyDestroyFinalizeSpec`, `GcProposal`, or `ConfigurationTransitionSpec`—never the applied record or resulting state. Applied records may name the already identified `ControlCommand` plus their assigned logical-command sequence; they may not use `StrongCommandRef` to name their own future `LogicalCommandRecord`. Actual Raft configuration entries are paired with typed configuration control commands and advance logical-command time; heartbeat/read-index/no-op bookkeeping advances neither sequence. Without an audit visibility fence, the local manifest points to the new logical root and the alternate slot publishes it. With a fence, the slot publishes a local manifest that still points to the prior visible logical root while `RaftStateRoot` strongly owns the new candidate; only the external-resolution mirror command may replace it with the final visible root (§12.8). The client receives `Committed(seq)` only after one-member or multi-member consensus, local D2 apply, configured acknowledgement durability, durable outcome record, completed protected-audit transition, and final root publication. A later revocation cannot retroactively undo a committed transaction; it fences subsequent work.

`txn_token` is the opaque durable idempotency identity inside §13's MACed `TxnOutcomeCapability`, scoped to database security namespace/incarnation, tenant/principal, authority ceiling, and an issuance/expiry interval. A capsule's `TxnOutcomePreparationRecord` declares `expected_prior_state = Absent | Submitted { state_version, state_digest }`. Normal synchronous commit may atomically move `Absent` straight to a terminal record; an optional `ControlCommand(kind=TxnSubmissionSpec)` first creates `Submitted` when an API has promised durable asynchronous status before terminal ordering. Commit/abort apply accepts only `Absent` or the exactly matching submitted version, so concurrent retries serialize on one token and can never fabricate the missing Submitted state. `TxnOutcomeIndex` in `LogicalStatePayload` maps the token to one authenticated `TxnOutcomeRecord { Submitted | Committed(marker_ref,effect_digest) | Aborted(reason_class) }`; submitted/aborted control results cite their already identified command and applied sequence, while committed results cite the already constructed marker. Before any terminal outcome is returned, that record is durably ordered through the same Raft log; concurrent retries join the same state. Records and verifier keys remain through token expiry plus clock-skew/retry/audit policy and the advertised outcome-lookup floor. After collection, the cryptographic token remains permanently non-reusable and is rejected as `TokenExpired`, never treated as a new transaction. Cancellation before ordering may durably abort; cancellation after publication begins may return `Unknown(txn_token)`, resolved only through scoped commit-status lookup. It never promises rollback of a possibly committed transaction.

The protocol has two **durability barriers**, not literally two syscalls: each barrier includes every file and parent-directory sync required by the platform. Group commit amortizes both. Recovery starts from the highest valid root slot, reconciles `PreparedPayloadRoot` with the one-member or multi-member Raft log, completes every committed-but-unapplied command, and only then considers fenced abandonment/collection of prepared records. An active audit fence is recovered from the local Raft closure; its candidate remains retained and invisible while recovery reconciles the external terminal record and orders the mirror command. Recovery verifies the authoritative checkpoint and contiguous logical-command suffix; for transaction records it additionally verifies the contiguous transaction-marker subsequence, authenticates and decodes every referenced final capsule, and applies canonical effects. RaptorQ may repair damaged compatible symbols within the configured erasure model; it does not replace root redundancy, payload replication, or fail-closed recovery beyond that model.

### 5.3 Snapshots

A read snapshot is `{ database_id, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, logical_state_root_oid, local_root_manifest_oid, local_root_generation, logical_command_seq, high_seq, graph_reference_bindings[], topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, retention_lease }`, captured by following one validated slot through its local root to one immutable `LogicalStateRoot`. `logical_command_seq` fixes the complete canonical state-machine prefix; transaction-only `high_seq` fixes graph system time. Bindings are unique by `binding_role_id` (not by graph/branch), and every selected head is at or below the same `high_seq`; all repeated historical roles acquire their own ancestry/schema/authorization evidence under the snapshot's closure lease. Certificates/replay use the logical root and role-bearing bindings; the retention lease pins the local realization plus its logical closure. A version is visible only when its commit sequence is at or below `high_seq`, its creation marker belongs to that reference role's selected branch ancestry, and no visible retirement/supersession marker follows it. Integer-only visibility is legal solely inside a branch-filtered physical projection carrying a verified `(binding_role_id, graph, branch, head, lineage_digest)` token; global sequence interleaving alone never proves ancestry.

Snapshot acquisition uses `ReadSlotAndRoots -> RegisterLease(local_root_manifest_oid, logical_state_root_oid, gc_epoch) -> RevalidateSlotRootsAndEpoch`; if publication or GC races, it drops the tentative lease and retries. This closes the read-root/reclaim gap and is lock-free only if the lease-registry implementation proves that progress property—there is no blanket wait-free claim. A retention lease pins the **meaning and disk reachability** of its checkpoint/marker/index/object closure; it does not pin all potentially readable buffer frames. Only an active cursor/borrow pins a resident frame. Long-running analytics, backups, and replay manifests expose attributable lease cost, expiry policy, and cancellation requests, and nothing silently slides a snapshot or view watermark forward.

Each committed marker carries the deterministic `commit_hlc` produced by §5.2's ordered state transition; previous HLC state is part of `LogicalStatePayload` and every checkpoint. `AS OF TIMESTAMP t` resolves through a versioned `(commit_hlc, commit_seq)` index to the greatest visible marker in the selected branch ancestry whose HLC is at most `t`; local clock regression cannot change replicated bytes or order. The result certificate returns the effective marker/sequence.

### 5.4 One Version Universe: retention tiers = temporal database

The authoritative recovery equation for complete canonical state is:

`state(L, C) = verified RecoveryCheckpoint(L₀, C₀) + contiguous LogicalCommandRecord suffix (L₀, L]`,

where `L` is `LogicalCommandSeq` and `C` is transaction-only `CommitSeq`. A `RecoveryCheckpoint` contains the covered command/marker identities, chain hashes, HLC and partition projections plus a generated `CheckpointStateVector`. That vector has exactly one entry for **every** field in the versioned `LogicalStatePayload` schema, classified as `CopiedStrong`, `ProjectedSnapshot`, `ResetByExplicitCommand`, or `SelfExcluded`. It covers, among other fields, graph partitions, catalog/schema/topology/security, allocator and external identity continuity, constraints/resources, privacy ledger and external privacy continuity, DP requests/results, transaction outcomes, branch heads/merge ledger, retained-stream floor/`RetentionMap`, derived-generation, delivery-lease and replay registries, key/signing/capability-verifier reachability, audit tickets/audit/external audit continuity/transparency, backup/legal-hold, GC decision, and configuration-payload state. `recovery_checkpoint_ref` is the sole `SelfExcluded` payload field and is replaced by this checkpoint when installed; the covered prior command is stored separately. Any rebuildable reset is legal only when the contiguous suffix contains the named explicit reset command. Generated schema coverage makes an unclassified or silently omitted field a format/CI failure.

Recovery requires the first retained command record to have sequence `L₀ + 1` and name the covered command as predecessor. Transaction command records form a subsequence whose markers advance contiguously from `C₀`; control commands do not advance `CommitSeq`. An empty command suffix requires the logical root to agree with both covered heads. A predecessor-checkpoint digest is weak audit metadata unless a retention root explicitly promotes it. The checkpoint becomes authoritative only after representation-equivalence verification, payload durability, root publication, and the configured replica-durability gate. This is an explicit transfer of recovery authority, not a derived cache silently becoming primary.

Retired versions then cool through three query-retention tiers:

- **Tier 0 — live MVCC:** current version chains and unsealed deltas.
- **Tier 1 — warm logical history:** columnar logical effects sorted by `(element_id, commit_seq)`, directly queryable for recent `AS OF`/`BETWEEN` predicates.
- **Tier 2 — historical checkpoints:** full compressed partition snapshots plus a bounded logical-effect suffix. Optional inter-object delta compression names an explicit base ObjectId, forms an acyclic bounded-depth dependency graph, and is applied **before** each resulting payload is independently RaptorQ encoded. RaptorQ itself is never described as a delta codec.

Retention is configured per graph as `KEEP ALL`, `KEEP <duration|seqs>`, or `KEEP NONE`, but physical collection is governed by the conservative union of all roots. Every durable edge is explicitly strong, conditional, or a non-followed weak identity/digest. A capsule's sequence-neutral effect directory and its `LogicalDeltaTemplate` entries use `ConditionalCoordinateRef{oid,graph,branch}`; after commit, the enclosing marker supplies the coordinate's retention sequence. While a capsule is pending or otherwise has no committed marker context, traversal from `PreparedPayloadRoot` treats every such conditional edge as strong. Once committed, an authenticated checkpoint/`RetentionMap` cut may stop only the matching coordinate at or below its verified boundary. Logical-command and marker predecessor links use `ConditionalCommandRef` and `ConditionalMarkerRef`; their corresponding checkpoint cuts may stop only the matching predecessor axis. Branch/fork/merge bases and all other strong roots still win. Missing bytes without a matching cut are corruption; a proved cut returns `HistoryUnavailable`, not a partial result. A delta-resume promise instead roots a self-contained materialized `LogicalDeltaBatch`/`DeltaDeliveryEnvelope` through `DeliveryLeaseRegistryRoot` or prevents cutting its source range; otherwise the subscriber receives a visible `GAP` and new baseline. Weak source-template/marker identities do not pull old capsule history back into reachability. Effective floors also include stable prepared owners and consensus commands, transaction outcomes, branch-merge idempotency state, identity/privacy/audit external continuity, DP request+seed+result, audit tickets/resolutions, replay registry/manifests/archives, delivery and snapshot leases, backup/legal holds, current/prospective configuration floors, keys, and transparency policy. Overrides, cuts, and retained bytes are observable.

Point-in-time restore, temporal queries, raw CDC, and audit all reuse Chronicle ordering, but they are not identical retention products. PITR is bounded by retained checkpoints/effects; compliance audit has its separate signed retention chain (§12.8); replay requires its evidence closure (§8.6); and derived subscriptions consume canonical `LogicalDeltaBatch` objects (§9), not unresolved intents.

### 5.5 Compaction

Compaction follows `BUILD -> VERIFY_EQUIVALENCE -> D1_NEW_OBJECTS -> ROOT_PUBLISH -> WAIT_SAFE_POINT -> RETIRE`. It marks from both valid root slots and explicitly traverses logical-command/schema-complete-checkpoint cuts, stable prepared-payload and consensus-log ownership, every branch/base and branch-merge ledger, transaction/DP outcome, all external continuity links, delivery/replay registries and archives, audit ticket/resolution/audit roots, snapshot and delivery leases, backup/legal hold, key/key-wrap, current/prospective configuration, and transparency roots. It then resolves marked logical/subobject identities through nonretaining physical locators, builds new immutable encodings, proves old/new canonical logical digests equal, publishes a new root generation, and retires old encodings only after no valid older root or lease can expose them. Active marker batches are never rewritten in place. Staging namespace creation, sealing, rename, and retirement include the required directory durability operations.

Replica positions and CRDT gossip are observations, not GC authority. The safe point is computed under a consensus-fenced membership epoch (§14); loss of quorum, uncertain membership, an unacknowledged root, or a missing key wrap stops reclamation. The actor is supervised and rate limited against foreground latency. Space amplification, checkpoint cadence, and controller settings are empirical/configuration gates with named workloads and model assumptions, not safety invariants.

### 5.6 Branches: git for graphs

A branch manifest is `{ graph, branch_id, fork_parent, fork_parent_head, fork_seq, base_checkpoint?, key_envelope_root, policy }`. Branch creation adds only metadata and recipient/key wraps, so its **data-copy complexity** is O(1); a durable creation still pays the root-publication latency and is never promised below an fsync-independent microsecond constant. Reads select the branch head and follow explicit branch-parent links atop structurally shared objects.

Branch merge uses one explicit **squash-merge visibility model**. Against declared merge bases, the evaluator computes the canonical logical difference contributed by each source and emits ordinary target-branch retractions/insertions at one new target `MarkerRef`; source heads are provenance/idempotency inputs, not additional visibility parents. The target head therefore follows exactly one target predecessor, and reads never need to guess whether imported source marker ranges became ancestors. A source-created element keeps its globally unique VId/EId and immutable `OriginBirthOrder`; the target version's `created_by` is the merge marker. If a query explicitly requests branch-arrival order, it uses a distinct `BranchAdmissionOrder { merge_marker_seq, merge_ordinal, element_id }` fixed by the resolution policy—`InsertionOrder` never silently rewrites an element's origin.

`MergeRecord { merge_token, merge_request_commitment, target, target_basis, source_heads[], merge_bases[], imported_range_tokens[], imported_range_keys[], resolution_policy_oid, resolution_digest, final_effect_digest }` is hash-linked from both capsule and marker. Each range token binds source branch, inclusive weak marker identities, base, and source chain digest without pretending that the source range became target ancestry. Merge idempotency has two independent keys. A graph-effect-free typed `PrepareMerge { client_request_nonce, request_commitment }` control command first durably maps the caller/target-scoped nonce to one unguessable stable `merge_token` and returns a MACed `MergeOutcomeCapability` containing that token, request commitment, database/security namespace, target coordinate, tenant/principal authority ceiling, policy/revocation state, issuance/expiry, and verifier-key epoch. Retrying preparation with the same scoped nonce returns the same capability; evaluation/charging cannot begin until that capability is acknowledged and resubmitted. `BranchMergeLedgerRoot` maps the token to the immutable original request plus `InFlight | Committed{target_marker:WeakMarkerIdentity,final_effect_digest} | Rejected{stable_reason_digest}`. Transaction apply validates the token/request/range keys and atomically installs `Committed` plus coverage with the merge marker; a deterministic pre-effect rejection uses a typed `MergeRejectSpec` control command to install `Rejected`. A retry with the capability returns the original outcome even if the target head has since advanced.

Separately, every requested source interval derives `ImportedRangeKey = H("fgdb:imported-range:v1", database_namespace, target_graph_branch, source_branch, source_range_and_chain_digest, merge_semantics_oid, resolution_policy_oid)`, deliberately excluding `target_basis`. The ledger maintains canonical imported coverage by source chain. A new request that exactly or partially overlaps committed coverage returns `AlreadyImported` or, only under a declared subtract-previously-imported policy, evaluates the canonical uncovered remainder; it never reapplies the overlap. `target_basis` remains an FCW/head-CAS validation input, not an idempotency key. Token outcomes and imported coverage are ordered with the merge command and retained in a compact authenticated non-forgetting map across checkpoint/history cuts, backup/restore, reconfiguration, and GC. Weak terminal identities prevent duplicate application without retaining old marker/capsule bytes forever. Only intent families with proved associative, commutative, idempotent joins may claim order independence. All other eligible operations use the resolution policy's pinned total order; unresolved conflicts produce a capability-redacted conflict graph. A vague cocycle check is not a substitute for algebraic laws or explicit ordering.

Shared encrypted objects keep their existing object DEKs. A branch introduces a write-key epoch and a key-envelope closure granting access to exactly its fork state and descendants; handoff wraps that closure to a recipient public key. It does not reveal sibling branches or parent commits after the fork. Revocation cannot erase plaintext already received and is described honestly as future-access/key-wrap revocation.

Use cases remain what-if analysis, agent isolation, schema experiments, and reproducible feature snapshots. The 10k-branch objective is an empirical gate parameterized by retained deltas, key wraps, subscriptions, and lease cost—not an assumption embedded in correctness.

### 5.7 Scrubbing and self-healing

A background scrubber verifies record framing, authenticated headers, ciphertext digests, full logical digests after decode, and decode proofs; repairs missing symbols only within one `EncodingId`; and escalates insufficient local symbols to replica or backup recovery. Twenty-percent local repair overhead is an erasure budget inside the declared source-block/failure-domain placement model, not a promise against whole-device or correlated loss.

Two complementary coverage mechanisms are mandatory:

1. **Deterministic exhaustive sweeps** eventually cover every reachable encoding and provide the non-statistical detection guarantee.
2. **Keyed random sampling without replacement**, stratified by device, segment age, object kind, source block, and encoding profile, provides earlier statistical evidence. Sampling keys are committed after each sample epoch so an adversarial placement cannot predict the next sample. Every e-process record names its population, sampling design, dependence assumptions, alpha, and effective sample size; it is a statistical claim (`FG-CAL-*`), never proof that no corruption exists.

A CTMC-based MTTDL report remains useful as `FG-CFG-01`, with fitted failure rates, confidence/sensitivity ranges, repair bandwidth, placement assumptions, and model version. `fgdb doctor` may reject a deployment profile below its modeled target, but the result is labeled a configuration-model gate rather than an invariant. Optional donor possession challenges may trigger full audit or reseed; they prove responses to sampled challenges, not Byzantine correctness of the donor or semantic correctness of an object.


### 5.8 Verifiable to outsiders: the accumulator and transparency layer

Content addressing, the marker chain hash, and decode proofs make the database auditable *by its operator*. This layer cashes the same architecture into proofs a party who does **not** trust the server can check — the missing half of the trust story for agent memory (B6), cross-organization branches, and regulated pipelines:

1. **Global MMR scope.** The MMR commits the globally ordered marker entries with explicit leaf indices and tree size. Inclusion proves that one marker belongs to that committed history; consistency proves append-only extension relative to a previously trusted root. Neither proof establishes the semantic correctness of the capsule nor completeness of a filtered query result.
2. **Two transparency modes without branch-name leakage.** `LocalRollbackDetection` lets a client cache a signed checkpoint and reject rollback relative to its own last root. Only `Witnessed { witness_set_epoch, threshold }` may claim cross-client non-equivocation: checkpoints include log size, MMR root, an opaque domain-separated `branch_head_commitment_root`, previous checkpoint, operator signature, and the required independent witness signatures. Branch identities/heads are revealed only through capability-authorized inclusion/consistency proofs or an explicit metadata-declassification policy; deployments may instead maintain independent per-security-domain logs. Witnesses can sign opaque roots without learning branches. Trust bootstrap, witness/key rotation, maximum checkpoint age, gossip/cross-logging, and fail-closed behavior when the threshold is unavailable are part of the deployment profile. Optional witnesses never justify a split-view-prevention claim.
3. **Feed proof scopes.** Raw CDC over the global log can prove continuity with contiguous leaf/range proofs. A `SUBSCRIBE MATCH` frame can prove membership of its source commits, but not that the server evaluated the predicate completely. Filtered completeness is claimed only if each commit carries an authenticated, capability-domain-specific delta commitment—including an empty commitment—and the client receives a complete range of those commitments. Otherwise the API labels the proof `membership_only`.
4. **Proof-carrying transfer.** Backup and replica transfer archives object inventories, full digests, `EncodingId`s, key wraps, and decode proofs. Those prove byte closure and successful decode; restore additionally verifies the canonical logical checkpoint digest before promotion (§16).
5. **Authenticated point reads.** A sealed authorized namespace may expose Merkleized index roots, but a proof covers only the specified key/value and root. Absence, range completeness, capability projection, and freshness each require their corresponding proof and watermark.

FG-INV-08 requires authenticated global-history continuity. Non-equivocation is conditional on satisfying the declared witnessed-transparency policy and is not claimed in local-only mode.

## 6. Strata: The Storage Engine (Bet B2)

Strata resolves the write/scan/space trilemma by refusing to pick one point on it. Authoritative incidence is partitioned by stable vertex identity and edge type, never by mutable labels. Each `(vertex, direction, edge_type)` adjacency exists across three tiers, migrating by temperature.

```
        └─ seal ─────┘── compact (k-way merge, generational) ──┘
```

- **Tier I (inline micro-adjacency)**: below the diagram's three tiers sits a zeroth one. A small bounded vector in the vertex row stores the first few active `(direction, edge_type, descriptor)` entries; the descriptor may inline up to eight `(dst_VId, EId, prop_locator)` incidences. Mutable tiers never store a bare snapshot ordinal. Additional edge types live in a sparse immutable descriptor table, so schema cardinality cannot bloat every row. Overflow promotes to Tier D; demotion is a verified representation transition.
- **A logical adjacency list = merge(inline slot, delta blocks, [newest…oldest] sealed runs)**, exactly an LSM, except runs are graph-shaped and sorted by `(src_snapshot_ordinal, dst_snapshot_ordinal, EId)`. Tombstones and run holes carry creation/retirement marker references or a lineage-pinned effective-interval sidecar; a branch-agnostic scalar retire sequence or current hole bitmap may never hide an edge from a sibling branch or older snapshot. Compression-ratio numbers are benchmark results tied to named corpora and encodings, not universal facts.
- **Direction**: canonical forward runs always; reverse runs are declared synchronously maintained materializations enabled by default and may be disabled per edge type only with the registered reverse-scan fallback. Every present/required reverse family must equal the transpose of authorized forward incidence at each snapshot; disabling it changes performance, never semantics.
- **Partitioning**: runs are segmented by stable source partition and snapshot-ordinal ranges—the unit of compaction, buffering, statistics, checksums, and parallel scans. Labels use separate membership bitmaps and derived Beacon projections. Source-range ownership is an edge-cut/vertex-owner layout; a future hub-splitting vertex-cut mode is a different protocol and is named separately in §14.6.

### 6.2 Tier D internals

1. **Sorted edge blocks** (Sortledton lesson): 4 KiB blocks holding ≤256 `(dst_VId, EId, user_key_ref?, prop_row_ref, flags)` entries sorted by stable identity; binary search and SIMD lower-bound use canonical stable-key encoding. A vertex's Tier-D presence is 0 blocks (common), 1 block, or a two-level skip list of blocks (hubs).
2. **Per-block MVCC**: each block heads a version chain in a bump arena (frankensqlite `VersionArena` design, `prev_idx` links, FG-INV-03 ordering). Readers continue down the chain until they find the first version satisfying **all** of sequence, branch-ancestry, schema, retirement, and own-write visibility predicates; a newer sibling-branch version is skipped rather than terminating the search. The current-line fast path may collapse this to one compare only under a verified lineage token.
3. **Concurrency and visibility**: writers take an eager block lock or append transaction-private nodes to an escalated delta chain. Every node carries transaction identity and remains invisible until its global marker publishes; abort cleanup and commit stamping are EBR-safe. Readers merge only committed nodes visible in their branch snapshot. Giant hubs stripe the neighbor-ID space into independently locked/versioned ranges, and witness granularity follows the stripe.
4. **Hot-burst overflow**: a bounded per-partition append log absorbs spikes. Entries remain transaction-tagged until publication; the drain actor preserves global commit order and verifies the same canonical logical digest when sorting them into blocks. Admission backpressures before memory is exhausted.
5. **Property deltas** live beside adjacency deltas as row-patches; sealed property data is columnar (§6.4).

### 6.3 Tier R internals (sealed runs)

- **Layout per run segment**: header (fenceposts, stats, checksum, seq-range, `source_ordinal_map_id`, `destination_map_table[]`) · Elias–Fano offsets for O(1) list location · per-list `NeighborCodec` chosen from `EliasFano`, `StreamVByte { skip_table }`, or `DenseIntervals` · stable EIds/`OriginBirthOrder`s and replaceable property locators · versioned marker-aware hole/retire metadata. A source ordinal is meaningful only under the source map. Each encoded destination is either stable VId or `(destination_map_slot, dst_ordinal)`, where the slot names an explicit `{ destination_partition, OrdinalMapId }`; no ordinal crosses a map boundary untagged. Codec/map choice is covered by the logical-digest validator over stable IDs.
- **Compressed intersections with an honest contract**: Elias–Fano neighbor lists support rank/select directly. StreamVByte lists use skip/fence metadata and bounded blockwise decode; dense intervals use interval arithmetic. Loom intersects without **full-list** decompression where the codec permits it—there is no claim that Elias–Fano offsets provide rank/select over a separate varint stream.
- **Runs are generational**: seal (Tier D → R₀ run), then merge R₀…R_k per partition. A snapshot pins run generations via epochs; compaction publishes a new segment set atomically in the manifest and retires the old under lease — readers never block, never see mixed generations.
- **The merge policy is a governed family, not a constant**: per `(partition, edge_type, direction)`, a bounded leveled↔tiered family is evaluated in shadow and deployed as a pinned deterministic table; static size-tiering is the permanent fallback. Birth–death and stability analyses are named configuration models with sensitivity bounds, while dwell/hysteresis and measured churn are runtime guards. They do not prove universal absence of oscillation.

### 6.4 Property storage

Vertex property chunks are grouped by `(stable partition, property/storage family)`, not mutable label. Label scans apply versioned membership bitmaps before reading rows. Edge chunks are grouped by `(edge_type, property, run generation)` and sequential by replaceable run row, with a stable EId sidecar. Tier-D patches obey the same MVCC/branch visibility rules. Embedding matrices are generation-pinned inputs to vector indexes; “zero-copy” is used only when requested dtype, alignment, authorization, and snapshot generation actually match.

### 6.5 Vertex directory

Per stable partition and generation, a packed array maps snapshot-local dense ordinal to `{ stable VId, label-set ref, property locator, small active-descriptor vector, sparse descriptor-table ref }`; a reverse map resolves VId to that generation's ordinal. IDs are never recycled (§4.5). Degree is O(1) per **authorized** descriptor only after Warden masking; unauthorized edge types do not contribute. Directory and label-membership segments are versioned immutable objects.

### 6.6 Write amplification & space discipline

Write amplification is `all physical bytes written / canonical logical input bytes` and includes canonical forward plus any configured reverse-incidence materializations, properties, constraint and optimizer indexes, checkpoints, FEC, staging waste, and every merge level; reports publish the component breakdown. Space amplification names the logical baseline and includes key wraps, repair symbols, and retained history. Numerical targets in §17 are benchmark gates, not format truths. Every representation migration emits a decision card with state, candidates, cost interval, minimum dwell, uncertainty, and pinned deterministic fallback.

Under Sextant (the calibration discipline within B5, §15.0), the card machinery adds statistical evidence without changing safety semantics:

- **Migration evidence is sequential, while dwell remains a hard guard.** An SPRT may evaluate promote/demote evidence under an explicit observation model and loss matrix; its reported error rates are conditional on those assumptions. Minimum dwell and benefit-greater-than-conversion-cost-plus-uncertainty remain deterministic anti-thrash rules even when the model is invalid.
- **Policies remain on trial.** An e-process may compare a policy with its pinned fallback on a declared outcome stream. Promotion evidence is `FG-CAL-01`; failure of assumptions or evidence reverts to the fallback through a stream-sequenced `PolicyEpoch`. It is not a production safety-invariant checker.
- **Counterfactual audit has a support condition.** Logged selection probability permits importance-weighted or doubly robust evaluation only for candidate actions with positive logging-policy support and adequate effective sample size. A deterministic action table with zero support cannot evaluate arbitrary alternatives. Every report names overlap, clipping, estimator, sample size, and confidence assumptions (`FG-EVID-01`).
- **Learning never directly touches the serving path.** Continuous policies learn in shadow and deploy only as versioned deterministic tables. Any regret or coverage statement stays attached to its population and assumptions; the deployed table inherits no universal guarantee by distillation alone.
- **One regime clock.** All calibration windows and retune decisions key off a single changepoint detector (Bayesian online changepoint + Shiryaev–Roberts, §15.0) emitting logged regime-epoch IDs — controllers stop debouncing independently, and "policy changed because regime 47 began" becomes a replayable causal statement.

### 6.7 Buffer & memory management

- **Extent-based buffer manager** over variable-size segments (Umbra lesson): segment handles are swizzlable — hot segments pin to direct pointers; cold segments fault through the manager. Reads use optimistic epoch-protected access (asupersync EBR): version-stamped, retry-on-conflict, no reader latches (LeanStore OLC discipline).
- **Admission/eviction**: ARC and an S3-FIFO-style design share one policy trait and may be ghost-evaluated in shadow. A snapshot protects object generations from GC; only an active `BufferHandle` pins a frame in RAM. Frames needed later by the same snapshot may be evicted and faulted back. Ghost metadata is cleaned when object generations become unreachable, and scans use admission bypass/resistance.
- **I/O**: io_uring via asupersync fs; O_DIRECT with page-aligned buffers; readahead planned by the executor (runs declare their scan intent, the buffer manager prefetches segment chains — B-tree-descent prefetch generalized).
- **Query memory and scratch**: every query's resident scratch uses its region heap. Spill uses a separate `ScratchStore`: region-scoped, non-authoritative, non-fsynced, not RaptorQ-coded, outside the database manifest, and keyed by a startup-cleanable generation. Region close unlinks only its own proven scratch objects. Admission bounds all database-accounted allocations and preserves an emergency reserve; allocation failure or an operator whose minimum resident set cannot fit returns structured `ResourceExhausted` with no partial success unless the query explicitly requested truncation. The plan does not claim immunity from host overcommit, allocator failure, or unaccounted foundation defects.
- **NUMA**: partition→socket affinity for pinned segments; morsel scheduler prefers local partitions.

## 7. Transactions: Block-MVCC + Graph-SSI + Semantic Merge

### 7.1 Isolation model

- Default: **SERIALIZABLE** via SSI over canonical logical read/write dependencies, conservatively represented by the witness types in §7.3. SSI establishes an acyclic serialization-graph order; it does **not** assert that commit-marker order is the serialization order. Strict serializability additionally requires that a transaction snapshot include every commit whose acknowledgement completed before the transaction began and that publication/acknowledgement obey the real-time commit state machine in §5.2.
- Ordinary serializable read-only transactions may receive a serialization failure. `READ_ONLY DEFERRABLE` may wait for a safe snapshot and, once acquired, is immune to SSI abort. `READ_ONLY_HISTORICAL`, `SNAPSHOT`, `SNAPSHOT_FOLLOWER(max_lag)`, and `BRANCH_CAUSAL` remain named typed contracts; the server rejects rather than silently weakens a requested minimum.
- SSI transaction records, rw-edge summaries, and SIREAD witnesses survive commit until every transaction that overlapped their lifetime has ended. Reclamation is tied to an explicit overlap horizon, not the reader's commit. Coarsening under memory pressure is an atomic monotone replacement: the coarse superset becomes visible before fine entries are removed. Pressure may increase false aborts but never erase a possible dependency; admission returns `ResourceExhausted` rather than dropping correctness metadata.
- SIREAD witnesses never block. Mutations remain proposal-private until finalization computes the complete block/constraint/quota lock set. The coordinator acquires that set in canonical `(lock_class, graph, partition, object)` order; an implementation may instead use a proved no-wait abort protocol. It never waits while holding an unordered subset, and discovery of a missing lower-order lock releases without publication and restarts validation. Lock exclusivity is FG-INV-02; wait-for acyclicity and cancellation cleanup have separate model/oracle coverage.
- Writers conflict on final logical effects after the merge ladder, constraints, FCW, and SSI are reevaluated. The monotone state machine is `Active -> Validating -> FinalPrepared -> PayloadDurable -> OrderSubmitted -> CommandCommitted -> Applied(MarkerRef) -> Acknowledged`, with `Aborted` legal only before the irreversible ordering boundary and externally reported `Unknown` representing an unresolved observer outcome, not an internal rollback state. Single-node mode collapses consensus substates but not their ordering; cancellation and retries follow §5.2.
- Core local invariants include idempotent transaction-token outcomes, fenced unique block/constraint-lock ownership, and finite correctly ordered version chains, but those alone do not prove SI or SSI. Publication, branch visibility, deletes, own writes, witness soundness, and metadata lifetime are separate registry obligations (§15.5, Appendix F).

### 7.2 Granularity choices (and why)

Blocks (~256 edges) are the graph analogue of frankensqlite's pages: fine enough that writers to different neighborhoods never touch (fan-out of real workloads means most concurrent writes hit distinct vertices, let alone blocks), coarse enough that lock/siread tables stay small and cache-resident. Vertex-level would false-conflict on hubs; edge-level would drown in metadata. Hub blocks that *do* contend escalate to delta-chains (§6.2.3), which converts conflict into commutative appends — contention is handled by *changing the data structure*, not by tuning locks.

### 7.3 Phantom protection for pattern queries

SSI needs to protect both observed elements and observed **absence**. Every physical access path declares an abstract logical-read domain plus a conservative witness encoding:

```text
BlockRange         { descriptor, stable_low_key, stable_high_key, generation,
                     visible_version, routing_translation_epoch }
AdjacencyRange     { descriptor, low_key, high_key, gaps, descriptor_epoch }
LabelPartition     { label_expr, partition, predicate, gaps, scan_epoch }
IndexRange         { index_generation, low_key, high_key, gaps, early_stop_boundary }
NegativePattern    { canonical_predicate, quantified_domain }
PathCut            { path_semantics, automaton_state, mode_state_class,
                     settled_domain, unexpanded_edge_domain, cost_boundary,
                     feasible_time_domain, topology_epoch }
```

Each witness implementation supplies three executable contracts: `concretization(w)`, the set of logical positive and negative observations represented; `may_affect(final_write, w)`; and `refine(coarse, trace)`. A `BlockRange` is logical-keyed: split, seal, and compaction publish a mutation-routing translation from old generation/range to its covering descendants, retained until the SSI overlap horizon passes; an implementation unable to retain that translation emits `AdjacencyRange` directly. A bare physical block address is never a witness. FG-INV-06 requires both `concretization(refined) ⊆ concretization(coarse)` **and** preservation of every logically relevant positive read, gap, absence observation, early-stop boundary, and path cut. Refining a predicate to only elements returned or encountered is forbidden unless the binder proved it was a point predicate.

`WitnessTrace` is execution evidence, not automatically the predicate domain. `ORDER BY/LIMIT`, anti/semi joins, optional matches, negative patterns, uniqueness probes, and early-terminated scans register the gaps or remaining domains whose absence affected the result. Path witnesses use the same mode-aware product state and proved dominance relations as §8.2; `(NFA state, partition, cost)` alone is not accepted for TRAIL, SIMPLE/ACYCLIC, temporal, capture-sensitive, or negative-cost semantics.

An apparent overlap may be refined under a bounded resource reservation. If exact overlap survives, or the budget expires, validation conservatively aborts and reports whether refinement was truncated. Canonical predicate normalization improves witness reuse but is not itself a proof of semantic equivalence; every rewrite used for witness identity carries a law certificate. The refine-vs-abort policy is a performance decision card and cannot change the conservative-abort fallback.

The default SERIALIZABLE claim is gated on: formal soundness of every witness constructor and refinement; preservation through every optimizer rewrite; exhaustive small-graph differential histories against an independent reference dependency checker; and model-based tests inserting precisely the missing edges/vertices/index keys that could invalidate negative and path results. Until a witness family passes those gates, queries using it are rejected in SERIALIZABLE mode rather than silently downgraded.

### 7.4 The semantic merge ladder (and why graphs love it)

On base-drift at commit (block changed since snapshot), before aborting:

1. **Semantic re-evaluation**: evaluate the original intents against the current branch heads under a capability-stripped `Cx`. Eligibility depends on operation semantics, active constraints, captured reads, property merge algebra, and branch policy. An edge addition is not coordination-free when uniqueness or cardinality makes it non-monotone.
2. **Structured physical reuse**: a disjoint physical patch may be retained only as an optional accelerator after the semantic result is fixed and only when its apply-basis digest still matches.
3. **Final-effect regeneration**: produce a new `CommittedEffectSet`, permanent IDs/keys, constraint reads, canonical logical deltas, write summary, and witnesses. Rerun FCW, SSI, and all affected constraints. Then build and persist a **new** capsule as required by §5.2; no successful rung may publish the pre-rebase capsule or its stale availability receipts.
4. **Abort/retry** with a capability-redacted machine-readable conflict report when no safe resolution exists.

frankensqlite built this ladder to rescue B-tree page conflicts. Selected graph intents have promising algebraic merge opportunities, but actual eligibility is constraint- and read-context-dependent; hit rate and skew benefit are benchmark questions, not architectural facts. The same evaluator serves transaction rebase, branch merge (§5.6), and multi-writer preparation (§14.4) through distinct typed policies. Rung-1 replay determinism is enforced by construction: the rebase evaluator runs under a capability-stripped `Cx`—no clock, entropy, network, or filesystem—and accepts every needed snapshot/constraint input explicitly, so ambient nondeterminism is inexpressible and missing inputs fail closed.

Three formal strengthenings of the ladder:

- **Rung eligibility is proved for a complete context.** `ConsistencyClass` is derived from the intent algebra only after composing schema constraints and read dependencies. CALM-style monotonicity is sufficient only within the declared lattice and authority domain; a context-free table is a cache of proved classifications, never the source of truth.
- **Multi-branch semantics are explicit.** Only registered associative, commutative, idempotent joins are order-independent. Other resolvable intents use the pinned total order `(source_head_seq, source_branch_id, source_intent_ordinal, element_id)` recorded in the merge policy; unresolved cases return conflicts. Merge records carry the target visibility parent plus source heads as non-parent provenance, all bases/imported ranges, policy, and final digest (§5.6), so retries are idempotent and order is auditable without inventing multiple target ancestries. No general cocycle claim is made without a formal merge algebra and higher-order coherence proof.
- **An invariant-preserving rung between commutative-replay and abort.** *Escrow* merge modes preserve numeric invariants (x ≥ 0, quota ceilings, degree caps) without coordination by pre-distributing rights per branch/replica with an idempotent transfer ledger; depletion escalates that intent to the ordered path. The model-checked transfer protocol lives in the sim lab's standing scenarios. This is the missing middle for exactly the counter-shaped properties (inventories, budgets, rate limits) that LWW mangles and aborts throttle.

### 7.5 Bulk & DDL paths

Two bulk paths are legal. **Offline fenced load** operates on an empty/new graph under an exclusive graph epoch. **Online load** pins a snapshot, builds sealed objects, records the global marker position, catches up every subsequent logical delta, enters ordinary coordinator validation for constraints/SSI/branch-head CAS, and atomically publishes runs plus one canonical logical effect digest. Neither path may overwrite concurrent Tier-D state. Online index/schema builds use the same snapshot-build → delta-tail → validate → publish protocol and advertise their watermark throughout.

### 7.6 The write path as asupersync choreography

WriteCoordinator is a supervised actor. Commit requests arrive on a two-phase MPSC whose reserve/commit protocol prevents a cancelled producer from publishing a partial queue entry. Its response is a oneshot obligation whose unresolved terminal state is detected by the obligation/leak oracle; detection is not confused with impossibility. Group-commit batching uses bounded queues and the three-lane scheduler with an explicitly tested fairness/deadline policy, rather than claiming that an EDF hint alone proves starvation freedom. These are typed runtime contracts plus lab-tested database state-machine obligations, not guarantees inherited without integration evidence.

## 8. Loom: Languages, Algebra, Optimizer, Execution (Bet B3)

### 8.1 Language surface

- **Native: GQL (ISO/IEC 39075:2024)** — graph pattern matching including quantified path patterns, path modes (WALK/TRAIL/ACYCLIC/SIMPLE), the edition's registered shortest/count selectors, label expressions, `LET`/`FILTER`/`FOR`, composite queries, catalog/DDL, and transactions/sessions. The exact minimum closure and optional ISO feature IDs are frozen in `LanguageContract`; an in-flight edition/corrigendum is a different contract and never “lands” silently.
- **openCypher compatibility layer**: the pragmatic on-ramp. GQL and Cypher have separate lexer/parser/profile adapters but converge only after binding and semantic normalization; documented divergences and the openCypher TCK prevent a parser shortcut from changing meaning.
- **Versioned FQL dialect extensions**: temporal, branch, Prism, retrieval, materialized-view, subscription, certificate, branch-admin, and `CHEAPEST`/cost-selector syntax beyond the pinned 2024 ISO feature map is enabled only by an explicit `FQL <major.minor>` declaration or `FQL`-prefixed production. The plan does not call unprefixed future-standard-looking keywords “namespaced.”
- **Datalog surface**: `fgdb-datalog` compiles the validated finite/stratified subset in §9.3 to the same typed algebra. Recursion, negation, and aggregates are accepted only when their termination and incrementalization contracts are registered.

`LanguageContract` is a canonically encoded, content-addressed tagged union with common `{ grammar_oid, lexer_profile_oid, normalizer_oid, semantic_profile_oid, scalar_profile_oid, unicode_data_oid }` fields and one surface payload. `Gql` carries `{ edition, corrected_printing, corrigenda[], conformance_profile, mandatory_feature_closure[], graph_type_conformance, selected_minimum_feature_ids[], optional_feature_ids[], unicode_version, ucs_basic_profile }`; `graph_type_conformance` is exactly `Open{feature:GG01}` or `Closed{feature:GG02, element_type_feature:GG20|GG21|GG22|GG23}`. `OpenCypher` carries the exact language/specification revision, grammar and TCK commit, supported scenario manifest, and documented-divergence ObjectId; `Fql` carries its declared major/minor grammar, extension-feature closure, and GQL-base contract; `Datalog` carries the accepted finite/stratified grammar subset plus recursion, negation, aggregate, and operator-registry ObjectIds. The GQL 2024 contract includes the ISO minimum-conformance closure and every prerequisite of its selected feature IDs, supplies the required character-string, Boolean, signed-integer, and floating property types, and pins Unicode 13.0 or later plus the mandated UCS_BASIC behavior; optional IDs are separate. Draft Edition 2 syntax can exist only under a visibly draft contract and cannot satisfy the 2024 claim. No loose dialect string or server default can change accepted syntax or semantics after binding.

Parsing never produces executable plans directly, and preparation never mistakes `@now` for a permanent head. `fgdb-bind` first constructs `PreparedQueryTemplate { language_contract_oid, catalog_contract, graph_reference_specs[{ binding_role_id, graph_id, branch_selector, temporal_selector, required_schema_contract }], parameter_types, non_widenable_authority_ceiling, typed_logical_template, output_schema_contract }`. It owns name/scope and correlated-reference resolution, deterministic role assignment, group variables, parameter inference/coercion, null and duplicate semantics, graph/table shape checking, path legality/termination, capability-domain injection, and incrementalizability classification. `PREPARE` therefore cannot freeze an execution option that it never received. At each execution the caller supplies an `AnswerRequirement` (default `Exact`); any query-language approximation clause narrows it, and an empty intersection is rejected. After parameters and temporal selectors are known, snapshot acquisition resolves every spec into a concrete `GraphReferenceBinding`; binding then produces `BoundQuery { prepared_template_oid, logical_state_root_oid, graph_reference_bindings[], topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, authority_digest, answer_requirement, instantiated_logical_plan, output_schema }`. Prepared handles pin the template and may be reused only while its catalog/schema compatibility predicates hold under same-or-narrower authority. Physical/logical plan-cache entries, certificates, and replay pin the concrete `BoundQuery`; a cross-graph or historical self-join plan cannot substitute one reference's head/schema for another. Optimizers consume only this bound typed IR.

### 8.2 Semantics discipline

GQL's formal-semantics literature is tracked; where the standard leaves latitude (result ordering, path enumeration order, duplicate semantics under morphism modes), FrankenGraphDB pins behavior via CGSE tie-break policies (§8.6) and documents each pin. Temporal clauses get algebraic semantics (snapshot-relativized pattern matching; `BETWEEN` yields per-binding validity intervals with interval-algebra predicates).

Each pin set is a versioned **SemanticProfile** referenced from `LanguageContract`—not an ambiguous name such as bare `gql-2024-strict`—fixing null/UNKNOWN handling, duplicate semantics per morphism mode, ordering defaults, coercion rules, and tie-break bindings. Two results are comparable only under the same language+semantic+scalar contracts, and corrigendum/dialect drift becomes a new content-addressed artifact.

`CanonicalScalarProfile` defines equality, hashing, total ordering, collation, coercion, and durable/result encoding for every value, with property tests enforcing `equal ⇒ same_hash`, comparator totality/transitivity, and encode/decode/order coherence. `STRICT_PORTABLE` canonicalizes NaNs and signed zero; fixes decimal scale/rounding/overflow; stores timestamp instant, zone/offset semantics, and a content-addressed tzdb version; pins Unicode data/normalization/segmentation and each non-binary collation by ObjectId; fixes map-key order over canonical bytes; and rejects unavailable profile artifacts rather than consulting host locale/tzdb. Floating execution uses fixed logical reduction trees independent of SIMD width, fixes denormal/rounding behavior, forbids target-dependent contraction, and uses pinned math kernels. A scalar/SIMD fast path ships in strict mode only after bit-identity testing on every supported target; otherwise it is RELAXED and the certificate names target/build behavior.

Path expressions carry a rewrite-protected `PathSemantics { mode, selector, hops, cost, temporal, enumeration, tie_break }`. `All + WALK` requires a finite hop bound. Every potentially infinite enumeration requires a finite selector or explicit `MaxResults`/resource budget; truncation is visible result status and certificate state, never silent success. NaN/infinite weights, arithmetic overflow, and a reachable negative cycle that makes the requested WALK optimum unbounded have defined errors. Negative finite weights are legal only for mode/selector combinations with a registered exact algorithm and termination contract. Dijkstra/bidirectional kernels require nonnegative costs; the matrix below governs other cases.

Executor state is `PathStateKey { automaton_state, vertex, mode_state, capture_state_id, feasible_valid_time, cost_state }`, where `CaptureStateId` is a full content digest whose lookup verifies canonical capture bytes on collision; TRAIL carries persistent used-edge state and SIMPLE/ACYCLIC carry persistent visited-vertex state. Coalescing is allowed only through a mode-specific proved dominance relation. The same abstraction defines §7.3 path witnesses. The physical registry names valid combinations: bounded RPQ WALK uses NFA-product BFS/DFS; nonnegative shortest uses BFS/Dijkstra/bidirectional variants; hop-bounded negative WALK uses dynamic programming over hops; unbounded WALK may use Bellman–Ford-class logic only to prove a finite optimum or report a reachable negative cycle. Negative TRAIL/SIMPLE/ACYCLIC uses exact finite history-aware search with an exponential complexity/resource contract or is unavailable. Yen/Eppstein is used only where its preconditions match; temporal paths include the feasible interval in product state.

### 8.3 One algebra ("GLA" — Graph-Logical Algebra)

Typed logical operators over binding tables include `ScanVertices`, `ScanEdges`, `Values`, `IndexProbe`, `HybridSearch`, `Expand`, `VarLengthExpand`, `PathFind`, `FreeJoin`, `HashJoin`, `LeftOuterJoin`, `SemiJoin`, `AntiJoin`, correlated `Apply`, `Select`, `Project`, `Unnest`, `NullExtend`, `Aggregate`, `Distinct`, full `OrderBy`, `Limit{offset,count}`, `SetOp{Union|Intersect|Except, All|Distinct}`, `Fixpoint`, `TemporalSlice`, `TemporalJoin`, `ProcedureCall`, `GraphConstruct`, `SemiringMxV`, `Mutate`, `ViewDelta`, and `CertEmit`. FreeJoin is the conjunctive inner-join core; it does not impersonate optional, negative, correlated, or set-operation semantics. Relational and pattern operators share a cost model, while Appendix C normatively defines each operator's memory, factorization, cancellation, witness, incrementalization, path-preservation, authorization, answer-contract transfer, and replanning contracts.

Approximation has three deliberately different phase types. `AnswerRequirement = Exact | AllowApproximate { permitted_kinds[], population_constraint?, minimum_quality?, evidence_floor, on_unmet:ExactFallback|Fail }` is an execution-time demand and contains no chosen generation. `ApproximationProfile { approximation_kind, population_contract, algorithm_profile_oid, semantic_build_and_search_parameter_schema, quality_evidence_schema, deterministic_exact_fallback_oid? }` is definition-time policy and likewise contains no generation identity. Only after access paths and parameters are bound does execution realize `AnswerContract = Exact | Approximate { approximation_kind, population_contract, error_or_recall_contract, source_generation_identities[], source_generation_digests[], algorithm_profile_oid, candidate_and_search_profile, evidence_identity, deterministic_fallback_oid? }`. A contract never strongly references the generation that carries it; the surrounding bound plan, generation registry, result/certificate envelope, and replay closure own concrete roots explicitly, avoiding definition/generation identity cycles.

Every realized logical stream is typed with an `AnswerContract`; this is a semantic type, not a certificate footnote. Each operator registers a total transfer rule that produces a contract satisfying the `AnswerRequirement`, proves exact completion, invokes the pinned exact fallback, or rejects the composition. In particular, absence-sensitive, non-monotone, recursive, aggregate, distinct, and top-k operations cannot turn missing candidates into an exact result; they accept approximate inputs only under a registered sound calculus and otherwise fall back or fail. `Mutate` and every `Exact` requirement consume exact inputs only. All approximation-affecting generation topology, candidate/search depth, fusion, stopping, sampling, and evidence identities are pinned in the bound query/plan/certificate/replay closure and are never classified as result-neutral physical tuning. An approximate result remains byte-replayable under its pinned closure, but it is not thereby promoted to exact semantics.

### 8.4 Factorization as a type

Factorization is a recursive value algebra, not a one-parent flag: `FNode = Flat(ColumnVector) | RunSlice(RunSliceRef) | Values(ColumnVector) | Product(Vec<FNodeId>) | Union(Vec<FNodeId>) | Filter{input,mask} | PathDag(PathDagRef)`. `FactorizedBatch` carries roots, multiplicities, logical-order metadata, projection generation, and ownership/lifetime. Run slices are borrowed only while their generation is pinned; transport uses canonical logical values and never exposes physical run references.

Every operator registers a factorization law. Selection preserves shape only when expressible as masks; projection preserves it unless correlated dimensions merge; distributive/algebraic aggregates may push down under named laws; holistic or order-sensitive aggregates may require flattening; global `DISTINCT` retains multiplicity-aware state. Flattening is explicit, spillable, and differential-tested to produce identical rows, multiplicity, and logical order. No blanket claim says all aggregates or `DISTINCT` push through at an optimal cost.

### 8.5 Optimizer

- **Statistics** are versioned `StatsSegment`s with a branch/schema/policy watermark: label/type counts, authorized degree histograms, property sketches, zone maps, and sampled path correlations. Plans may use only statistics whose security domain cannot reveal hidden population information; otherwise they use capability-specific projections or conservative public bounds. Shrinkage, unseen-mass floors, robust estimators, influence caps, and targeted synopsis refresh are candidate estimators evaluated on named workloads. Their risk properties are conditional on their model/population and never promoted into semantic invariants.
- **Enumeration and access orders**: DP over connected subpatterns produces hybrid binary/FreeJoin plans. Every atom advertises available trie attribute orders and cursor complexity; forward/reverse Strata runs satisfy only their declared endpoint orders, while unavailable key/property orders require COLT construction or a Beacon view. Tier-D merge, tombstones, generation count, buffer I/O, and preprocessing enter physical cost. AGM/fractional-cover bounds are worst-case guardrails recorded separately; they are not compared numerically as if they were expected binary-plan costs.
- **Cost intervals are assumption-bearing evidence.** Split/Mondrian conformal intervals may report finite-sample marginal coverage within a frozen stratum only under the named exchangeability or online-validity conditions. Continuously selected, refreshed workload data does not inherit that guarantee automatically. Calibration artifacts record population, window, stratum construction, selection policy, alpha, and nonexchangeability penalty. Drift monitors can trigger fallback/recalibration; they do not retroactively preserve coverage. Plan choice uses intervals and tail-risk penalties as performance evidence (`FG-CAL-02`), while correctness remains independent of estimator quality.
- **Adaptivity uses registered safe points.** Every operator declares `ReplanClass = None | BeforeOutput | PartitionBoundary | StateTransfer`. A `ReplanCheckpoint` commits input frontier, emitted-output frontier, optional state object, and logical-order floor. Global joins, distinct, aggregates, sort, fixpoints, and paths may switch only at proved materialization/iteration barriers or through verified state transfer; a generic morsel boundary is insufficient. Strict hedges buffer output until a complete winner is selected by deterministic policy, or both candidates independently produce the same canonical stream—wall-clock race order never chooses logical output. Triggers and latency envelopes are assumption-bearing decision evidence, not correctness certificates.
- **Logical identity is orbit-canonical; physical identity is not.** Law-certified normalization assigns `LogicalOrbitId` modulo valid logical commutativity/associativity symmetries, allowing semantic proofs and logical cache entries to transfer. Every concrete access order, algorithm, materialization, index generation, and safe-replan policy has a distinct `PhysicalPlanId`, cost evidence, performance baseline, and replay record. A physical member never inherits another member's measurements merely because their outputs are equivalent.
- **Views**: Ripple-maintained materialized views register as rewrite targets (view matching over the algebra).

### 8.6 Determinism contracts (CGSE at query level)

Every query declares a tie-break policy; strict default is storage-independent `LexMin` over canonical scalar/element encodings. `ORDER BY` wins. `InsertionOrder` is defined as immutable element-origin order and is available only when elements carry durable `OriginBirthOrder { commit_seq, intent_ordinal, merge_ordinal, element_id }`; `BranchAdmissionOrder` is a separately named policy. Compaction/run order never defines either. `fnx-parity` is an explicit projection profile with its own adjacency-order metadata. Parallelism, spilling, factorization, and safe replanning must preserve the canonical logical row sequence or require `RELAXED` posture.

The replay artifact is a `ReplayManifest`, not `(certificate, seq, seed)`. It pins database/security namespace/incarnation, logical-command and commit sequences, topology/policy/revocation epochs, the execution `AnswerRequirement`, realized result `AnswerContract`, `LogicalOrbitId`/`PhysicalPlanId`, result-encoding version, and canonical result digest. Every evidence slot is an explicit `ReplayEvidenceRef<T> = Present { object: ReplayObjectRef<T> } | Unavailable { class, expected_logical_object_id?, expected_canonical_digest?, reason_code } | Redacted { class, commitment?, redaction_policy_oid }`. A present `ReplayObjectRef` is either a typed local strong edge or an explicitly nonlocal archive identity verified through the active registry; the latter is never a local GC edge. Slots cover logical state, role-bearing binding set, language/semantic/scalar/numeric profiles, bound and normalized query, typed parameters, replay-authority snapshot, UDF/module set, mediated external inputs/nondeterminism, exact derived generation snapshots, decision cards/policies, evidence, `ExecutableArtifactSet`, and a typed `StructuralReplayProjection`. No archivable edge relies on a bare `_root`, `_ref`, optional field, or digest whose default could silently become local reachability or conceal missing evidence.

Digests alone are not executable input. A versioned `ReplaySlotSchema` derives—not trusts—a manifest's immutable **structural completeness** from the slot states and the recorded execution's actual semantic dependencies. `Replayable` requires every semantically required slot to be `Present` and must identify the exact engine binary **or** a complete reproducible-build closure, Rust toolchain and target, CPU-feature contract, runtime/allocator configuration, VM/JIT/compiler artifacts if used, kernel/profile registry, and platform ABI. `StructuralReplay` requires a present typed `StructuralReplayProjection` plus every slot its declared reproduced control/data-shape classes require; every omitted class is explicitly unavailable or redacted. `VerifiableIfArtifactsSupplied` requires every missing slot to carry an expected identity/digest sufficient to validate later supplied bytes. `AuditOnly` permits explicit unavailable/redacted slots and makes no byte-replay claim. An encoded structural grade that disagrees with the generated slot matrix is invalid. At access time, the verifier separately derives `effective_completeness` by combining that maximum with live local/archive availability, lease, trust, resolver, and authorization state; it can only stay equal or become weaker without mutating the manifest. Reading or resolving any item requires explicit `Replay` authority scoped to the complete evidence/artifact closure. Replay compares canonical logical-result encoding before transport framing; fetch size, wire factorization, and frames are irrelevant.

FG-INV-19 guarantees byte replay only while the full required evidence closure and matching executable artifacts remain retained locally or through a verified `ReplayArchiveRecord` under a lease whose expiry and trust domain are visible in the manifest. Canonical `ReplayRegistry` is itself in `LogicalStatePayload` and strongly roots each active manifest plus the latest active archive record; archive records carry only nonlocal archive identities and weak closure commitments, never accidental local ObjectId edges. Renewal is an authenticated logical-command-sequenced extension. Revocation, expiry, resolver failure, or closure-verification failure causes the slot verifier to derive a weaker advertised grade and prevents a new `Replayable` claim. Expiry never deletes bytes directly: a consensus GC command first removes the relevant manifest/archive registry edge and then accounts for every other root. `KEEP NONE` does not preserve replay unless the query acquires a local or archive lease. Certificate modes `Compact`, `Budgeted`, `Full`, and `Forensic` may vary diagnostic detail, but a mode advertised as replayable may never truncate an identity field or referenced closure object. Strict floating results use §8.2's portable profile; RELAXED results are reproducible only within their recorded target/build scope.

### 8.7 Execution engine

- **Push-based, vectorized, morsel-parallel** execution uses partition-aligned tasks in the query region. Every kernel declares a maximum time/bytes/work between cancellation checkpoints; a morsel boundary is the outer bound, not permission for one unbounded decode or UDF call.
- **FreeJoin access** uses a `TrieCursor` contract advertising attribute order, `open/next/seek`, multiplicity, generation, and complexity. Forward/reverse Strata runs are tries only for declared endpoint orders; properties/keys and other orders require COLT or Beacon access paths. Hash/binary and radix-partitioned paths remain part of the same physical family. The executor accounts for all active runs, Tier-D overlays, tombstones, decoding, and I/O.
- **Path and recursion operators** implement the legality/algorithm matrix in §8.2 and the validated fixpoint subset in §9.3. Mode-aware history, captures, temporal intervals, and cost state are spillable. `RETURN PATH` may use a canonical shared-prefix/path DAG with multiplicity and order metadata; flattening remains reference-equivalent.
- **SIMD and memory-level parallelism** use separately ledgered unsafe-island crates with scalar fallbacks. STRICT kernels are fixed by the portable numeric/scalar profile and must be bit-identical across supported dispatch paths. AMAC/prefetch policy is physical-only only if result and logical order remain identical.
- **Larger-than-memory contracts are per operator.** Full `ORDER BY` uses external merge sort; joins, aggregate, and distinct use radix/grace partitioning; WCOJ/COLT build is partitioned; factorization/path frontiers spill as canonical DAG/state blocks; Ripple arrangements use an LSM-backed state store; Prism materialization streams or spills. Appendix C declares minimum resident set, stable merge order, I/O complexity, cleanup, and `ResourceExhausted` behavior for every operator.

### 8.8 Result delivery

Streaming begins only at an operator's declared safe emission point. Two-phase channel obligations provide bounded backpressure, while output-row/byte, egress, idle-time, and snapshot-age reservations prevent a slow client from holding unbounded resources. The canonical result digest covers schema, canonical values, multiplicity, and logical order before framing. Factorized frames encode a canonical logical factorization DAG—not Strata run references—and clients may request flat frames without changing result identity.

## 9. Ripple: Incremental Everything (Bet B4)

### 9.1 The unification claim

Four features that vendors often build separately—recursive evaluation, materialized views, subscriptions, and incremental analytics—share computation over **Z-sets** advanced by a total committed time. The source is not the unresolved intent log. Finalization persists a sequence-neutral `LogicalDeltaTemplate` whose canonical header binds `{ intent_semantics_oid, final_effect_digest, source_intent_digest }` and whose sorted coordinate entries bind `{ graph, branch, relation_id, validated_schema_binding, schema_transition?, delta_payload: ConditionalCoordinateRef<ZSet<CanonicalRow>> }`. It is exactly equal to the canonical before/after change, and its strong template reference plus digest are embedded in the capsule. A prepared-root traversal has no committed marker/cut context and therefore follows every conditional payload as strong; after commit, the marker supplies the retention coordinate. This preserves relation and per-coordinate before/after schema identity for atomic multi-graph commits without naming the future marker or defeating per-graph retention cuts.

Apply supplies the committed marker identity/sequence, verifies the template against the capsule, derives every resulting coordinate binding, and canonically materializes a self-contained `LogicalDeltaBatch { source_template_digest:WeakDigest, commit_marker_identity:WeakMarkerIdentity, commit_seq, coordinate_entries[{ source_binding, resulting_binding, relation_id, delta_payload_ref:StrongRef }], global_frontier, batch_digest }`. Its ObjectId is the `LogicalDeltaBatchId` and is a deterministic function of the verified template bytes, marker identity, resulting bindings, and copied strong payload identities; retries either reproduce identical bytes or fail closed. The source-template and marker fields prove provenance but are nonretaining, while each included payload is owned directly. The batch is derived, not a second source of truth: it can be rematerialized while marker+capsule+template remain in the authoritative suffix, or retained independently by derived/subscription/replay/backup leases without retaining that suffix. A verified checkpoint cut may reclaim the old capsule/template only after all delta-retention leases either move to these self-contained batch objects or receive an explicit `GAP`/new-baseline contract. Every circuit applies **all relevant coordinate entries or none**, records the batch ID/idempotency key, and publishes one state root/global batch frontier only after crash-atomic integration; recovery replays an incomplete batch. No-op intents emit no row delta; schema changes use separate versioned system relations.

### 9.2 The circuit engine

`fgdb-ripple` implements exact integer Z-set arithmetic: `ZWeight` uses checked `i128` as a fast representation and promotes before overflow to an in-house canonical signed-limb integer. Limb allocation/spill is resource-accounted; exhaustion aborts or lags the derived computation without publishing partial state. Thus the semantic domain is the integer additive group rather than a bounded partial imitation. The engine provides lift/differentiate/integrate/delay, registered derivatives for GLA operators, factorized delta forms with proved flattening equivalence, and spillable LSM-backed arrangements. Batch and incremental plans share typed algebra but have distinct physical optimization/resource contracts.

Logical change weights always form the integer additive group required for retractions. Optional annotations are separate products: Boolean annotations express existence; provenance-polynomial/DAG annotations express why-provenance; tropical values express costs. Each annotation family registers operator, deletion, differentiation, boundedness, and serialization laws. Replacing Z weights with Boolean or tropical values is forbidden because it would erase the additive inverses needed for general deletion.

### 9.3 Recursion = incremental fixpoint

`FixpointSpec` records recursive SCCs, monotonicity class, finite active-domain bound, duplicate rule, and termination contract; nested time is `(commit_seq, iteration)`. Monotone finite-domain SCCs use differentiated/semi-naïve evaluation. Negation and non-monotone aggregates must be stratified outside a recursive SCC unless a separately proved complete-lattice semantics exists. Every accepted recursion has a finite domain, convergence proof, or explicit visible budget; non-convergence returns a structured error rather than a partial fixpoint unless truncation was requested. Only registered derivatives are incrementally maintained—“for free” is not a substitute for an operator law.

### 9.4 Materialized graph views

`CREATE MATERIALIZED VIEW` produces either `BindingTable(schema)` or an explicitly declared `ConstructedGraph(graph_schema)`; arbitrary scalar/path/aggregate results are never mislabeled as virtual labels or edge types. A view generation is `DerivedGeneration { identity: DerivedIdentity, root_oid, global_batch_frontier, answer_contract: AnswerContract }` using §12.4's common identity; its coordinate-coverage set, schema roots/epochs, definition, circuit build profile, security/semantic epochs, authorization contract, and exact/approximate semantics therefore cannot drift independently. A definer view pins definer-authority digest plus a separately authorized result-exposure policy; an invoker view is maintained/read in an authority-partitioned domain or recomputed through that invoker's secure view. Branch fork/squash merge, authority change, and schema change have explicit rebuild, tail, or reset rules.

Constructed graphs use a rebuild-pure view-local identity namespace. Canonical `ViewElementKey` bytes commit definition OID, construction-clause ordinal, output kind, canonical source-binding identity, and deterministic multiplicity/occurrence ordinal. The durable/API identity is `ViewElementRef { key_digest:[u8;32], canonical_key_bytes }`; the digest is only an accelerator and equality/order always verify the complete bytes, so there is no collision-allocation choice or authoritative map to lose. Base and constructed identities are a tagged `ElementIdentity::Base(VId|EId) | Constructed(ViewElementRef)`. A generation may assign dense local ordinals, but those are discardable projections and never escape it. Full recompute and incremental maintenance therefore derive identical identities from definition and inputs alone. The definition fixes duplicate/parallel-edge keys, endpoint mapping, property-conflict aggregation, provenance, and deletion/multiplicity laws; the derivative-vs-batch oracle compares complete canonical keys and graph contents.

A query supplies target `GraphReferenceBinding[]` and selects `ViewReadPolicy = WaitForSnapshot | TailCompensate | AtWatermark | AllowStale`. Each generation carries sorted `DerivedSourceCoverage[]` inside `DerivedIdentity`, associating every input role/relation with a base binding and `Fixed|Advancing` tracking mode. It is direct only when every advancing source base equals its role's target coordinate, every fixed source still names its exact retained historical binding, and the global frontier agrees. Forward tailing is legal only when branch-lineage proofs show every advancing base is the exact ancestor of its target and contiguous crash-atomic batches cover every required source and the shared global frontier; fixed inputs never move. Roles may be coalesced only by a registered circuit equivalence law. A fork requires its registered fork overlay/base rule, while a squash merge appears as an ordinary target-branch delta. For a generation newer than any historical target, the reader uses a retained compatible generation, a proved exact inverse path with full support/history, or recomputes/fails; it never reads future state, and waiting cannot fix a historical query. `AtWatermark`/`AllowStale` return effective bindings/frontier. Every view declares deletion/multiplicity/inversion semantics, retained support, memory/spill bounds, and fallback.

Staleness envelopes may be computed from a max-plus maintenance model under named arrival/service assumptions and used as a configuration gate. CREATE rejects an SLO that the current model predicts cannot be met, but the returned artifact is a model-qualified estimate. Runtime monitors expose actual watermark lag and assumption violations; correctness relies on the explicit read policy, not the prediction.

### 9.5 Standing queries & changefeeds

Subscription creation atomically returns a baseline with one `DerivedIdentity`, its sorted source coverage, and one global batch frontier `w`, plus `START_AFTER(w)`. The transport `stream_id` is session-bound, but durable resumption uses a MACed `ResumeCapability { capability_format_version, issuer_id, mac_key_id, mac_key_epoch, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, tenant, principal, non_widenable_authority_digest, resume_cursor_id, derived_identity_digest, baseline_id, global_frontier, last_acked_delivery?, outstanding_delivery_lease_id?, policy_epoch, revocation_index, expiry, nonce }` that intentionally is not bound to one connection. All fields are in the domain-separated MAC. The stable cursor lets an older still-valid capability discover the one authority-matching outstanding lease after a disconnect; the optional lease ID is a checked hint, never an ownership edge. The capability may be replayed only idempotently, rotated on successful resume, never widened by `AUTH_REFRESH`, and becomes unusable after expiry, revocation/reset, incarnation change, acknowledged successor issuance, or baseline retirement. It is lookup authority, **not** a GC root; its canonical delivery lease is the retention authority.

Every emitted delta is a durable tagged `DeltaDeliveryEnvelope { database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, tenant/principal authority digest, derived_identity_digest, baseline_id, start_frontier, resulting_frontier, payload, delivery_digest }`. `payload = Atomic { batch_ref: StrongRef<LogicalDeltaBatch>, batch_digest, commit_seq }` names exactly one crash-atomic batch, or `Coalesced { payload_ref: StrongRef<CoalescedDeltaPayload> }`. The coalesced payload strongly owns its canonical net Z-set and binds `{ first_batch_id, last_batch_id, ordered_source_commitment, source_count, start_frontier, resulting_frontier, net_delta_digest }`; the source commitment includes every batch ID/digest, including zero-net intermediates. `DeliveryDigest = BLAKE3("fgdb:delivery-digest:v1" ‖ canonical envelope bytes excluding delivery_digest)` is the public cross-wire integrity value. `DeliveryId` is the full keyed `ObjectId` of the complete envelope and is intentionally distinct from that public digest.

Before the first frame, a `DeliveryLeaseTransitionSpec` is ordered and `DeliveryLeaseRegistryRoot` installs `DeliveryLeaseRecord { delivery_lease_id, resume_cursor_id, envelope_ref: StrongRef<DeltaDeliveryEnvelope>, baseline/derived/authority scope, state: Outstanding, issued_logical_command_seq, promised_through, verifier_key_epoch }`. The registry enforces at most one outstanding lease per cursor/frontier. The envelope strongly owns the atomic batch or coalesced payload. Delivery transitions are distinct typed control-command variants: resume/capability rotation consumes the old capability generation but preserves the same outstanding lease and envelope; complete ACK advances the cursor/frontier and removes the lease; reset or expiry may terminalize/remove it only after every advertised resume/delivery promise and in-flight obligation has ended. Checkpoints, backup/configuration floors, restore, and GC preserve the registry. Frames/chunks carry `{ delivery_id, delivery_digest, chunk_index, chunk_count }`; FGP sends the lease ID, envelope's batch or coalesced-payload ObjectId, and canonical metadata, so an authorized receiver can verify the public digest and content identity it was promised. ACK, deduplication, resume, and retention operate only on a fully received `(DeliveryId, DeliveryDigest)`, never an ambiguous singular batch field. `BUFFER n` is lossless only until capacity, then emits `GAP` and disconnects; `COALESCE` does not preserve intermediate events but preserves their committed range identity; `DISCONNECT` returns the rotated resume capability.

Authorization evolution has explicit relation semantics. `STICKY_AUTHORITY` fixes a non-widenable creation authority and terminates on revocation/expiry. `LIVE_AUTHORITY` ends the old segment at a policy boundary with `AUTHORITY_RESET { old_digest, new_digest, effective_seq }`, then emits a newly authorized snapshot plus `START_AFTER`; an optional retraction/insertion diff is used only when its disclosure is policy-approved. Ordinary data deltas never silently mix authority domains, and a cursor cannot resume across a reset without acknowledging the new baseline. Schema or branch changes likewise use a declared migration/reset contract.

MMR proofs authenticate commit membership and raw-log continuity as scoped in §5.8; they do not prove arbitrary MATCH-circuit completeness. Post-commit triggers provide **exactly-once durable scheduling records**. External effects are exactly once only with a transactional sink; otherwise the invocation carries a stable idempotency key and is at-least-once. The plan never derives external exactly-once effects from an internal obligation alone.

### 9.6 Incremental analytics maintainers

For algorithm families with known delta algorithms, Ripple hosts maintainers that keep results warm under updates: delta-PageRank (residual propagation), incremental connected components (union-find + bounded rebuild on deletes), incremental triangle/k-core counts, degree/label statistics (this is how §8.5's StatsSegments stay fresh). Each maintainer declares a **staleness contract** (exact | bounded-error(ε) | eventually-refreshed) surfaced in query certificates; recompute via Prism is the universal fallback. This directly answers the market's "derived state goes stale" complaint — including for the vector/FTS indexes, whose ingestion pipelines (§10) are Ripple consumers too.

For `bounded-error(ε)` maintainers, a conformal/risk controller may advise recompute under its named calibration population and exchangeability/nonexchangeability assumptions. The semantic contract is enforced by deterministic error bounds or exact validation where available; otherwise the result is explicitly approximate and carries the model-qualified error evidence. Assumption alarms trigger deterministic recompute/fallback but do not retroactively guarantee prior estimates.

## 10. Beacon: The Index Fabric

Every index generation exposes `IndexSnapshot { identity: DerivedIdentity, root_oid, answer_contract: AnswerContract }`; an index definition normally has one canonical advancing source role. A query separately supplies target `CoordinateSchemaBinding[]`. A root is direct only at exact binding/frontier equality; it may tail only when each advancing `DerivedSourceCoverage.base_binding.selected_head` is a proved ancestor on that exact branch and a contiguous delta path reaches every target under the shared frontier. Fork overlays and squash-merge deltas follow their explicit branch rules. A newer/nonancestor root is unusable except through a retained matching historical generation or separately proved inverse/recompute path. Definition/topology/authority/profile mismatch is not “close enough.” No path silently omits a tail. Authorization applies during statistics, candidates, traversal, scoring, and result construction—not as a final filter.

Canonical versioned `IndexDefinition` is not opaque DDL text. Common fields bind source graph predicate/bound expressions, branch policy, authority mode, semantic/scalar/collation profiles, null/duplicate/missing-value laws, a definition-time `ApproximationProfile?`, and update/deletion regime. Kind profiles bind: B-tree/hash key encoding/comparator; FTS Unicode/tokenizer/normalizer/stemmer/stopword/scorer artifact OIDs; vector dtype/dimension/metric/normalization plus HNSW/IVF/PQ and recall-population contract; adjacency order/projection; or path edge/label/cost projection and deletion semantics. Every parameter that can change membership, score, order, recall population, or stopping—including approximate topology seed/generation, candidate/search depth, quantizer, rerank count, and early-stop rule—is semantic and appears in `definition_oid`, `DerivedIdentity`, the `ApproximationProfile`, or the consuming realized `AnswerContract`; only proved answer-preserving layout/scheduling knobs may live in a physical build profile. Independent rebuilds may claim equivalence only when they reproduce the same exact contents or the same pinned approximate generation/answer contract; a rebuilt ANN topology is a new semantic generation even when configured from the same DDL.

Indexes expose `IndexAuthority = ConstraintCriticalSynchronous | DerivedRebuildable`. `ConstraintCriticalSynchronous` is the branch/head/schema-scoped canonical state owned by Chronicle/Txn's `fgdb-constraints`; Beacon may provide its access implementation but never asynchronous ownership or a second truth. Derived indexes may be discarded. If constraint state is missing/corrupt, affected writes block until a verified base-state rebuild; recovery never accepts a constraint-affecting write while enforcement is unavailable.

1. **Property B-trees and hash indexes** operate over stable constraint domains and label-membership projections. Unique/key/cardinality maps are `ConstraintCriticalSynchronous`; optimizer indexes and filters are `DerivedRebuildable`. Composite keys, canonical scalar encoding, range gaps for SSI, watermarks, and authority domains are part of the access contract.
2. **Adjacency views (A+-style)**: user-declared alternative sort orders / partitionings of an edge type's runs — e.g. `INDEX ON :RATED ORDER BY (dst, rating DESC)` — giving Graphflow-style tunable secondary adjacency for query shapes the primary run order can't serve. These are just extra run families; the whole Strata machinery is reused. The same mechanism hosts **dense projections**: bitmap or dense-tile adjacency views over selected (label, type, label) subgraphs — derived, Ripple-maintained, snapshot-cursored — feeding the semiring kernels (§8.7) and set-intersection fast paths without ever becoming a storage-tier citizen.
3. **Full-text**: segment-based inverted index, in-house tokenization (Unicode word-boundary + language-pluggable stemmers), BM25 with positions/phrase/fuzzy(edit≤2 via Levenshtein automata over the term ART); frankensqlite's FTS5 module is the in-family behavioral reference.
4. **Vector ANN with historical topology.** HNSW uses mutable delta plus immutable generation roots, but each query pins one `IndexSnapshot`. Nodes newer than the requested snapshot cannot participate as routing bridges; deleted or capability-hidden nodes cannot influence a STRICT authorized traversal unless a formally validated filter-aware topology says otherwise. Rebuild creates a new root and never changes replay pinned to a retained old root. A coverage base older than a target is usable only with the exact per-coordinate ancestry and contiguous-tail proof above; otherwise the query waits or selects an exact scan—never output-filters an incompatible topology into a false claim of snapshot completeness. Exact reranking is exact only within the candidate set.

   SQ8/PQ/IVF-PQ are physical codecs with versioned reconstruction-error evidence. A JL projection may carry a probabilistic multiplicative bound with dataset size, dimension, and failure probability; PQ generally carries residual/additive or empirical distance-error bounds, not a universal `(1±ε)` guarantee. Recall sampling is keyed and names the query population, exact baseline, snapshot/authority domain, sample design, and alpha; e-processes are health evidence and rebuild triggers, not proof of per-query recall. STRICT queries may request an exact fallback.
5. **Path/reachability indexes (adaptive)**: 2-hop/pruned-landmark labeling is built *on demand* for an explicit graph/authority projection when repeated reachability or shortest-path load justifies it, then maintained by Ripple or rebuilt/dropped under its declared update regime. Landmark and portfolio selection may use lazy greedy. A `(1−1/e)` badge is legal only when the registered measured-benefit objective is proved nonnegative, monotone, and submodular under the actual cardinality budget; other byte, dependency, or matroid constraints use the theorem matching that constraint or label the choice `HEURISTIC`. Every decision still publishes objective samples, marginal-gain trace, feasibility checks, uncertainty, deterministic fallback, and any valid upper bound—never a generic “optimality certificate.”
6. **Hybrid retrieval operator.** `HybridSearchSpec` pins target `GraphReferenceBinding[]`, an explicit input-role→`IndexSnapshot { identity:DerivedIdentity, root_oid, answer_contract }` mapping, every snapshot's coordinate coverage/global frontier, text/vector candidate depths, expansion budget/path semantics, fusion build/profile ObjectIds, duplicate aggregation, tie policy, authority domain, output `AnswerRequirement`, and exact fallback. RRF fixes positive integer `k0`, one-based rank, missing-modality zero, and canonical per-source ties; STRICT comparison uses exact rational cross-products with overflow promotion to canonical big integers, while exposed Decimal128 scores use the profile's fixed scale/rounding. Weighted fusion names exact Decimal128 weights and versioned normalizers. Graph expansion defines duplicate/path-score aggregation and prunes only with admissible upper bounds. BM25 statistics, ANN traversal, seed resolution, and expansion use the authorized bindings; hidden elements cannot influence scores or routing. The GLA operator composes every input contract, tail state, fusion stage, and expansion stage; it emits `Exact` only after complete exact sources/fallback and otherwise emits a fully populated `Approximate` contract satisfying that requirement.
7. **Temporal connectivity index:** rollback/persistent union-find and offline segment-tree-over-time generations accelerate declared graph projections. Each generation pins branch, edge-type/label projection, authority domain, sequence range, and deletion semantics. `sealed-exact` roots may answer only covered snapshots; an uncovered tail or branch overlay is compensated completely or falls back to BFS at the requested snapshot. Online approximate roots are never selected for an exact `CONNECTED` contract.

## 11. Prism: In-Database Analytics via franken_networkx

### 11.1 Surface

`CALL fnx.<algorithm>(...) [YIELD …]` exposes only registered, semantically compatible fnx signatures. Versioned `FnxSignatureRegistry` describes per algorithm: zero/one/multiple graph inputs; typed parameters/defaults/coercions; output schema; attribute roles (weight, capacity, demand, partition, label, etc.); callback/matcher module type/effects; RNG policy/seed; directed/multigraph/self-loop laws; numeric/determinism profile; complexity witness; and larger-than-memory implementation class. Each graph argument becomes an `AuthorizedProjection { graph_reference_binding, logical_state_root_oid, authority_digest, directedness, labels/types, parallel-edge law, attribute bindings with missing/coercion laws, node order }`. Two-graph algorithms bind two independently authorized role/head/schema projections; generators bind no hidden graph; callbacks are pinned deterministic module OIDs or explicitly non-replayable native effects. Opaque `params` cannot smuggle an unscoped graph, attribute, callback, or entropy source. Only complete signature rows are callable, and all input projections/implementation paths ride in the certificate.

### 11.2 The bridge and its honest copy boundary

The current fnx trait borrows `&[usize]`; Strata's compressed `u32/u64` rows cannot safely implement that contract zero-copy. W8 therefore upstreams a borrowed neighbor-cursor/GAT interface with an associated node-index type. Algorithms generic over that interface traverse decoded/merged authorized cursors without whole-graph materialization. Until then, compatibility uses explicit epoch-shared `Vec<usize>` caches and is labeled `DECODED_CACHE`, not zero-copy. Typed directed multigraphs are reduced only through an `AuthorizedProjection` bound by the registered `FnxCallSpec`; parallel-edge aggregation is never implicit.

### 11.3 Determinism & witnesses flow through

CGSE policy semantics and the `ComplexityWitness` schema are preserved, but each in-database witness binds the authorized projection digest, projection-specific `n/m`, adapter/materialization path, numeric profile, and fnx implementation hash. Differential tests require the same projected input and policy to match standalone fnx; they do not compare a raw graph with a capability-filtered projection or pretend adapter work is free. The resulting ledger is folded into the query's plan certificate.

### 11.4 Coverage strategy

Algorithms not cursor-generic take a `MATERIALIZED` path that streams/spills an authorized projection into a generation-scoped representation. An algorithm is exposed as a database operator only after it has either a bounded cursor path or a declared external-memory/spill implementation with cleanup and I/O bounds; otherwise it remains in the source catalog rather than violating the larger-than-memory product contract. `ResourceExhausted` remains legal when even the registered minimum resident set or scratch quota is unavailable, not as a substitute for designing the operator. W8 extends coverage by family and adds native kernels only after differential semantics tests. Residual artifacts record norm, numeric profile, iteration trace, stopping rule, and the mathematical assumptions behind any convergence bound; deterministic iteration count alone does not create cross-machine floating identity.

### 11.5 Scheduling

Analytics run in dedicated regions on a bulkhead-isolated lane (combinator: `bulkhead`) so a Louvain over 10⁹ edges cannot starve p99 OLTP; budgets + cancellation apply; long runs checkpoint progress as obligations (a cancelled community detection cleans up its scratch deterministically).

## 12. Warden: Security & Governance

### 12.1 Threat and authority model

The trust matrix distinguishes untrusted clients, mutually hostile tenants, honest-but-curious storage, crash-fault replicas, malicious/stale donors, independent transparency witnesses, trusted embedded host code, and a compromised operator/server. The baseline trusts the executing database process and active key boundary to enforce plaintext confidentiality and policy; a compromised server process can exfiltrate data it can decrypt, and this plan claims no TEE/Byzantine-confidentiality magic. Witnessed transparency/audit may detect only their scoped history/administrative attacks, not undo disclosure. Ordinary Raft tolerates crash faults, not Byzantine replicas. Every deployment profile declares actors, protected assets, assumptions, and out-of-scope failures before selecting a claim.

Stable identities include `TenantId`, `PrincipalId`, `IssuerId`, `TokenId`, `DatabaseId`, `PolicyEpoch`, `RevocationEpoch`, and `KeyEpoch`. `EffectiveAuthority` binds tenant/subject/issuer/audience, database security namespace and cluster incarnation, token/policy epochs, operation classes, graph/branch/label/edge/property/system-field/temporal scopes, procedure/UDF/module ObjectIds and invocation mode, external-I/O classes, Replay evidence/resolver closure, redaction profile, and resource ceiling. Operation classes are `Read, Mutate, Ddl, Subscribe, Analytics, Replay, ExecuteProcedure, InstallModule, ExternalIo, Export, Backup, Restore, Observe, Admin, KeyManage, Replicate`.

### 12.2 Token lifecycle and restricted policy IR

Macaroons follow `Issued -> Active -> Attenuated -> Revoked|Expired`, binding issuer, audience, database security namespace, tenant, subject, token ID, key/revocation/policy epochs, not-before/expiry, cluster incarnation, and optional channel binding. Revocation and evaluator epochs are Chronicle-sequenced. An `AuthorizationLease` is a short-lived, authority-leader-signed capability binding token ID, authority digest, non-widenable ceiling, database namespace/incarnation, tenant/principal, policy semantics/epoch, applied revocation-log index, Raft term/security-leader epoch, expiry, and permitted read snapshot range. Long queries and streams reauthorize before the earlier of lease expiry or a locally observed security-epoch boundary; buffered-but-unsent results are discarded on failure. The maximum lease TTL is the deployment's explicit revocation-enforcement bound, not hidden “eventual” behavior.

Graph caveats compile to a versioned, deterministic, total, resource-bounded `PolicyIr`, not arbitrary GQL. It may express bounded label/type/property/system-field masks, scalar predicates, branch/time bounds, operation/resource limits, and finite allow-lists of procedure/UDF/module ObjectIds and invocation modes. Policy evaluation never invokes those modules; it excludes arbitrary recursion, procedure calls, nondeterministic functions, and unbounded path evaluation. A policy-version ObjectId identifies semantics permanently. Old evaluators remain available for retained tokens or issuance is explicitly migrated/revoked—CI cannot enumerate hypothetical offline macaroons and pretend to prove semantic compatibility.

### 12.3 Enforcement is a mandatory access type

Only `fgdb-secure-view` can construct `AuthorizedSnapshot`, `AuthorizedCursor`, `AuthorizedGraphView`, `AuthorizedMutation`, `AuthorizedMutationPermit`, and `AuthorizedDerivedState`; W1's verifier is the sole permit constructor behind that facade. Fabric, Loom, Ripple, Beacon, Prism, UDFs, backup/export, observability, and constraint reporting cannot obtain raw Strata cursors or unscoped commit submission. Planner pushdown is an optimization atop this boundary, not the authorization root. The coordinator independently verifies the permit and final-effect authorization as §5.2 requires, so a forged internal call cannot bypass policy merely by reaching a lower crate.

Authorization occurs before retrieval/statistics/ANN traversal/expansion and both before and after mutation. Hidden-state constraints require an explicit `ConstraintDisclosureContract`: `CompleteDomainAuthority` (the mutator may observe the entire constrained domain), `AuthorityPartitioned` (integrity is defined independently inside a tenant/authority partition), or `ExplicitConflictDeclassification` (a narrowly scoped capability deliberately releases one padded conflict bit, with audit ticket, rate limit, and no conflicting identity/value). A blind writer without one of these contracts is rejected before probing; error redaction cannot make global uniqueness and zero-disclosure coexist. The remaining contract covers hidden-edge cascades, property/provenance/embedding masks, invoker-versus-definer view rights, subscription reauthorization, capability-safe conflicts and certificates, branch merge, system graph, backup/restore, and descriptor masking so hidden edges cannot affect visible degree. Every API and operator declares permitted declassifications and has a noninterference row plus cross-capability differential tests.

### 12.4 Derived state, caches, and errors

One canonical type keys every persisted or reusable derived object:

`DerivedIdentity { database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, kind, definition_oid, source_coverage: sorted Vec<DerivedSourceCoverage { input_role_id, source_relation_id, base_binding: CoordinateSchemaBinding, tracking_mode: Fixed|Advancing, lineage_digest }>, global_batch_frontier?, authority_digest, policy_semantics_oid, policy_epoch, revocation_index, topology_epoch, language_contract_oid?, semantic_profile_oid, scalar_profile_oid, collation_profile_oid?, redaction_profile_oid, build_profile_oid }`.

Fields irrelevant to a kind are explicit canonical `None`, never ambient defaults. View, index, statistics, subscription, plan/result cache, `DerivedGenerationRegistry`, certificate, and replay schemas embed this exact identity or its digest plus a strong reference to the identity object. A lookup must match all fields before applying an explicitly registered tail/rebuild rule; no singular graph/head/schema key may stand in for a coordinate set. Derived objects carry an `AuthorityReuseLaw`, not a bare subset slogan. Row-complete raw caches may derive a narrower domain only through an authorization-preserving projection that also masks metadata. Aggregates, cardinalities, sketches, BM25 statistics, DP results, and constraint outcomes require exact-domain state or a proved sufficient decomposition; HNSW/graph-routing topology requires exact authorized topology or a separately proved filter-aware construction. Definer views pin definer authority plus result-exposure policy; invoker state is authority-partitioned. Revocation or incarnation advance removes reachability even if bytes remain cached. Hardened errors use bounded capability-safe classes; plans, abort reports, witnesses, timing padding, and telemetry are redacted before crossing the boundary.

### 12.5 Encryption and key lifecycle

Logical, ciphertext, FEC-encoding, and placement identities are defined separately in §5.1. One random object DEK encrypts one `CiphertextId`; that ciphertext may have several `EncodingId` FEC realizations. Sharing adds recipient/database/branch/replica/backup wraps rather than re-encrypting the object. `CryptoCx` obtains production entropy independently of deterministic replay seeds, and secret entropy is never exported in incident bundles. Data-key states are `Generated -> Active -> Retiring -> RetainedDecryptOnly -> DestroyPending -> Destroyed`; token/signing revocation is a separate state machine. `RetainedDecryptOnly` forbids new encryption/wrap grants but still opens every reachable object. Destruction is deliberately two-stage and idempotent: a `KeyDestroyProposal` control command proves the typed zero-reference/configuration/backup/legal-hold floor and moves the key to `DestroyPending`; an external KMS/HSM operation returns an authenticated destruction receipt; a later `KeyDestroyFinalizeSpec` command verifies the proposal, receipt, unchanged-or-stronger floor, and current configuration before creating the applied `KeyDestroyRecord` and `Destroyed` state. Neither pre-order spec names its future result. Merely marking a key revoked, timing out, or seeing an external receipt without the finalize command never bypasses FG-INV-14. The spec includes algorithm IDs/agility, nonce/SIV mode, authenticated-data digest, salt and Argon2 parameters for passphrase mode, KMS/HSM mode, rotation, zeroization, replica provisioning, backup escrow, signing-key rotation, and crypto-erasure limits.

Cryptographic primitives require published vectors, constant-time/side-channel testing, misuse tests, independent expert review, and a release-blocking external audit. Where an audited foundation primitive exists, it is preferred. “Built in-house” never means unaudited cryptography is accepted merely because functional vectors pass.

### 12.6 Leakage and redaction

Timing/cardinality/error leakage is acknowledged. Padding, quantization, pools, and bounded errors are mitigations. Any mutual-information or channel-capacity result is a deployment-specific `StatisticalClaim` naming secret distribution, attacker observations, concurrency assumptions, padding schedule, sampling design, confidence bound, and identity-level cumulative budget; adversarial CI fixtures do not prove universal noninterference or a global bits/query ceiling.

### 12.7 Differential privacy

DP authority binds `PrivacyPrincipalId`, an explicit vertex- or edge-neighboring relation, contribution bounds, mechanism/query fingerprint, and an identity-level ledger spanning tokens and aliases. Each population/accounting trust domain has a stable `PrivacyAccountingDomainId` that normally survives cluster incarnations, same-identity restores, branches, and backups. Canonical `DpRequestKey` binds that domain, principal/authority, database security namespace, request nonce/idempotency identity, logical root and exact `GraphReferenceBinding[]`, normalized bound query and parameters, neighboring relation, clipping/contribution bounds, mechanism/version/parameters, policy/revocation epochs, and output encoding; cluster incarnation is recorded as provenance but does not create a fresh accounting domain or erase an existing request tombstone.

Privacy accounting has an external monotonic authority analogous to ID continuity but specialized to irreversible composition. Before any sensitive data access, a threshold-authenticated CAS registry atomically advances `PrivacyContinuityRecord { privacy_accounting_domain_id, privacy_epoch, cumulative_renyi_state_commitment, request_tombstone_root, predecessor_digest, external_registry_id, cas_version, status, issued_at, signer_set_epoch, threshold_signatures }`, records the exact request key and debit, and returns an `ExternalPrivacyChargeReceipt`. The debit cannot be rolled back or refunded. Chronicle then orders a typed DP charge command containing that receipt and mirrors the exact external head in `PrivacyLedgerState`/`LogicalStateRoot`; only after both the external CAS and local/consensus charge transition are durable may execution observe data, data-dependent timing, or a query-specific error. A crash after external CAS but before local mirroring leaves a charged orphan that recovery must import/reconcile—it never restores the budget. The database disables DP and fails closed whenever the external head is unavailable, behind, forked, or inconsistent.

The uniqueness-serialized durable machine is `Prepared{no_sensitive_access,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq} -> Charged{budget_debit,external_charge_receipt_ref,encrypted_seed_ref,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq} -> Computing{same_charge_and_seed,attempt_generation,no_output=true,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq} -> ResultCommitted{same_charge_and_seed,encrypted_result_ref,result_digest,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq} -> Delivered{same_charge_seed_result_refs,result_digest,delivery_receipt_digest,delivery_channel_class,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq}`; only `Prepared` may transition to `Abandoned{stable_reason,applied_command_ref,applied_logical_command_seq}`. Each input `DpTransitionSpec` is sequence-neutral and each result names the already identified `ControlCommand`, never its future `LogicalCommandRecord`. Every transition advances logical-command time. Cancellation/crash after charge consumes the budget and recovery/retry resumes with the same protected seed; if retained seed/result state cannot be reconciled after restore, the old request fails `DpStateUnavailable` and cannot draw new noise under that key. Result, seed, receipts, and necessary key wraps are strong through the privacy/retry floor; no output or data-dependent completion signal precedes `ResultCommitted`. Concurrent retries wait on the same key or return its committed result. No independent hedge, stale-follower budget, or duplicate execution may draw another sample.

A same-identity restore must reconcile to the latest external privacy head and import every post-backup debit/tombstone before DP is enabled; advancing `cluster_incarnation` is not sufficient. A clone gets a new privacy domain only when an approved policy proves the protected populations and identities are disjoint; otherwise it remains in the source domain and inherits its cumulative state. Rényi composition, reservation recovery/retention, collusion boundary, branch/snapshot/clone composition, and allowed aggregate catalog are normative. Motifs or arbitrary queries remain unavailable until sensitivity and clipping are proved. Budget refusal is structured and never silently changes epsilon or mechanism.

### 12.8 Audit

Compliance audit is a separately retained, signed append-only chain—not “whatever commit history remains.” Signatures, sequence continuity, and anchoring prove **append integrity and post-ticket gap/truncation detection**, not that a buggy or compromised ingress could never omit an event before logging. Each same-identity lineage has a stable `AuditDomainId`. An external monotonic authority CAS-publishes `AuditContinuityRecord { database_security_namespace_id, audit_domain_id, audit_epoch, highest_ticket_sequence, ticket_index_commitment, unresolved_ticket_commitment, audit_chain_head_digest, predecessor_digest, external_registry_id, cas_version, status, signer_set_epoch, threshold_signatures }`, while retaining authenticated ticket/resolution bytes or authenticated resolver references—not commitments alone.

An `AuditCompletenessProfile` names which operations require an externally reserved `AuditTicket` before protected processing. Ticket issuance first CAS-adds a pending ticket externally, then orders its local mirror. Resolution first reaches one locally ordered terminal state. For a state-changing operation, that apply may construct an **unpublished** candidate root, but the visible root/closed-read prefix is fenced before the protected command. While this single global prefix fence is active, the coordinator admits only its audit mirror/recovery commands and Raft maintenance; unrelated mutations cannot accumulate behind it. The sequencer then CAS-resolves the ticket, a following mirror command orders the returned continuity head, and only the final root containing both effect and mirrored resolution is published. No leader/follower snapshot, result frame, changefeed, observable system-graph/metric detail, or acknowledgement may cross the fence earlier. Read-only protected work similarly buffers output until terminal CAS plus local mirror. Recovery completes any interrupted idempotent transition before lifting the fence. Ticket and resolution signatures bind database security namespace, `AuditDomainId`, tenant/principal scope, event class/request, cluster-incarnation provenance, sequence, profile, and terminal content. `LogicalStatePayload`, checkpoints, configuration/GC/key floors, and backups strongly retain the local continuity head and authenticated external locator. Same-identity restore reconciles the complete external ticket/resolution history before ticket-gated operations resume. If the external registry or resolvers cannot supply required evidence, those operations stay disabled and a signed permanent completeness-gap/downgrade record is published; the database never silently advertises complete audit coverage. Only events admitted through this gate receive a source-completeness claim within the trusted-process threat model. Unticketed/sampled read telemetry is labeled non-complete. The chain records successful and denied authentication/authorization, queries at the configured detail, mutations, DDL, token/key/policy changes, backup/export/restore, and replication administration, with explicit redaction and access rules.

## 13. Fabric: Server, Protocols, and the Embedded API

1. **Embedded**: `fgdb::Database::open(path | :memory:)` → authorized sessions → prepared statements → streaming results; Rust-native typed accessors and capability-gated procedures. `fgdb-python` consumes the allowed `fnx-python` binding boundary rather than adding PyO3 directly to engine crates; ABI3/NumPy support remains isolated from durable/runtime layers. A claimed NumPy view is zero-copy only for matching contiguous authorized buffers and otherwise returns an explicit copy.
2. **`fgdbd` server**: multi-database, multi-tenant by capability; connection admission via semaphore + rate-limit combinators; graceful drain on shutdown (region close = no dropped in-flight commits, structurally).
3. **FGP (native wire protocol)** has one connection machine only: `TransportEstablished -> VersionNegotiated -> Authenticated -> Ready -> Draining -> Closed`. While `Ready`, it owns orthogonal typed children: at most one transaction child `Idle -> Begun -> CommitSubmitted -> Committed|Aborted|Unknown -> Idle`; zero or more query children `Accepted -> Bound -> Executing -> StreamingResults -> Completed|Cancelled|Failed`; and zero or more subscription/export children `Opening -> Baseline -> Delta -> Resetting|Ended`. The negotiated profile states whether independent autocommit queries may coexist with an open transaction; transaction-bound statements are serialized by default, and any concurrent profile must define savepoint/error and result-order laws. `Draining` rejects new children and lets existing children reach explicit terminal states. Every request/query/stream has an ID. Frames cover begin/commit/rollback, prepare/execute, schema/results, snapshot+subscription bootstrap, ACK/resume/gap, flow control, auth refresh, query versus commit cancellation, commit-status lookup, and retry/fatal classification. Once commit publication begins, cancel may return `Unknown(txn_outcome_capability)` but cannot promise abort.

   Ephemeral `stmt_id`, `stream_id`, open-query, active-transaction, and transient-reservation handles are server-minted random or MACed capabilities scoped to protocol version, database security namespace/incarnation, tenant, principal, authority ceiling/digest, **session**, and creation policy/revocation epochs. Prepared statements bind the `PreparedQueryTemplate` digest, language/catalog/schema compatibility contracts, and non-widenable authority ceiling; each execution resolves a fresh concrete `BoundQuery` and reauthorizes it. Two durable capabilities deliberately survive connection loss: §9.5's `ResumeCapability`, and a `TxnOutcomeCapability { capability_format_version, issuer_id, mac_key_id, mac_key_epoch, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, tenant, principal, non_widenable_authority_digest, txn_token, issuance_policy_epoch, issuance_revocation_index, expiry, nonce, mac }`. Every field is covered by a domain-separated MAC. The active transaction remains session-bound, but that outcome capability permits idempotent retry/status resolution from a newly authenticated session under the same-or-narrower live authority; it cannot mutate an open transaction, is rate limited, rotates only under a recorded rule, and expires only after the outcome-retention floor makes the promised lookup impossible. Capability encodings are never reinterpreted across versions; verifier-key records and old format decoders remain strongly retained through the maximum live capability expiry/outcome-retention floor, after which a stream-sequenced rotation record may retire them. `AUTH_REFRESH` affects only new work: it cannot widen any handle/capability or an open transaction's authority ceiling; narrowing/revocation fences unsent output, and commit still performs current revocation/final-effect validation. `COMMIT_STATUS` accepts only the outcome capability (or an explicit admin capability) and returns one capability-safe `NotVisibleOrUnknown` class rather than revealing whether another principal's token exists.

   `FrameLimits` bounds encoded and uncompressed bytes, compression ratio, nesting, columns, parameters, FEC symbols, streams, in-flight bytes, result rows/bytes, and idle time. The decoder validates declared sizes and reserves resources before allocating or FEC decoding. Authentication follows protected transport and transcript-bound negotiation. Compression is disabled for mixed attacker-controlled/secret frames unless a reviewed profile permits it. Factorized frames carry canonical authorized values, never run references. Session types enforce order; runtime validators enforce values, epochs, authorization, and resource bounds. Appendix D is normative, not a sketch.
4. **HTTP/2 + gRPC + WebSocket** (asupersync http): query endpoint, subscription streams, health/metrics, bulk import/export endpoints (multipart or ATP bonded pull).
5. **Bolt-compat subset** (`fgdb-bolt`): enough of Bolt v5 + Neo4j type mapping to let standard Neo4j drivers and visualization tools connect for read/query workloads — the adoption wedge. Documented divergence table; not the native path.
6. **QoS and admission** separates ephemeral runtime reservations from durable quota state. A hierarchical `ResourceVector { cpu_ns, resident_bytes, temp_bytes, durable_bytes, read_iops, write_iops, egress_bytes, output_bytes, output_rows, snapshot_age, branch/index/view/subscription counts, subscription_backlog }` is reserved before transient work and released by obligation. Durable bytes/branches/indexes/views/subscriptions/history are enforced by an authoritative Chronicle-sequenced `ResourceLedgerState`; every create/grow/delete/expiry carries `ResourceReservationId` plus canonical `ResourceChargeEffect`/release in the same final capsule as the durable object change (§5.2). Concurrent sessions therefore serialize quota validation with publication, and crash recovery replays charge and object atomically. Maintenance has protected emergency reserves. Hedging is allowed only within ephemeral reservations and never chooses strict logical order by wall-clock race; DP work forbids independent hedges.
7. **Formats and migration** (`fgdb-formats`) import the declared fnx/CSV/JSONL/Parquet-lite profiles under explicit size, recursion, schema, and decompression limits. A `.fgdb` archive is a signed `BackupManifest` plus exact root/head, object inventory, base dependencies, key wraps, format/profile epochs, and transparency checkpoint. Decode proofs alone do not establish backup completeness or semantic validity (§16).
8. **Deterministic extensibility:** replicated/apply/merge logic is limited to validated Datalog or the fuel/memory/stack-bounded deterministic bytecode VM with mediated authority and pinned arithmetic. Native Rust procedures remain embedded-host extensions and are categorically barred from replicated logical effects, merge/apply, constraints, and replay-guaranteed queries; a conformance test cannot prove arbitrary native code deterministic. Native read procedures receive authorized views and are labeled external nondeterminism unless their outputs are excluded from strict replay.
9. **CDC export** sends canonical committed logical deltas with stable idempotency tokens and at-least-once delivery. Raw global feeds may attach contiguous-range proofs; filtered feeds label membership versus completeness exactly as §5.8 requires. External sinks provide their own transactional/idempotent boundary.

## 14. Aegis: Replication, HA, and the Distribution Doctrine

**Doctrine:** single-node correctness is the base product; replication adds crash-fault durability, availability, and read scale. Canonical logical effects/digests agree across replicas, but repair ESIs, ciphertext encodings, cache state, and physical placement need not be byte-identical. Horizontal sharding is a post-1.0 protocol workstream, not a configuration toggle.

1. **One commit state machine with durable Raft state.** §5.2 remains authoritative in one-member and multi-member configurations. Transactions advance through `Draft -> Validated -> FinalPrepared -> PreparedRootDurable -> PayloadAvailable -> Proposed(term,index) -> CommandCommitted -> Applied(logical_command_ref,marker_ref) -> Acknowledged`. Consensus commits the normative strongly referenced `CommitCommand`; the standalone `CommitMarker`, `LogicalStatePayload`, `LogicalCommandRecord`, and outer state root are deterministically created in that acyclic order after `LogicalCommandSeq`/`CommitSeq`/HLC assignment. Before proposal, each donor has root-published its `PreparedCommitRecord`, and the leader collects signed/MACed `PayloadReceipt`s binding stable `PreparedOwnershipId`, base-closure digest, and exact `EncodingId`/`PlacementId` coverage in named failure domains. The canonical `PayloadAvailabilityCertificate` proves the configured recoverability predicate—including quorum one—and is a strong command edge.

   Raft persistence itself obeys the no-extra-mutable-file doctrine. Immutable `RaftLogSegment`, `RaftHardState { current_term, voted_for, log_tail, commit_index, applied_index, visible_index, config_state, snapshot_ref }`, configuration, receipt/certificate, and `RaftSnapshot` objects are rooted by replica-local `RaftStateRoot`, which the local `RootManifest` publishes through the dual-slot protocol. A node persists and syncs the new hard-state/log closure **before** granting a vote, acknowledging `AppendEntries`, reporting a payload receipt, or advancing commit durability. Recovery loads term/vote/log/config, reconciles log ownership with `PreparedPayloadRoot`, validates the exact command/payload closure, and idempotently applies committed-but-unapplied commands. A snapshot/truncation cut may advance only through `visible_index`; it cannot cross an active audit fence. Otherwise log truncation waits for a durable snapshot and a complete `ConfigPayloadFloor`; its typed inventory includes every `LogicalStatePayload` class, prepared/ordered ownership, delivery leases, replay/archive, external identity/privacy/audit continuity, backups/legal holds, keys and current/prospective configuration state. Local Raft-root publication may advance `RootSlot` while leaving canonical state unchanged. Leader loss yields scoped `Unknown` resolution through `TxnOutcomeCapability`, not guessed success/failure.
2. **Membership and payload authority.** SWIM+Lifeguard supplies suspicion, routing hints, donor choice, and operator evidence only; it cannot add/evict Raft voters or storage-durability members. A `ConfigPayloadFloor` is always based on an already identified older `LogicalStateRoot`, never the resulting root. It strongly references the current, prospective-joint, and prospective-final configuration objects and records basis logical-command/commit/Raft positions; failure-domain policy; recoverable checkpoint and suffix cuts; closed-through logical-command and Raft endpoints; typed mandatory inventory; prepared-root generations/stable ownership IDs; and per-domain receipts/acknowledgements. If the authoritative prefix advances during transfer, the floor is extended or recomputed; a stale floor cannot authorize retirement.

   Reconfiguration is `ProposeJoint -> CatchUpLog -> TransferMandatoryClosure -> VerifyFloorAtBasis -> CommitJoint -> CommitNew -> RefreshFloorThroughCommitNew -> CommitRetirementFloor -> RetireOld`. `CommitJoint`, `CommitNew`, and `CommitRetirementFloor` are paired Raft configuration entries and typed `ConfigurationControlCommand`s, so each semantic configuration transition increments `LogicalCommandSeq`; heartbeats/no-ops do not. The final retirement floor proves that the prospective durability set can recover through the committed `CommitNew` transition and every mandatory retained suffix/object and protected retry/accounting/idempotency closure under the new failure-domain policy. Old members cannot leave, discard payload, or advance GC until that final floor command is committed and acknowledged. Statistical suspicion may tune alerts but never authorizes membership, payload retirement, or GC.
3. **Read contracts and revocation/audit fences.** `LeaderRead` and `ReadIndexRead` provide linearizable data paths. `FollowerLeaseRead { term, lease_epoch, closed_logical_command_seq, closed_commit_seq, audit_visible_logical_command_seq, applied_logical_command_seq, applied_commit_seq, expiry, authorization_lease }` is legal only when both canonical prefixes are fenced at or below the audit-visible publication prefix, the requested snapshot/control state is closed and applied, all referenced objects/keys are retained, and a retention lease is acquired. Independently, the follower must validate §12.2's signed `AuthorizationLease`, be in its permitted snapshot range, and have applied through its policy epoch and revocation-log index. Otherwise it contacts the authority leader or fails closed; data staleness never grants security staleness, and unpublished ticket-gated effects never leak through a follower. Open streams discard unsent buffered frames and reauthorize within the configured maximum lease TTL. `bounded_staleness` returns effective transaction sequence/lag, logical-command/audit-visible prefixes, and security index/lease expiry. Historical reads retain the same applied, retention, key, branch, audit, and authority predicates. Read-your-writes capabilities bind database security namespace/incarnation, tenant/principal and non-widenable authority, resulting coordinate heads, policy/revocation indices, Raft term, logical-command/commit sequences, audit-visibility floor, expiry, format/key epoch, and MAC.
4. **Writer-anywhere preparation.** Followers may prepare `TxnProposal`s and candidate physical work, but only the leader/global Raft order validates final effects, builds or approves the final capsule, and publishes. Preparation can scale; ordered commitment remains bounded by validation, payload distribution, and Raft. No near-linear committed-write claim ships without a benchmark separating preparation from ordered publication.
5. **Transfer, anti-entropy, and safe GC.** ATP may fetch compatible symbols from multiple donors. Every symbol is authenticated against the exact logical/encoding identity and §5.1 MAC transcript; donor agreement alone creates no authority. Merkle anti-entropy locates missing encodings and logical-digest divergence. Applied/durable positions may gossip through a CRDT cache, but branch heads derive only from committed head CAS records and GC is `CollectTypedRoots(stable_membership_epoch,config_payload_floor) -> ResolveMarkedPhysicalRealizations -> ProposeSafePoint -> ValidateAllTypedRootsAndCuts(prepared,commands,leases,pins,merge,privacy,replay,keys,backups,audit,transparency) -> CommitGcEpochThroughRaft -> Reclaim`. Uncertain membership, incomplete new-configuration payload closure, partition, missing root/prepared-owner acknowledgement, external privacy-continuity mismatch, or unavailable key wrap stops reclamation. Backup/restore follows §16, not a decode-proof shortcut.
6. **Post-1.0 sharding workstream W12.** Format v1 reserves stable partition identities and topology epochs, but sharding requires a global transaction protocol. Each participant durably transitions `None -> Prepared(prepare_oid, topology_epoch, effects/SSI/constraint/auth digests) -> GloballyCommitted(global_seq) -> Applied(global_seq)`, or to `GloballyAborted` only after a fenced meta-Raft decision/proof of nonexistence; it never heuristically aborts during meta-Raft unavailability. A meta-Raft `GlobalTxnRecord` atomically names all participant prepares, branch-head CAS values, and the next deterministic `GlobalCommitSeq`. Shards expose a transaction only after applying its decision, and cross-shard snapshots use a `GlobalAppliedSeq` barrier: every participating shard must have applied through the selected global prefix, or the read waits/fails. Recovery resolves in-doubt prepares idempotently from meta-Raft; prepared objects remain pinned until a terminal decision and safe point. Distributed uniqueness/cardinality, cross-shard predicate/path witnesses, schema/topology/security fencing, branch snapshots/merges, Ripple global watermarks, repartitioning with prepares, and joint reconfiguration all receive models and crash tests before W12 exits. Source-range partitioning is an edge-cut/vertex-owner scheme; an optional hub vertex-cut has separate replicated-vertex metadata and edge-ownership rules. Until W12 passes, the product rejects cross-shard databases rather than weakening atomicity.

## 15. Determinism, Calibration & Verification Doctrine (Bet B5)

This is not a QA appendix; it is a design pillar with its own budget. It opens with the runtime stratum (§15.0 — the calibration plane that governs the live system), then the verification stack from cheapest to strongest.

### 15.0 Sextant: the calibration discipline within B5

Sextant supplies e-processes, conformal predictors, sequential tests, robust estimators, regime/change detectors, and propensity/OPE tooling as deterministic transformations of logged inputs. It governs performance and operational choices; it never upgrades assumption-bearing evidence into exact correctness.

Every evidence envelope carries `ClaimClass`:

- `SafetyInvariant`: must hold on every execution; failure is a correctness defect.
- `FormalModelClaim`: theorem or model-check result with abstraction, bounds, and refinement status.
- `StatisticalClaim`: population, sampling/selection rule, alpha, power/effective sample size, and assumptions.
- `ConfigurationModelClaim`: model version, fitted inputs, sensitivity, and validity domain.
- `EmpiricalGate`: fixture, machine/profile, sample count, variance budget, and comparison rule.

`invariants.toml` contains only exact safety/liveness obligations. `evidence.toml` contains `FG-CAL-*` and `FG-EVID-*`; `slo.toml` contains `FG-CFG-*`, performance, durability-model, and controller targets. E-processes may be anytime-valid under their named filtration/null assumptions; conformal coverage is marginal under its named exchangeability or online-validity conditions; drift alarms do not retroactively preserve a broken assumption; OPE requires positivity/overlap and sufficient effective sample size. A monitor that drives no named decision is dead code, but binding it to a decision does not make it an invariant.

Every adaptive policy retains a deterministic fallback valid without the statistical model. Answer-affecting execution policies are bound to the `AnswerRequirement`, plan, result contract, and replay closure; canonical-state-affecting promotions are logical-command-sequenced and replay-pinned; only proved answer-preserving physical policies may remain local. Calibration windows, selection policy, strata, propensity support, regime epochs, fallback, and failure behavior are immutable evidence identities. This is how B5 makes adaptation inspectable without confusing “well measured” with “proved safe.”

### 15.1 Simulation-first development (`fgdb-sim`)

The entire database runs under asupersync's lab runtime with virtual time/network/disk, seeded scheduling, chaos, and deterministic pressure models. DPOR is exhaustive only within the declared bounded scenario/state model and the soundness of its independence relation; broader campaigns remain falsification, not proof of bug absence. Every failure still becomes a replayable seed/crashpack, which is the practical value.

Five force multipliers on the same substrate:

- **Targeted falsification (LDFI).** Random chaos asks “what if something fails?”; lineage-driven fault injection derives minimal fault hypotheses from successful-run dependencies. It targets every file/directory action in D1/D2, dual root slots, checkpoint/GC publication, payload availability, and Raft acknowledgement. It is a high-yield falsification tool, while residual random chaos guards lineage blind spots.
- **A model-qualified stopping heuristic.** Novelty estimates and coverage maximization prioritize finite CI time under a named sampling model. They may stop a campaign according to policy; they never certify that no unexplored bug remains. Deterministic bounded-state completion is reported separately from statistical/heuristic stopping.
- **Failing runs shrink themselves.** Hierarchical delta debugging + replay minimization reduce every failing seed (schedule + workload trace) to a minimal reproducer before filing, and divergence diagnostics explain exactly where a replay departed from its recording. Crashpacks arrive pre-shrunk; the bug report writes itself.
- **Lab-vs-live dual runs.** The same scenario, same seed, runs under the lab and against the live build, and the traces are diffed — closing the classic simulation-testing loophole ("we verified the simulator, not the system"). This is the runtime-behavior counterpart of the `fgdb-reference` semantics differential.
- **Production incident replay is graded.** Production retains bounded mediated nondeterminism records under a fail-closed redaction contract. Each bundle carries the explicit `ReplayCompleteness` grade `Replayable | StructuralReplay{reproduced_classes[],omitted_classes[]} | VerifiableIfArtifactsSupplied{missing_classes[]} | AuditOnly{missing_or_redacted_classes[]}`. Crypto entropy is never recorded. Full internal forensic bundles may be exact when policy permits; customer-safe bundles never overclaim byte identity.

### 15.2 Consistency oracles

In-sim checkers include snapshot/branch visibility, an SSI dependency oracle, obligation/quiescence, root/capsule closure, authorization noninterference, and Elle-class histories. The SSI oracle uses a deliberately independent small Tarjan/SCC implementation over trace-derived logical dependencies; production graph algorithms may be a secondary differential, never the sole checker of their own transaction graph. Every invariant has at least one checker outside the optimized component it checks where practical.

Production assertions and complete audits can enforce exact invariants; sampled e-processes are statistical sentinels in `evidence.toml`, not the enforcement column of `invariants.toml`. Replay uses the complete `ReplayManifest` in §8.6. Security verification additionally covers cross-capability differential results/plans/errors/timing classes, revocation during streams, cache/view/index isolation, hidden-state constraints/cascades, donor poisoning, key rotation, restore authorization, parser/resource exhaustion, and audit-chain completeness.

### 15.3 Fault & recovery torture

Crash matrices kill at every D1/D2 file and directory durability point, both root-slot writes, writer-fence takeover, post-rebase capsule finalization, marker batch/root publication, checkpoint authority transfer, compaction safe point, delivery-lease create/ACK/reset, configuration-floor refresh/retirement, each payload-receipt/Raft/apply transition, and every audit phase from external ticket reservation through candidate-root fencing, terminal CAS, mirror command, and visibility publication. Campaigns cover partial marker tails, full-digest collisions in the 128-bit locator bucket, mixed `EncodingId` symbols, erasures within and beyond each source-block budget, multi-process stale writers, uncertain membership/GC, donor poisoning, key loss/two-stage destruction/rotation, and idempotent `Unknown` recovery through a scoped outcome capability. Each scenario declares its failure and placement model.

### 15.4 Differential & conformance testing

- **Semantics oracles**: an executable **reference engine** (`fgdb-reference`) — a deliberately simple, single-threaded, obviously-correct implementation of the full logical semantics (values, visibility, path modes, intents, temporal selectors, branches) over canonical maps, compiled for tests/fuzzing/model-checking only, never shipped, never optimized, so "what should this return" is a program rather than a debate; openCypher TCK; a GQL feature-conformance corpus keyed to ISO feature IDs (published as a matrix); differential vs. Neo4j & Memgraph on a curated corpus (behavior parity where standards align, documented divergence elsewhere); Prism results differential vs. standalone fnx (which is itself NetworkX-parity-locked — a two-hop oracle chain to ground truth). Model-generated histories run against both engines and compare snapshots, results, certificates, and permitted abort outcomes.
- **Storage oracles**: model-based testing of Strata against an in-memory reference graph (arbitrary op interleavings, equality after every op under every open snapshot); metamorphic suites in the house style (e.g., pattern-match results invariant under compaction, seal, branch-fork, encode/decode round-trips).
- **Fuzzing**: parser (GQL/Cypher grammars), FGP frames, every fgdb-formats reader (extending fnx's 8-fuzzer precedent), SymbolRecord decoder.

### 15.5 Formal anchors (scoped, honest — the asupersync posture)

Lean targets the full visibility relation—publication, branch ancestry, create/retire, own writes, and reclamation—not merely three local MVCC facts. SSI work separates: dangerous-structure theorem, lifetime of dependency metadata, soundness of each witness constructor's concretization, refinement preservation of positive/negative/gap/path observations, and optimizer transport of witness obligations. Merge proofs cover the registered algebra in its constraint context. Ripple proofs cover only the explicitly registered operator/annotation subset.

TLA+/TLC models the dual-slot root and schema-complete checkpoint authority transfer, final-capsule commit, marker/global-branch links, delivery-lease ACK/resume, audit candidate visibility fencing/external reconciliation, compaction/GC, stable prepared ownership plus payload-availability/Raft, membership reconfiguration floors, branch merge/token/range idempotency, closed-sequence leases, and post-1.0 global shard transactions. The generated durable-reference checker separately enforces per-field `PriorObject` construction annotations and rejects cycles in reachable object instances; recursive persistent-tree types remain legal because every concrete parent points only to already identified children. Neither method is mislabeled as the other. Every model run states bounds. Trace export demonstrates that captured executions are allowed by a spec; it is **trace conformance**, not an implementation-refinement proof. Runtime monitors detect selected violations but do not inherit model-check completeness. Proof-lane manifests name abstraction, assumptions, bounds, refinement bridge, and exactly what remains unproved.

Appendix F is the normative design registry. G0 materializes it as checked-in `invariants.toml` with owner, checker path, dependencies, and gate before Genesis implementation begins; the plan does not falsely claim the currently absent file already exists.

### 15.6 Performance regression apparatus

Criterion-style in-house microbench harness + macro suites (§17) with baselines committed, variance-aware gating (the asupersync/fnx baseline artifact discipline), and complexity-witness regression locks: an algorithm or operator whose instrumented operation count exceeds its registered bound on an in-domain fixture fails CI. Correctness, security, durable-format, recovery, authorization, invariant-checker, and required conformance gates are **nonwaivable**. A public, expiring waiver may cover only a named empirical performance threshold, statistical-evidence threshold, or non-safety coverage budget; it records owner, evidence, reduced claim, expiry, and fallback and cannot weaken an invariant or enable a feature whose safety checker failed.

6. **Admission, backup, and upgrade**: ephemeral hierarchical ledgers reserve/charge/release the transient portion of §13's `ResourceVector`; Chronicle's canonical durable ledger atomically accounts for persistent branches/indexes/views/subscriptions/history/durable bytes through `ResourceChargeEffect`s. Startup/recovery reconciles ephemeral reservations by obligation generation and verifies durable ledger state against committed effects—neither local session counters nor telemetry are quota authority. Maintenance and recovery retain protected emergency capacity. Optimization models may advise allocations, but hard caps and shedding order govern ENOSPC, quorum loss, key unavailability, output backpressure, and tenant isolation.

   Backup is `Authorize -> AcquireRetentionLease -> FreezeRoot -> EnumerateClosure -> Copy -> Verify -> SealSignedManifest -> ReleaseLease`. Canonical `BackupManifest` pins database/security namespace and cluster incarnation, branch heads, logical-command/commit range, root/checkpoint and `CheckpointStateVector`, mandatory and policy-retained object inventory, delivery leases, prepared ownership, incremental-base closure, schema/topology/policy/revocation/key/signing/capability-verifier epochs, recipient wraps, transparency checkpoint, external identity/privacy/**audit** continuity records and resolvers, anti-rollback/configuration floors, format matrix, and threshold signature set. Trust policy names accepted signers, threshold, signer-rotation chain, revocations, external anchor, and minimum restorable checkpoint.

   Restore has two noninterchangeable modes. `RecoverSameIdentity` requires an externally recorded fence proving the old cluster cannot continue, verifies the archive/trust/rollback floor, preserves `DatabaseId`, security namespace, `K_oid`, privacy domain, and `AuditDomainId`, advances `cluster_incarnation`, and **CAS-reserves an `AllocationEpoch` strictly above the external `IdentityContinuityRecord` floor before enabling writes**. This burns every possibly issued ID range after the backup even when its database bytes were lost. Before DP is enabled, restore fetches the latest external `PrivacyContinuityRecord`, verifies its predecessor/CAS chain, imports or tombstones every post-backup debit/request, and makes the restored privacy ledger exactly reconcile with that monotonic head. Before any ticket-gated operation is enabled, it likewise fetches the latest `AuditContinuityRecord` and externally retained ticket/resolution evidence, reconciles every pending and terminal transition, and restores the audit chain head. Unavailable privacy authority disables DP; unavailable audit evidence disables completeness-gated operations and requires an explicit signed permanent-gap/downgrade record when continuity can resume. Neither case is hidden by an incarnation bump.

   Same-identity recovery also resolves or explicitly resets delivery leases through ordered commands, invalidates old sessions/capabilities as policy requires, rotates channel/signing/wrap authority, and publishes signed recovery/transparency/identity/privacy/audit-continuity records. Without cluster fencing and allocation continuity, recovery is read-only or must clone. `CloneNewIdentity` assigns a new database/security namespace and `K_oid`, decodes every reachable logical object, rehashes references, re-encrypts/rewraps, rebuilds signatures/accumulators, and verifies the new canonical semantic digest; it is not a metadata rename. It inherits the source **privacy** domain unless an approved disjoint-population policy authorizes a new one. It always creates a new namespace-bound `AuditDomainId` and publishes an `AuditCloneBoundary` signed by source and target authorities, linking the source audit head/evidence scope without pretending old tickets were issued in the target namespace; deployments requiring end-to-end audit lineage retain both chains. Both modes execute `Authorize -> VerifyTrustAndKeys -> Stage -> VerifyClosure -> ReplayAndSemanticDigest -> IdentityModeTransform -> ReconcileExternalContinuity -> AtomicPromote`. Partial staging remains unreachable and is cleaned only by its generation-scoped proof, never a wildcard. RPO/RTO, incremental-base retention, offsite failure domains, key recovery, split-brain fencing, rollback/ID/privacy/audit-continuity detection, and production-format restore drills are deployment gates. Rolling upgrades use a tested format/protocol matrix and rollback point.

Reference machine: 32-core/64-thread, 256 GB RAM, PCIe-4 NVMe, single node. Every number below is a provisional `EmpiricalGate`, activated only when its benchmark manifest pins CPU/microcode, kernel/filesystem/mount options, NVMe model/firmware and measured fsync distribution, toolchain/build, database configuration, graph/schema, transaction size and concurrency, retention/FEC/encryption/index policy, cold/warm state, result consumption, and statistical comparison rule. | Domain | Gate |
|---|---|

| Transactional ingest | Provisional ≥ 2M edge inserts/s for the pinned batch/txn mix; latency includes final rebase/constraint work, payload D1, root D2, configured encryption/FEC, and in replicated mode payload quorum + Raft. No marker-only benchmark is reported as commit latency. |

Method: every gate has a bench binary, a committed baseline, a variance budget, and a flamegraph artifact on regression; the harness itself is deterministic (seeded workloads, pinned schedules) so regressions replay. Performance work follows the asupersync "How We Made It Fast" discipline: profile → remove one contention/allocation → re-verify determinism and cancel-correctness → commit with evidence — one lever per change, never a bundle. Five standing laws bind every published number: (1) *no benchmark-only semantics* — durability, isolation, and result consumption match the declared production mode; (2) *distributions, not averages* — p50/p95/p99/p99.9 and worst hot-key behavior are always reported, with extreme quantiles carrying their fitted-tail confidence intervals rather than raw sample maxima; (3) *never hide compaction* — foreground latency during compaction, checkpoint, GC, and index build is part of the result; (4) *memory is a first-class metric* — bytes per live edge include versions, indexes, witnesses, and allocator slack, not just payload; (5) *adaptive numbers disclose their policy epoch* — any result produced under a non-fallback policy names it, and the fallback's number is published beside it.

## Appendix A — On-Disk Object Formats (normative contract)

All durable encodings use a declared format major/minor, little-endian fixed integers, canonical unsigned LEB128 only where the field says varint, length-before-allocation, checked arithmetic, and per-kind maximum sizes. Unknown additive-minor fields are length-delimited and skipped; unknown required flags fail closed. `CanonicalScalarProfile` defines float/decimal/string/time bytes. Readers authenticate/validate headers and resource bounds before allocating or decompressing.

**Reference semantics.** Every ObjectId-bearing edge declares a wire tag: `StrongRef{oid}` (always followed); `StrongMarkerRef{marker_ref}` and `StrongCommandRef{command_ref}` (retain the named history object); `ConditionalCoordinateRef{oid,graph,branch}` (sequence-neutral payload whose retention sequence comes from its enclosing committed marker); `ConditionalMarkerRef{marker_ref,axis:Global|Branch(graph,branch)}` and `ConditionalCommandRef{command_ref,axis:Logical}` (followed until an authenticated matching checkpoint/cut); `WeakMarkerIdentity{marker_oid,commit_seq}` (provenance/identity only); `WeakLocator`/`PhysicalRealizationRef` (nonretaining local lookup metadata); or `WeakDigest{digest}` (comparison only). Bare `MarkerRef{marker_oid:[u8;32],commit_seq:u64}` and `CommandRef{command_record_oid:[u8;32],logical_command_seq:u64}` are identities, **not reachability by themselves**; an enclosing tag supplies that. `StrongCommandRef` always means a previously constructed `LogicalCommandRecord`; an applied result that needs to cite its input uses `StrongRef<ControlCommand>` plus its scalar applied sequence instead.

For compactness in the schemas below, an otherwise unannotated field ending in `_oid`, `_root`, `_ref`, or an ObjectId-valued collection element is encoded as `StrongRef`; a field ending in `_digest` is comparison-only `WeakDigest` unless an explicit wrapper says otherwise. Bootstrap-slot root identity is an external GC root and is followed as strong. Pending/prepared traversal treats `ConditionalCoordinateRef` as strong because no committed-marker cut context exists. Archive identities are a separate nonlocal type defined below and can never enter the local ObjectId mark set. Every strong/currently-followed-conditional field carries a generated `PriorObject` construction annotation: its concrete target bytes and full identity must exist before the parent encoder runs. Recursive persistent nodes are legal only in that child-before-parent direction. A generated schema/reference/DAG checker compares every field below with Rust encoders and registry rows, rejects unresolved/self/mutual/future-result cycles in reachable instances, and fails G0 on drift. Unknown tags/context fail closed.

**RootSlot + RootBootstrap.** Each slot is exactly 4096 bytes at offsets 0 and 4096:

```text
RootSlot {
  magic="FGRT", format_major:u16, format_minor:u16, slot_generation:u64,
  local_writer_fence_epoch:u64, database_id:[u8;16],
  database_security_namespace_id:[u8;32], cluster_incarnation:u64,
  root_manifest_oid:[u8;32],
  bootstrap: RootBootstrap, reserved_zeroes, tear_checksum:[u8;32]
}
RootBootstrap {
  root_encoding_id:[u8;32], root_placement_id:[u8;32],
  root_placement_epoch:u64, failure_domain_policy_id:[u8;32],
  root_failure_domain_id:[u8;32], segment_id:[u8;16], offset:u64,
  encoded_len:u64, root_symbol_inventory_digest:[u8;32],
  object_kind:u16, canonical_plaintext_len:u64, codec_profile:u32,
  compressed_len:u64, data_crypto_profile:u32, dek_id:[u8;32],
  nonce_len:u8, nonce_or_siv:[u8;32], object_tag_len:u8,
  fec_profile:u32, transfer_length:u64, oti_common:u64, oti_scheme:u32,
  symbol_size:u32, source_block_count:u32, symbol_auth_profile:u16,
  ciphertext_id:[u8;32], ciphertext_digest:[u8;32], opener_kind:u16,
  oid_key_id:[u8;32], opener_payload_len:u16, opener_payload:[u8;1024],
  opener_digest:[u8;32]
}
```

Unused nonce/opener bytes are zero. Profile registries fix bounds and tag lengths. Together with `root_manifest_oid`, these fields reproduce the root's exact `CipherDescriptorWithoutDigest`, complete `EncodingDescriptorWithoutId`, and specialized `PlacementDescriptorWithoutId { encoding_id, root_placement_epoch, failure_domain_policy_id, ContiguousSpan { root_failure_domain_id, segment_id, offset, encoded_len, root_symbol_inventory_digest } }` byte-for-byte. Recovery scans the declared span, authenticates canonical SymbolRecords, verifies the sorted inventory commitment, and recomputes `root_placement_id` before decode. `opener_payload` is a versioned bundle of inline wraps or authenticated KMS/HSM locators sufficient to recover both the named root DEK and immutable `K_oid` without consulting the object index; its digest covers kind, key IDs, locators/wraps, and namespace/incarnation. The authenticated root repeats the exact database/security-namespace/incarnation tuple, and recovery rejects disagreement before following its closure. A structurally valid highest-generation slot is never silently rolled back after authentication/closure failure (§5.1).

**Cipher/encoding/placement records and SymbolRecord.** `CipherDescriptorWithoutDigest` is `{ logical_oid, object_kind, canonical_plaintext_len, codec_profile, compressed_len, data_crypto_profile, dek_id, object_nonce_or_siv, object_tag_len }`. Object AAD is the domain string plus logical OID and this canonical descriptor. `CiphertextRecord` is `{ descriptor, ciphertext_id, ciphertext_digest, object_tag_digest, protected_length }`; `CiphertextId` hashes descriptor+ciphertext+object tag. Immutable `EncodingDescriptorWithoutId` is `{ ciphertext_id, fec_profile, transfer_length, oti_common, oti_scheme, symbol_size, source_block_count, symbol_auth_profile }`, and `EncodingId` hashes the domain/version plus its **complete canonical bytes**. Separately, `PlacementDescriptorWithoutId` is `{ encoding_id, placement_epoch, failure_domain_policy, location_form }`, where `location_form = ContiguousSpan{failure_domain_id,segment_id,offset,encoded_len,symbol_inventory_digest} | Explicit{sorted_symbol_inventory_and_locators,failure_domains}`. `PlacementId` hashes the placement domain/version plus exactly that descriptor; immutable `PlacementRecord { placement_id, descriptor }` merely stores and cross-checks it. Moving/adding symbols creates a new placement record without changing encoding identity. KEK/wrap epoch is absent from both ciphertext and encoding identity. The authenticated local physical-realization index maps `CiphertextId`, `EncodingId`, and `PlacementId` to these records with nonretaining locators. An ordinary decoder obtains the complete descriptor/OTI/auth profile from an authenticated `EncodingDescriptorWithoutId` (or FGP's authenticated encoding prelude) before accepting any symbol; a bare `SymbolRecord` is never self-authorizing.

```text
SymbolRecord {
  magic="FGEC", format_version:u16, header_len:u16, record_len:u32,
  logical_oid:[u8;32], ciphertext_id:[u8;32], encoding_id:[u8;32],
  object_kind:u16, source_block:u32, esi:u32, symbol_len:u32,
  transfer_length:u64, oti_common:u64, oti_scheme:u32, flags:u32,
  symbol_mac_profile:u16, symbol_mac_len:u16,
  payload:[u8;symbol_len], symbol_mac:[u8;symbol_mac_len]
}
```

The symbol MAC key/transcript are exactly §5.1's domain-separated construction. All framing/OTI/length fields and payload are MACed. Truncated/noncanonical tails are ignored only in proved-unreferenced staging; a reachable sealed tail triggers authenticated repair or fail-closed recovery.

**LogicalStatePayload / LogicalStateRoot.** `LogicalStatePayload` is `{ database_id, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, applied_logical_command_seq, applied_commit_seq, global_transaction_marker_head:StrongMarkerRef?, branch_head_map_root(entries carry StrongMarkerRef), branch_merge_ledger_root, graph_partition_state_roots, catalog_root, schema_roots, id_allocator_root, identity_continuity_record_ref, constraint_state_roots, resource_ledger_root, privacy_ledger_root, privacy_continuity_record_ref, dp_request_index_root, txn_outcome_index_root, recovery_checkpoint_ref, retained_stream_floor, retention_map_root, derived_generation_registry_root, delivery_lease_registry_root, replay_registry_root, commit_hlc_state, topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, replicated_key_wrap/signing/capability-verifier roots, audit_ticket_index_root, audit_root, audit_continuity_record_ref, transparency_root, backup_registry_root, gc_decision_root, config_payload_floor_ref }`. `LogicalStateRoot` is only `{ payload_ref:StrongRef<LogicalStatePayload>, logical_command_head:StrongCommandRef? }`; genesis is the sole state with `None`. It is canonical replicated state at one complete logical-command prefix. `DerivedGenerationRegistry` maps `DerivedIdentity` strong references to `{generation_root:StrongRef, global_frontier?, answer_contract:AnswerContract, retention_class}`; it never reconstructs a singular graph/head/schema key. These references make selected view/index/stats/subscription state available and replayable without making it authoritative for base semantics.

**Replica-local RootManifest**: `{ generation, database_id, database_security_namespace_id, cluster_incarnation, replica_id, logical_state_root_oid, local_writer_fence_epoch, metadata_tree_bootstraps[Location|Encoding|KeyOpener], object_location_index_root, local_encoding_root, local_key_opener_root, prepared_payload_root, raft_state_root, local_applied_logical_command_seq, local_visible_logical_command_seq, local_applied_index, local_durable_index, active_audit_visibility_fence_ref?, nonretaining_predecessor_root_digest }`. A `MetadataTreeBootstrap` embeds `{tree_kind,root_oid,cipher_descriptor,ciphertext_id,encoding_descriptor,placement_records,tree_key_opener,closure_digest}`. Each authenticated internal edge carries `{child_oid,child_cipher_descriptor,child_ciphertext_id,child_encoding_descriptor,child_placement_descriptor,wrapped_child_dek_under_tree_key,child_wrap_profile,child_wrap_aad_digest,child_wrap_digest}`. This is the exact key-opener path for each independently random child DEK; an opened parent/tree key alone is insufficient. Tree-root bootstrap edges are strong, but ordinary location/encoding/placement/key-opener **entries** are `WeakLocator`/`PhysicalRealizationRef` and do not determine liveness. Semantic/prepared marking first decides which identities live, then resolves their containing realization. The predecessor is `WeakDigest`, not a retention edge. The opened location index supports authenticated nonretaining subobject locators `{container_encoding_id,placement_id,offset,length}` for independently hashed markers.

`MetadataTreeBootstrap.closure_digest` commits only the already-built metadata-tree closure. That same generation MUST NOT contain any entry—strong, weak, or physical-locator—for its current `RootManifest`, `RootSlot`, or bootstrap bytes; the slot bootstrap is the current manifest's sole opener. This is a construction rule in the generated DAG checker, not merely a GC classification.

**Raft persistence.** Every durable posture has this layer; single node is a one-member configuration. `RaftLogSegment` is a contiguous, checksum/MAC-protected range of `{index,term,entry_type,command:StrongRef<CommitCommand|ControlCommand>,command_digest}`; an accepted log entry therefore retains its normative command closure. `RaftHardState` is `{current_term,voted_for?,log_first/log_last,commit_index,applied_index,visible_index,config_state_oid,snapshot_ref?}`. `RaftSnapshot` binds `{ last_included_index, term, logical_state_root_ref:StrongRef<LogicalStateRoot>, config_state_ref, config_payload_floor_ref, recovery_checkpoint_ref, checkpoint_state_vector_digest, prepared_ownership_floor, digest }`; `last_included_index <= visible_index`, no active audit fence may be crossed, and its referenced checkpoint/vector must cover every payload field. `AuditVisibilityFence` is `{ticket_ref:StrongRef,blocked_after_logical_command_seq,candidate_logical_state_root_ref:StrongRef<LogicalStateRoot>,terminal_resolution_ref:StrongRef}`. `RaftStateRoot` strongly references hard state, log segments, current/joint configuration, snapshot, retained payload-certificate ledger, and any active audit fence/candidate. Each term/vote/append/fence transition publishes and syncs this local closure before its protocol reply (§14).

**AdjRunSegment**: `{ graph, branch_projection, source_partition, direction, edge_type, source_ordinal_map_id, destination_map_table[{partition,ordinal_map_id}], source_ordinal_range, edge_count, seq_range, codec_table, offsets_ef, encoded_neighbor_lists, stable_eids, origin_birth_orders, branch_admission_orders?, marker_aware_retire_or_hole_data, property_locators, canonical_stable_id_digest, checksum }`. Each destination carries either stable VId or a destination-map-table slot plus ordinal. Mutable labels do not identify a primary run; per-list codec governs legal rank/select/skip operations.

**IdentityContinuityRecord / IdAllocatorState / IdRangeLease**: `IdentityContinuityRecord` binds `{database_security_namespace_id,allocation_epoch,predecessor_digest,external_registry_id,cas_version,status,issued_at,signer_set_epoch,threshold_signatures}`; its external registry/witness copy is the anti-rollback authority and its Chronicle copy is checked against it. Its applied lease is `{lease_id,graph,element_kind,allocation_epoch,partition,half_open_range,authority_digest,applied_command_ref:StrongRef<ControlCommand>,applied_logical_command_seq,issuance_not_after,permanent_spent_commitment}`. Expiry ends issuance authority only; every epoch/range pair remains permanently spent and can never overlap or recycle.

**CommittedEffectCapsule**: `{ read_snapshot_basis, validated_apply_basis, intent_semantics_oid, read_graph_reference_bindings[], validated_write_coordinate_bindings[], coordinate_schema_transitions[], topology_epoch, policy_epoch, revocation_index, effect_directory_root, committed_effect_refs[ConditionalCoordinateRef], constraint_evidence, authorization_decision_ref:StrongRef<AuthorizationDecisionRecord>, authorization_decision_digest:WeakDigest, authorized_permit_scope_digest, resource_transition_refs[StrongRef<ResourceLedgerTransition>], resource_charge_effects, txn_outcome_preparation_ref:StrongRef<TxnOutcomePreparationRecord>, final_read_witnesses, final_write_summary, birth_ordinals, merge_record_ref:StrongRef<MergeRecord>?, logical_delta_template_ref:StrongRef<LogicalDeltaTemplate>, logical_delta_template_digest:WeakDigest, optional_physical_accelerators[{apply_basis,digest,payload_ref:StrongRef}], txn_token, final_effect_digest }`. The immutable effect directory Merkle-commits coordinate-enveloped payload OIDs but remains sequence-neutral; the owning committed marker supplies retention time. Repeated digests cross-check the strongly referenced bytes and never replace ownership. The referenced template's full ObjectId and bytes must match the digest and final effect set. Role-bearing read bindings preserve repeated historical graph references; role-free write bindings own state transitions. The capsule contains permanent IDs and no unresolved conditional command, ambient input, local writer fence, or stale derived delta. The marker produced by apply becomes the resulting head in each transitioned coordinate's next `CoordinateSchemaBinding`; the capsule never self-references that future marker.

**CommitCommand / ControlCommand / LogicalCommandRecord / PreparedCommitRecord.** A transaction order unit is `{ command_version,database_security_namespace_id,cluster_incarnation,txn_token,capsule_ref:StrongRef,logical_delta_template_ref:StrongRef,authorization_decision_ref:StrongRef,resource_transition_refs[StrongRef],txn_outcome_preparation_ref:StrongRef,payload_availability_certificate_ref:StrongRef,expected_logical_command_seq,expected_branch_heads[],topology_epoch,policy_epoch,revocation_index,proposed_physical_hlc,final_effect_digest }`. A `ControlCommand` is `{ command_version,kind,schema_version,typed_payload_ref:StrongRef<SequenceNeutralSpec>,expected_logical_command_seq,expected_state_roots_or_epochs[],proposed_physical_hlc?,authorization_decision_ref:StrongRef,audit_ticket_ref:StrongRef? }`. The kind registry includes `TxnSubmissionSpec`, `CheckpointInstallSpec`, `IdRangeGrantSpec`, `MergePrepareSpec`, `MergeRejectSpec`, `DpTransitionSpec`, `DeliveryLeaseTransitionSpec`, `AuditTicketAdmissionSpec`, `AuditTerminalSpec`, `AuditContinuityMirrorSpec`, schema/policy/resource/revocation specs, `KeyDestroyProposal`, `KeyDestroyFinalizeSpec`, `GcProposal`, and `ConfigurationTransitionSpec`. Actual committed merge effects use the ordinary transaction `CommitCommand`/capsule/marker path; only token preparation and deterministic pre-effect rejection are control commands. Each control schema declares whether an audit ticket is mandatory, optional, or structurally inapplicable, and none may point to its applied result or resulting root. Apply constructs result objects, then `LogicalStatePayload`, then `LogicalCommandRecord { logical_command_seq, predecessor:ConditionalCommandRef{axis=Logical}?, command:StrongRef<CommitCommand|ControlCommand>, transaction_marker:StrongMarkerRef?, logical_command_hlc, resulting_state_payload_digest:WeakDigest }`, then `LogicalStateRoot { payload_ref:StrongRef, logical_command_head:StrongCommandRef? }`; only genesis uses `None`. A replica-local `PreparedCommitRecord` is `{ prepared_ownership_id,prepare_id,record_generation,predecessor_ref:StrongRef<PreparedCommitRecord>?,database_security_namespace_id,cluster_incarnation,configuration_epoch,local_writer_fence_epoch,txn_token,capsule_ref:StrongRef,command_ref:StrongRef?,base_closure_inventory_digest,current_closure_inventory_digest,strong_logical_refs[StrongRef],exact_realizations[{object_or_subobject_id,encoding_id,placement_id,key_opener_ref:StrongRef}],status:Preparing|Receipted|Ordered{raft_index}|Applied{logical_command_seq,commit_seq}|Abandoned{fence_proof_digest},receipt_nonce }`. `PreparedPayloadRoot` maps the stable ownership ID to the latest immutable successor. Every successor repeats the same base inventory and has an identical or superset current closure; a receipt commitment is retained. `Receipted`/`Ordered` ownership cannot be removed by timeout or crash; only the fenced terminal rules in §5.2 and a committed GC decision may release it. On apply, ownership transfers to the command record/marker/logical state/outcome index before the prepared entry becomes terminal.

**LogicalDeltaTemplate / LogicalDeltaBatch.** A template is `{ format, intent_semantics_oid, final_effect_digest, source_intent_digest, coordinate_entries[] }`, where entries are canonically sorted and each is `{ graph, branch, relation_id, validated_schema_binding, schema_transition?, delta_payload_ref:ConditionalCoordinateRef, delta_payload_digest }`. The payload is a canonical exact Z-set over `CanonicalRow`; duplicate coordinates/relations, zero weights, noncanonical order, a transition inconsistent with its binding, or a payload not equal to the capsule's before/after effects are invalid. Prepared traversal follows every conditional edge as strong; committed traversal supplies the enclosing marker sequence and may apply only a matching `EffectCut`. A self-contained materialized batch is `{ format, source_template_digest:WeakDigest, commit_marker_identity:WeakMarkerIdentity, commit_seq, coordinate_entries[{source_binding,resulting_binding,relation_id,delta_payload_ref:StrongRef}], global_frontier, batch_digest }`. `batch_digest` covers all preceding canonical fields and is also the batch idempotency key; the object's full ObjectId is `LogicalDeltaBatchId`. Apply/rematerialization must reproduce the same object. A retained batch owns payloads directly but does not retain template, marker, capsule, or predecessor history. Delta-history leases are explicit roots and checkpoint cutting cannot silently invalidate a promised resume range.

**CoalescedDeltaPayload / DeltaDeliveryEnvelope / DeliveryLeaseRegistry.** `CoalescedDeltaPayload` is `{ format,first_batch_id,last_batch_id,ordered_source_commitment,source_count,start_frontier,resulting_frontier,net_zset_refs[StrongRef],net_delta_digest }`; the ordered commitment covers every source batch ID/digest, including zero-net intermediates. `DeltaDeliveryEnvelope` is `{ format,database_security_namespace_id,cluster_incarnation,tenant_principal_authority_digest,derived_identity_digest,baseline_id,start_frontier,resulting_frontier,payload:Atomic{batch_ref:StrongRef<LogicalDeltaBatch>,batch_digest,commit_seq}|Coalesced{payload_ref:StrongRef<CoalescedDeltaPayload>},delivery_digest }`. `DeliveryDigest` is the public domain-separated hash of canonical envelope bytes excluding that field; `DeliveryId` is the envelope's full keyed ObjectId. `delivery_lease_id` and `resume_cursor_id` are random/idempotency identities fixed in the pre-order transition spec, never hashes of their result record. A `DeliveryLeaseRecord` is `{delivery_lease_id,resume_cursor_id,envelope_ref:StrongRef<DeltaDeliveryEnvelope>,baseline_id,derived_identity_digest,authority_digest,state:Outstanding,issued_logical_command_seq,promised_through,verifier_key_epoch}`. `DeliveryLeaseRegistryRoot` is the canonical exact map of active leases and enforces one outstanding lease per cursor/frontier. Creation precedes the first frame. Its ordered `DeliveryLeaseTransitionSpec` variants are exact: `RotateCapability` preserves the outstanding registry entry and envelope while replacing only the capability generation; `AckComplete` advances the cursor/frontier and removes the lease; `ResetOrExpire` may remove it only after every promise and transport obligation is terminal. **RecoveryCheckpoint / CheckpointStateVector / RetentionMap.** A checkpoint is `{ checkpoint_logical_command_seq,checkpoint_commit_seq,covered_logical_command:StrongCommandRef,covered_global_marker:StrongMarkerRef?,covered_command_chain_hash,covered_marker_chain_hash,commit_hlc_state,checkpoint_state_vector_ref:StrongRef<CheckpointStateVector>,covered_logical_state_payload_digest,nonretaining_predecessor_checkpoint_digest }`. **PayloadReceipt / PayloadAvailabilityCertificate / ConfigPayloadFloor / GcEpoch.** `RemotePreparedIdentity { storage_member,prepared_ownership_id,base_closure_inventory_digest,latest_record_oid?,observed_prepared_payload_root_generation? }` names the stable signed ownership promise; the optional observations are not its identity. Renewal creates a logical-command-sequenced successor after re-verifying the entire inventory. **Authorization, durable capabilities, DP, resources, and audit records.** Canonical `MutationPermitRecord` and `AuthorizationLease` carry the §5.2/§12.2 database/security-namespace/incarnation, tenant/principal/issuer, authority ceiling/digest, policy-semantics/epoch, revocation index, exact role-bearing read bindings, role-free write coordinates, snapshot/operation scope, nonce, expiry, signer/term, and signature/MAC transcript; Rust exposes only verified nonconstructible wrappers. `DurableCapabilityVerifierKey { issuer_id,key_id,key_epoch,algorithm,public_or_protected_verifier_material,not_before,verify_through,format_versions[],rotation_predecessor?,status }` is Chronicle-sequenced and strongly retained until no live `ResumeCapability`, `TxnOutcomeCapability`, `MergeOutcomeCapability`, or read-your-writes capability can require it. ## Appendix B — Graph Intent Log (the semantic vocabulary)

Intents are deterministic commands captured at statement execution; they are **not** committed effects or Ripple deltas. A mixed-semantics proposal is invalid. Temporary references/IDs are proposal-local and must be resolved through canonical allocator state before finalization.

LWW policies use canonical `EffectOrder { commit_seq, intent_ordinal, merge_ordinal, effect_ordinal }`, never element birth or an ambient wall clock. ## Appendix C — GLA Operator Inventory (executor contracts)

Every physical implementation registers input/output factorization, strict/relaxed determinism, minimum resident set and spill algorithm, maximum work between cancellation checkpoints, incremental derivative or `none`, witness domain, authorization boundary, path preservation, input/output `AnswerContract` transfer or rejection, and legal `ReplanClass`. “Propagate” below means retain the complete upstream approximation provenance and apply a registered sound transformation; it never means relabel approximate data as exact. ## Appendix D — FGP Protocol Contract

Connection states are only `TransportEstablished -> VersionNegotiated -> Authenticated -> Ready -> Draining -> Closed`. `Ready` owns at most one transaction child `Idle -> Begun -> CommitSubmitted -> Committed|Aborted|Unknown -> Idle`, zero or more query children `Accepted -> Bound -> Executing -> StreamingResults -> Completed|Cancelled|Failed`, and zero or more subscription/export children `Opening -> Baseline -> Delta -> Resetting|Ended`. The negotiated `concurrency_profile` declares permitted coexistence; baseline profiles serialize transaction-bound statements while allowing only explicitly independent autocommit children. `Draining` creates no child and closes only after each existing child has a terminal record. Every frame has `{ frame_len, protocol_version, frame_type, flags, request_id, stream_id }` and is rejected before allocation when it exceeds negotiated `FrameLimits`. Ephemeral statement/stream/query/active-transaction/reservation handles bind the current session; only the explicitly typed `ResumeCapability` and `TxnOutcomeCapability` cross sessions, under §9.5/§13's namespace/incarnation, same-or-narrower-authority, expiry, revocation, rotation, replay, and capability-safe-lookup rules. A `ReplayManifest` ObjectId may be presented in a later authenticated session but conveys no authority; `REPLAY_*` always reauthorizes the complete closure. No generic “cursor” or “transaction token” is implicitly transferable.

Normative frame families:

Frame-order validity does not replace value, authorization, decompression, or resource validation. `DELTA_BEGIN` carries every canonical envelope field needed to recompute `DeliveryDigest`; its payload tag/ObjectId is exact, and repeated coalesced frontiers must equal the envelope header or the frame is invalid. A delta delivery, snapshot, or replay manifest may exceed `FrameLimits`, so deterministic chunking prefers canonical row/record boundaries and every chunk is covered by the whole-envelope/payload digest. A legal canonical row larger than one frame is split only by `DATA_FRAGMENT` at deterministic byte offsets; the receiver validates declared total row length against its negotiated logical-row limit, reserves bounded assembly/spill resources before accepting bytes, verifies per-row and whole-envelope digests, and exposes nothing until the row and enclosing `*_END` are complete. Thus `max_frame_bytes` need not equal `max_logical_row_bytes`, and fragmentation never changes logical encoding. Clients apply/ACK only after matching `*_END`; an incomplete unacked delivery is discarded and replayed with the same `DeliveryId`/digest on resume, never skipped via a frontier. The tagged envelope makes atomic and coalesced delivery ACK/resume semantics identical without pretending a coalesced range is one `LogicalDeltaBatch`. Baseline completion is similarly atomic. `replay_manifest_ref` is fixed-size metadata, not inline unbounded evidence; replay retrieval/execution requires current scoped authority and reports the effective grade after live closure/archive verification. Each `FEC_SYMBOL` payload is the exact canonical `SymbolRecord` from Appendix A, including all authenticated header fields and MAC; a receiver verifies it against the authenticated complete descriptor before admission. Compression/FEC is negotiated per stream after authenticated metadata and budget reservation. Result framing never changes canonical logical identity. HTTP/gRPC/WebSocket/Bolt adapters preserve transaction/idempotency semantics and define origin/CSRF, admin-listener, metrics authorization, header/trace-baggage, and decompression limits. All adapters, token parsing, bytecode validation, and nested/compressed/FEC envelopes have fuzz/conformance corpora.
